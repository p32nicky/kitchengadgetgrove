Three Quick Veggie Prep Routines

Pick one routine each week and keep a batch of pre‑sliced veggies ready in the fridge. All three rely on the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide to speed up the process.

  1. Rainbow Carrot Sticks: Peel carrots, then slice them lengthwise into ¼‑inch sticks using the holder. Toss with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of cinnamon for a sweet‑savory twist. Store in a sealed container with a damp paper towel to stay crisp.
  2. Mini Tomato & Cucumber Cups: Slice cucumbers and tomatoes into ¼‑inch rounds. Use a small cookie cutter or the tip of a knife to hollow out the center of each cucumber slice, creating a cup. Fill with a dollop of hummus or Greek yogurt and top with a tomato slice. The uniform slices keep the cups stable for transport.
  3. Sweet Potato Chips: Peel sweet potatoes, then slice them thinly (about ⅛‑inch) with the slicer. Spread on a baking sheet, lightly coat with oil, and bake at 400°F for 15‑20 minutes. The even slices crisp up uniformly, making a nutritious alternative to bagged chips.

All three can be prepared in under 15 minutes once you have the slicer on hand, and they keep well for up to three days.

Meal‑Prep Tips to Keep Veggies Fresh

Even the best‑cut veggies can wilt if stored improperly. Here are a few tricks that pair nicely with the quick cuts you achieve using the onion holder:

  • Separate moisture: Store sliced veggies in a dry container, and keep a damp paper towel in a separate compartment. This prevents sogginess.
  • Use airtight containers: Plastic or glass containers with tight seals keep air out, preserving crunch.
  • Label by date: A simple sticky note with the prep date helps you rotate older veggies first, reducing waste.

When you combine these storage habits with the uniform cuts from the slicer, you’ll notice less bruising and a longer shelf life—perfect for busy school mornings.
