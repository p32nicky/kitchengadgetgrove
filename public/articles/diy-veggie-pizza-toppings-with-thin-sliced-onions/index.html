Step 1: Prep the Onions with the Onion Holder

First, peel your onions. The stainless‑steel prongs of the onion holder grip the onion, preventing slips and keeping the layers aligned. Place the onion on the cutting board, secure it with the holder, and slice with a sharp knife. Aim for 1‑to‑2‑mm thickness. The holder’s anti‑cut design protects your fingers while the prongs hold the onion steady, so you can focus on the rhythm of the cut. Step 2: Build Your Pizza Base

Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) with a pizza stone inside. Roll out your dough on a floured surface to about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or the stone. Spread a thin layer of sauce, leaving a small border. Sprinkle cheese, then arrange the fresh veggies: sliced bell peppers, mushrooms, spinach, olives, and artichoke hearts. Drizzle a light coat of olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and oregano.

Step 3: Add the Thin‑Sliced Onions

Distribute the onion rings evenly over the toppings. Because they’re thin, they’ll cook quickly and caramelize slightly, adding depth without overpowering the pizza. If you like a touch of sweetness, sprinkle a few sugar crystals on top before baking. The onion holder’s versatility means you can also slice tomatoes, potatoes, or even tenderize meat with the same tool, streamlining prep for multi‑topping pies.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

Slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or sheet. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust edges are golden and the cheese is bubbling. Keep an eye on the onions; they’ll finish cooking in the last 2 minutes of baking. Remove the pizza, let it rest for a couple of minutes, then slice and serve hot.

Pro Tips for the Best DIY Veggie Pizza

  • Use a sharp knife—a dull blade makes uneven cuts and can cause the onions to tear.
  • Don’t overload toppings—too many ingredients can trap steam and make the crust soggy.
  • Pre‑cook denser veggies like mushrooms or onions lightly on the stovetop to reduce moisture release during baking.
  • Experiment with herbs—a sprinkle of fresh basil or thyme after baking adds a fresh finish.
  • Keep the onion holder clean—wash it after each use; stainless steel resists rust and holds up to daily slicing.

FAQ

Can I use the onion holder for other vegetables?

Yes—its stainless‑steel prongs work well with tomatoes, potatoes, and even some fruits. The design keeps the produce steady for even slicing.

How do I clean the onion holder safely?

Wash it by hand with warm soapy water, rinse, and dry immediately. Avoid soaking it in water for extended periods to prevent rust.

Will the onion rings burn if I bake the pizza too long?

Thin slices cook quickly. Keep an eye on the pizza and remove it once the cheese is bubbly and the crust is golden; the onions should caramelize without burning.
