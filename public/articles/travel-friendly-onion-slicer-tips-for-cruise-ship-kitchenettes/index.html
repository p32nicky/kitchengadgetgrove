Prep your onion the right way

Even the best slicer can be thwarted by a poorly prepped vegetable. Follow these steps before you engage the holder:

  1. Trim the root end, leaving a small “stem” so the layers stay together.
  2. Peel the outer skin; the stainless steel prongs grip the flesh, not the slippery skin.
  3. Place the onion in the holder with the root side up; the prongs will pierce the top and bottom, keeping it steady.

Once secured, a gentle rocking motion of your knife yields even slices without crushing the layers.

Maximize safety on a rocking vessel

Ships can pitch, and a loose knife can become a hazard. The anti‑cut design of the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide means the blade never contacts the metal prongs, so you’re only cutting the vegetable. Keep the following safety habits in mind:

  • Always slice away from your body, even if the holder feels secure.
  • Store the knife in a sheath or the drawer when not in use.
  • Use a non‑slip silicone mat under the holder for extra stability during rough seas.
