Preparing Your Onion for the Perfect Cut

Start with a fresh, firm onion. Follow these steps before you introduce the holder:

  1. Remove the papery outer skin and trim the root end, leaving about a half‑inch of the base intact. This helps keep the layers together.
  2. Slice the top off (the opposite end) so the onion sits flat on the cutting board.
  3. Rinse and pat dry. A dry surface reduces slipping when you place the onion in the holder.

Now, open one of the stainless‑steel prongs of the holder, slide the onion onto the base, and close the prongs. The onion should sit snugly, with the cut side facing up. The holder’s design keeps the onion steady, so you can focus on the blade rather than worrying about the vegetable moving under the knife.

How to Slice Uniform Strips Using the Onion Holder

With the onion secured, it’s time to slice:

  • Choose the right knife: A sharp chef’s knife or a santoku works best. A dull blade will crush the layers and produce uneven strips.
  • Set your angle: For classic salsa strips, aim for a 45‑degree angle relative to the board. This creates thin, diagonal cuts that are easier to chew.
  • Make steady, even strokes: Let the knife do the work; don’t force it. A smooth, continuous motion yields uniform thickness.

Because the onion is held in place, you’ll notice the cuts become more consistent after just a few slices. If you need even thinner strips (ideal for pico de gallo), simply adjust the angle to a shallower cut.

Storing Your Strips and Maintaining Freshness

After you’ve sliced the onions, keep them crisp by:

  1. Placing the strips in a bowl of ice water for 5‑10 minutes. This reduces the pungent bite and preserves color.
  2. Draining well and patting dry before adding to your salsa mix.
  3. Covering the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerating if you’re not serving immediately. The strips stay fresh for up to 24 hours.

When you’re done slicing, wash the holder immediately with warm, soapy water. The stainless‑steel surface resists staining, but a quick rinse helps prevent any lingering onion oils from building up.
