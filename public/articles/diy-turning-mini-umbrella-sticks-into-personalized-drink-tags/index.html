Step-by-Step: Creating DIY Personalized Drink Tags Mini Umbrellas

The key to a professional-looking tag is the shape. I prefer a small banner or a circular disc. Cut your cardstock into small rectangles (about 1 inch by 2 inches) or use a circle punch for a cleaner look. Once you have your shapes, write your guests' names clearly in the center. If you're hosting a large crowd, you can prep these tags a day in advance.

Once the names are written, punch a small hole at the top of the tag. Now, take one of the picks from the 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks. Gently slide the wooden stem through the hole in the cardstock. Position the tag just below the umbrella canopy so it doesn't interfere with the opening mechanism.

To ensure the tag doesn't slide down the stick into the drink, secure it with a tiny piece of clear tape on the back or a pinpoint drop of glue. Make sure the glue is completely dry before inserting the pick into a glass to avoid any residue in the beverage.

Tips for Stability and Presentation

When placing your personalized umbrellas in a drink, avoid sticking them directly into ice cubes, as they can slip or lean. Instead, aim for the fruit garnish. If you're serving a drink with a pineapple wedge or a maraschino cherry, pierce the umbrella through the fruit first. This anchors the tag firmly in place and prevents it from tipping over.

For drinks with a heavy foam or a thick cream top, like an Espresso Martini or a White Russian, place the tag slightly off-center. This allows the guest to see the name clearly without the umbrella canopy blocking the view of the drink's surface. If you are serving appetizers alongside drinks, these same picks work beautifully as food markers for a charcuterie board.

FAQ

Can I use these tags in hot drinks?

It is not recommended. The paper parasols and the cardstock tags are designed for cold beverages. Heat can cause the paper to wilt or the adhesive to melt, which could ruin the drink.

What is the best glue to use so it's food-safe?

Since the glue only touches the wooden stick and the paper tag (and not the liquid itself), a small amount of clear craft glue or a glue dot is sufficient. Just ensure the glue is fully cured before serving.

How do I stop the tags from soaking up the drink?

Use a heavier weight cardstock rather than standard printer paper. For extra protection, you can lightly coat the tags in clear packing tape or a spray sealant before assembling them to make them water-resistant.
