Safety First: How to Avoid Common Pitfalls

Telescopic forks are sturdy, but they’re not indestructible. Follow these quick safety checks before each use:

  1. Make sure the locking mechanism clicks firmly after extending. A loose lock can cause the fork to collapse mid‑stir.
  2. Inspect the tines for bends or dull spots. A bent tine can snag food and damage the shaft.
  3. After cooking, rinse the fork with warm water and dry it thoroughly. Even stainless steel can develop spots if left wet in a humid campsite.

These steps keep the fork reliable for years and prevent unexpected breakage while you’re busy handling the fire.
