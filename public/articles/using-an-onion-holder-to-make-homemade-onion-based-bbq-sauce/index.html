Step-by-Step: Making BBQ Sauce with the Onion Holder

Start with fresh onions for a homemade sauce that’s anything but store-bought. Follow this quick method:

  1. Slice with precision: Use the onion holder to thinly slice 2 large onions. For caramelized onions, cut thicker slices.
  2. Caramelize or blend: Cook sliced onions in butter until golden (for depth) or blend raw slices into your sauce base for a zesty kick.
  3. Combine ingredients: Mix cooked onions with ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire, and smoked paprika. Simmer until thickened.

Tips for Perfect Onion-Based BBQ Sauce

Maximize flavor and efficiency with these pro tips:

  • Use yellow or Vidalia onions for balance; red onions add a spicy edge.
  • Blend raw onion slices into the sauce for a smoother texture.
  • Store leftovers in an airtight jar—caramelized onions keep for a week; raw slices freeze well.
