Choosing the Right Corn for a Wedding BBQ

Fresh, sweet corn is the star of any summer grill. Look for ears that are bright yellow (or red, if you want a pop of color) with tightly packed kernels. The husks should be green and tightly wrapped; if they’re dry or shredded, the kernels inside may be past their prime. For a wedding crowd, buy about 1.5 ears per guest if corn is a side dish, or 1 ear per guest if it’s the main vegetable offering.

Prep Steps That Save Time and Mess

Do the heavy lifting before the guests arrive. Here’s a quick workflow:

  1. Shuck the corn, removing husks and silk. Rinse quickly to get rid of any remaining silk.
  2. Brush each ear with a thin layer of melted butter or oil. This helps the seasoning stick and creates a caramelized crust.
  3. Season with a blend of salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and a pinch of cayenne for subtle heat.
  4. Insert a Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holder into each ear. The silicone handles give a firm grip, and the interlocking design keeps the holders neat in storage.

Once the corn is pre‑seasoned and fitted with holders, cover the trays with foil and refrigerate. The corn can sit for up to 12 hours without losing flavor, so you can prep the night before.

Grilling Techniques for Perfectly Charred Corn

When it’s time to fire up the grill, aim for medium‑high heat (about 400‑450°F). Directly place the corn on the grates, turning every 2‑3 minutes. The silicone handles on the Girla holders stay cool enough to handle, yet sturdy enough to keep the cob from slipping. After 12‑15 minutes, the kernels should be lightly charred and the husks (if left on) will be crispy.

If you prefer a smoky flavor, add a handful of wood chips to the coals or use a smoker box. The corn will absorb that subtle oak aroma without becoming bitter.

Finishing Touches: Butter, Herbs, and Creative Twists

Once off the grill, brush the corn once more with flavored butter. Some crowd‑pleasers include:

  • Lemon‑Herb Butter: mix softened butter with lemon zest, chopped parsley, and a dash of garlic powder.
  • Spicy Chipotle Mayo: combine mayo, chipotle in adobo, and a squeeze of lime for a smoky kick.
  • Parmesan‑Crisp: sprinkle grated Parmesan and a pinch of smoked paprika, then broil for 30 seconds for a crunchy topping.

Serve the corn directly on the holders; guests can simply pull the silicone grip and enjoy the cob without getting buttery fingers on the table.

Serving Ideas for a Wedding BBQ Spread

Grilled corn pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes. Consider these simple combos:

  • Fresh garden salad with a citrus vinaigrette.
  • Smoked brisket or pulled pork sliders.
  • Cold pasta salad with cherry tomatoes and basil.
  • Seasoned baked beans or a quinoa‑black bean mix for a healthier option.

Arrange the corn in a rustic wooden crate or on a long serving board. The bright silicone handles add a splash of color that complements any wedding theme, from boho chic to classic white.
