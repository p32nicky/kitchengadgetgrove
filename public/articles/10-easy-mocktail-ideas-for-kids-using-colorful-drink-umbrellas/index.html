1. Pineapple Punch with a Tiki Twist

Ingredients: pineapple juice, coconut water, a splash of lime juice, and a pinch of sea salt. Stir until the salt dissolves. Serve over crushed ice and garnish with a pineapple‑colored umbrella.

2. Berry Blast Slush

Blend frozen mixed berries, a splash of orange juice, and a splash of vanilla yogurt. Pour into a tall glass, drop in a red‑or‑purple umbrella, and let the kids sip the rainbow.

3. Citrus Cooler

Combine equal parts orange juice, lemon‑lime soda, and a squeeze of fresh lemon. Add a dash of sparkling water for fizz. Top with a bright yellow umbrella for a sunny vibe.

4. Tropical Mango Tango

Mash ripe mango with a splash of coconut milk and a spoonful of honey. Chill and serve with a green‑colored umbrella that looks like a little jungle canopy.

5. Grape Glee

Puree fresh grapes, add a splash of sparkling water, and chill. Pour into a glass, drop a purple umbrella, and watch the kids enjoy the natural sweetness.

6. Watermelon Wonder

Blend watermelon cubes with a hint of lime. Strain to remove pulp, pour into a glass, and garnish with a pink umbrella for a fresh, summery look.

7. Strawberry Lemonade Surprise

Make classic lemonade, add pureed strawberries, and stir until smooth. Serve over ice with a red umbrella that pops against the clear glass.

8. Orange‑Ginger Fizz

Mix freshly squeezed orange juice with ginger syrup, top with club soda, and add a splash of soda water for extra bubbles. Finish with a orange‑orange umbrella for a playful touch.

9. Coconut‑Berry Cooler

Combine coconut milk, frozen blueberries, and a touch of maple syrup. Blend until creamy, pour into a glass, and garnish with a blue umbrella that mimics a tropical sky.

10. Peachy Keen Punch

Puree fresh peaches, add a splash of sparkling water, and a squeeze of lemon. Chill, pour into a glass, and top with a peach‑colored umbrella for a soft, inviting color.

Safety Tips for Kids and Drink Umbrellas

While the umbrellas add excitement, keep an eye on little ones. They’re small enough to pose a choking hazard if swallowed. Use the umbrellas as a decorative element and supervise children when they sip. If you’re serving the drinks at a party, place the umbrellas on the side of the glass or in a holder so kids can pick them up safely.

Storing Your Umbrellas

After use, store the umbrellas in a dry, cool place to keep them crisp. They’re recyclable and can be reused in future parties or as craft decorations. The 150‑Pack offers plenty of options for future celebrations.

FAQ

Can the drink umbrellas be used in hot drinks?

It’s best to avoid hot beverages. The paper material can warp or burn, and the umbrella might fall out. Stick to cold or room‑temperature mocktails for the best experience.

How many umbrellas are needed for a party?

A good rule of thumb is one umbrella per drink. With 150 umbrellas, you can easily host a large gathering or keep extras for future events.

Are these umbrellas safe for kids with allergies?

The umbrellas themselves are made from paper and contain no allergens. However, always check the drink ingredients for potential allergens, especially if you’re adding fruit or sweeteners that kids might be sensitive to.
