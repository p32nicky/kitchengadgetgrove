Step-by-Step: Deglaze Cast Iron with a Wooden Spurtle

  1. After baking, let the skillet cool slightly (avoid sudden temperature changes).
  2. Add 1-2 cups of water, broth, or wine directly into the pan. The liquid helps loosen stuck-on bits.
  3. Use the wooden spurtle’s edge to gently scrape the bottom and sides. Work in circular motions to dislodge debris.
  4. Combine the liquid and loosened food, then deglaze with your chosen liquid (wine, vinegar, or stock). Simmer to create a sauce or reduce for flavor.
  5. Rinse the skillet, dry thoroughly, and apply a thin layer of oil to maintain seasoning.

Tips for Using Your Wooden Spurtle

Maximize your tool’s versatility by storing it in a dry place and oiling it occasionally with food-safe mineral oil. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don’t submerge your cast iron in water for long periods. Focus on deglazing with the wooden spurtle to preserve seasoning. Also, avoid using the spurtle on glass or ceramic surfaces—the tool is optimized for cast iron, stainless steel, or mixing jars.
