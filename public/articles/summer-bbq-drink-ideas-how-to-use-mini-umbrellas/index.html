Refreshing Summer BBQ Drinks to Try

When you're managing a grill, you don't have time to shake individual cocktails for twenty people. The key is to create high-impact drinks that can be made in pitchers or dispensers. Start with a Watermelon Lime Cooler: blend fresh watermelon, a squeeze of lime, and a splash of sparkling water. It's hydrating and pairs perfectly with salty grilled meats.

For those who want something with a kick, a Spicy Pineapple Margarita is a crowd-pleaser. Muddle a slice of jalapeño with pineapple juice, tequila, and agave. To elevate the look, use one of the three styles of mini paper parasol picks from the 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks to hold a piece of fresh pineapple or a lime wheel in place.

If you're serving non-alcoholic options, don't let them be boring. A Cucumber Mint Spritz—made with muddled mint, sliced cucumber, and club soda—looks sophisticated and tastes crisp. Adding a colorful umbrella instantly tells your guests that the non-alcoholic choices are just as special as the cocktails.

How to Use Mini Umbrellas for Maximum Impact

Mini umbrellas are more than just a nostalgic throwback; they are functional tools for garnish management. Most people just poke them in the ice, but there are smarter ways to use these picks to improve the drinking experience. If you are using the 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks, you have a variety of styles that can match different drink vibes—from a tiki-style rum punch to a light, breezy spritz.

Instead of letting your fruit garnishes float haphazardly or sink to the bottom, slide the umbrella pick through a cherry, an olive, or a cube of melon first. This anchors the fruit at the top of the glass, keeping the drink looking polished even as the ice melts. It also prevents guests from having to fish out their fruit with their fingers.

Pairing Your Summer BBQ Drinks with Food

The right drink can actually enhance the flavor of your grilled food. High-acid drinks (like those with citrus or vinegar) cut through the richness of fatty meats like brisket or pork shoulder. When planning your menu, think about the color palette of your beverages to match your table setting.

For a cohesive look, try these pairing ideas:

  • Grilled Burgers & Dogs: Classic iced tea or a crisp lager garnished with a lemon wedge and a bright yellow umbrella.
  • Spicy Grilled Shrimp: Mango-habanero mojitos with a green mini parasol to mirror the tropical flavors.
  • Vegetable Kabobs: Sparkling pomegranate lemonade with a red cocktail pick and a fresh mint leaf.
  • Corn on the Cob: A chilled Paloma (grapefruit and tequila) with a pink umbrella for a vibrant, summery aesthetic.

FAQ

Do drink umbrellas affect the taste of the beverage?

No, the paper and wood used in quality mini parasols are food-safe and do not leach flavors into your drinks. They are designed purely for decoration and as a functional pick for fruit.

How do I stop the umbrellas from tipping over in the glass?

The best way to keep them upright is to push the pick through a piece of fruit (like a strawberry or lime slice) first. The fruit acts as a weighted base, keeping the umbrella stable against the side of the glass.

Can I use cocktail picks for hot appetizers?

While they are great for cold appetizers and fruit, avoid using paper umbrellas in deep-fryers or extremely hot ovens. They are best suited for room-temperature or chilled finger foods.
