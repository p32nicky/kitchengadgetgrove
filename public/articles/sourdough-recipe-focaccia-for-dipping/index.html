Why Sourdough Focaccia Is the Ultimate Dipping Bread

If you’re hunting for a sourdough focaccia dipping recipe that delivers a chewy interior and a crisp, olive‑oil‑laden crust, you’re in the right place. This recipe balances the natural tang of sourdough with a generous drizzle of olive oil and sea salt, creating a bread that’s ideal for dipping into marinara, pesto, or a simple vinaigrette. The best part? The dough is easy to manage, especially when you pair it with the Sourdough Whisk Set – the Danish dough whisk, scraper, and scoring lame that every beginner needs.

Gathering the Right Ingredients

  • 500 g (1 lb ½) sourdough starter, active and bubbly
  • 350 g (1 ½ cups) warm water (about 110°F)
  • 600 g (5 cups) bread flour (or a mix of bread and all‑purpose for a lighter crumb)
  • 2 tsp sea salt
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for greasing and drizzling
  • Optional toppings: rosemary, cherry tomatoes, caramelized onions, or olives

Step‑by‑Step Dough Prep Using the Sourdough Whisk Set

1. Mix the Starter and Water
In a large bowl, combine the sourdough starter with warm water. Use the Danish dough whisk to blend until the mixture is smooth and airy.

2. Add Flour and Salt
Gradually fold in the flour and salt. With the same whisk, work the dough until it’s cohesive but still slightly sticky. The whisk’s gentle, wide strokes help incorporate air without over‑working the gluten.

3. First Rise
Cover the bowl with a damp towel and let the dough rise at room temperature for about 4–6 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

4. Shape and Second Rise
Turn the dough onto a well‑oiled surface. Use the dough scraper to fold it into a rectangle, then stretch it to fit a 9×13‑inch baking pan. Lightly brush the surface with olive oil and let it rest for 30‑45 minutes.

Scoring, Topping, and Baking

Before baking, use the scoring lame to cut shallow slashes across the dough. This not only gives a rustic look but also allows the focaccia to expand evenly. Drizzle a generous amount of olive oil over the top, then sprinkle sea salt and your chosen toppings. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 20–25 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the underside sounds hollow when tapped.

Serving Ideas for the Perfect Dip

Let the focaccia cool for a few minutes, then cut it into wedges. Pair it with:

  • Classic marinara for a tomato‑based dip.
  • Garlic aioli for a creamy, herby option.
  • Roasted red pepper sauce for a smoky, sweet twist.

Because the dough is slightly chewy and the crust is crisp, it holds up well to any dip, making it a crowd‑pleaser at dinner parties or casual gatherings.

Tips for Consistent Results

  • Use a digital kitchen scale – accuracy is key in sourdough.
  • Keep the dough at a stable room temperature – too cold slows fermentation, too warm can over‑proof.
  • Do not over‑knead – the Danish dough whisk provides enough aeration without turning the dough tough.
  • Experiment with olive oils – a high‑smoke‑point oil like grapeseed gives a milder flavor, while a robust extra‑virgin olive oil adds depth.

FAQ

Can I use a different flour blend?

Yes. A 50/50 mix of bread and all‑purpose flour will give a slightly lighter crumb, while adding whole wheat flour can introduce nuttier flavors. Just keep the total flour weight the same.

What if my starter is too thick?

Add a tablespoon of warm water at a time until the starter reaches a thick‑but‑pourable consistency. This helps the dough stay manageable.

How long can I store leftover focaccia?

Wrap it tightly in plastic and keep it at room temperature for up to 2 days. For longer storage, freeze in a freezer‑safe bag; thaw at room temperature before reheating in a 350°F oven for 10 minutes.
