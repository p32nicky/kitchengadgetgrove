Step‑By‑Step Batch‑Prep Process

Follow this routine to have a full week’s supply of ready‑to‑use onion slices:

  1. Gather your tools. You’ll need the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS), a sharp chef’s knife, a large bowl for waste, and a zip‑top bag or airtight container for storage.
  2. Peel and trim. Cut off the root end, then slice a thin piece off the top to create a stable base. This prevents the onion from rolling when you place it into the holder.
  3. Secure the onion. Open the stainless‑steel prongs, slide the onion inside, and close the holder. The onion should sit snugly, with the root side facing up.

Storing Sliced Onions for Peak Freshness

Proper storage is crucial to keep the slices from turning mushy or developing off‑flavors. Here’s how to lock in that crisp bite:

  • Rinse briefly. A quick cold‑water rinse removes excess sulfur compounds that can cause bitterness.
  • Pat dry. Use paper towels to absorb surface moisture; excess water speeds up degradation.
  • Seal airtight. Transfer the slices to a zip‑top bag, squeeze out air, and lay flat in the fridge.
  • Label with date. A simple label helps you rotate stock and avoid waste.

When you need onions for a quick salad or a hot sauté, you’ll have them ready to go, no peeling or chopping required.
