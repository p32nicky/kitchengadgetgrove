Why stainless steel beats plastic and cheap metal

Stainless steel is more than just a shiny finish. Its alloy composition (typically 18‑8 chromium and nickel) creates a passive layer that protects against corrosion, so you won’t see rust spots after weeks of washing. Unlike cheap aluminum or plastic, stainless steel maintains its edge and structural integrity even after repeated exposure to hot water, dishwashers, and acidic foods like tomatoes.

Maintaining your stainless steel kitchen tools

Even the toughest stainless steel needs a little care to stay at its best. Follow these simple steps and your tools will serve you for years:

  1. Hand‑wash when possible: Use warm, soapy water and a non‑abrasive sponge. This prevents tiny scratches that can dull a blade.
  2. Dry immediately: Although stainless steel resists rust, prolonged moisture can cause spotting.
  3. Sharpen knives regularly: A honing steel or stone keeps the edge true; a dull knife forces you to apply more pressure, increasing the chance of slips.
  4. Store smart: Keep knives in a block or magnetic strip, and place other tools in a drawer with a soft liner to avoid clanging.

For the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide, a quick rinse after each use removes food particles that could lodge in the prongs. If you notice water spots, a light wipe with a cloth dampened in vinegar and water will restore the mirror finish.
