Step‑by‑Step: Building the Perfect Frozen Slushie

1. Prep the ice. Fill a zip‑top bag with ice cubes and pulse in the blender for 10‑15 seconds. The goal is a snow‑like texture that will melt quickly if over‑blended.

2. Choose your flavor base. For a tropical vibe, combine pineapple juice, coconut milk, and a splash of lime. For a classic margarita slushie, use tequila, triple sec, lime juice, and a touch of orange juice.

3. Blend with ice. Add a cup of your liquid base to the crushed ice and blend on high for 20‑30 seconds. Stop and stir if needed; you want a thick, spoon‑able consistency.

4. Sweeten to taste. Taste the slushie and add simple syrup or honey if it’s too tart. Blend a few more seconds to incorporate.

5. Pour and garnish. Transfer the slushie into clear cups, then add fresh fruit, a sprig of mint, or a pinch of toasted coconut. Finally, insert a 100-Pack Umbrella Straw for that instant beach‑party feel.

Repeat the process for each flavor you want to offer. A good rule of thumb is to prepare at least three distinct colors—red, green, and blue—to make the bar visually pop.

Tips for Keeping Your Slushies Frozen Longer

Even the best‑crafted slushies can melt quickly if the environment is too warm. Try these simple tricks to keep the frozen fun going:

  • Pre‑chill your glasses. Store them in the freezer for 15‑20 minutes before serving.
  • Use a bowl of ice. Place the cups in a larger bowl filled with ice to maintain a low temperature.
  • Serve in small batches. Only blend what you need for the next 10‑15 minutes; extra slushie can be stored in a shallow pan and refrozen.

These methods work especially well for outdoor gatherings where the sun can melt your frozen treats in minutes.

FAQ

Can I reuse the umbrella straws?

Yes, the 100PCS Umbrella Straw are made of sturdy, food‑safe plastic. Rinse them with warm water after each use if you plan to reuse them for another party.

Do the umbrella straws affect the flavor of the slushie?

No. The straws are purely decorative and do not leach any flavor. They’re designed to sit alongside the drink, so the taste remains unchanged.

What’s the best way to store leftover slushie?

Spread the remaining slushie in a shallow metal pan and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Freeze for up to 24 hours, then give it a quick stir before serving again.
