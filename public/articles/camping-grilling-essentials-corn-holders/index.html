Traditional metal skewers can bend, and wooden sticks often splinter after a few uses. Corn holders solve these problems by giving you a firm grip, keeping the cob upright, and allowing you to rotate the corn without burning your fingers.

How to Use Corn Holders Effectively on the Campfire

Using corn holders is straightforward, but a few tips ensure perfect results every time:

  • Prep the corn: Remove the husk and silk, then brush the kernels with melted butter, oil, or a spice rub.
  • Clamp the cob: Open the holder, slide the cob into the gap, and release the clamp. The silicone handles give you a firm grip without scorching.
  • Grill over indirect heat: Place the cob on the grill grates away from direct flames. Rotate every 2‑3 minutes for even char.
  • Finish with flavor: After grilling, drizzle extra butter, sprinkle salt, or add a squeeze of lime for a burst of taste.

Because the Girla holders interlock, you can pack them flat and pull out only the pair you need, keeping the rest protected from dirt and damage.
