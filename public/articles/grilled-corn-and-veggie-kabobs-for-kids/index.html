Why Kabobs Work for Little Eaters

Skewering corn kernels, cherry tomatoes, and bite‑size pieces of zucchini onto a stick turns them into a fun, handheld snack. Choosing the Right Ingredients

Pick produce that holds up well on the grill and offers a range of textures:

  • Corn on the cob: Cut the cob into 2‑inch sections. The natural sweetness caramelizes quickly.
  • Bell peppers: Use red, orange, or yellow for color. Cut into 1‑inch squares.
  • Zucchini: Slice lengthwise into half‑moons, about ½‑inch thick.
  • Cherry tomatoes: Whole – they pop when they hit the heat.
  • Mushrooms: Baby bellas or button mushrooms work great; they stay juicy.

All of these items are safe for kids to handle with adult supervision, and they all stay on the grill without falling apart.

Preparing the Kabobs

1. Pre‑heat the grill to medium‑high (about 400°F). A hot grill sears the corn and veggies quickly, preventing them from sliding off.

2. Season lightly. Toss the cut veggies in olive oil, a pinch of salt, and a sprinkle of mild paprika or garlic powder. For the corn, a drizzle of butter or a brush of olive oil works wonders.

3. Assemble the skewers. Using stainless‑steel or bamboo skewers, thread a piece of corn, then a bell pepper square, a zucchini slice, a tomato, and repeat until the skewer is full. Keep the pattern consistent so each bite offers a mix of flavors.

When you’re done, set the kabobs on a plate lined with parchment paper. This is where the Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders shine. Slip the silicone‑handled holders onto each corn piece; they keep the hot corn secure while you arrange the skewers, and the interlocking design makes storage a snap after the meal.

Grilling Tips for Kids’ Kabobs

Watch the time. Corn sections need about 8‑10 minutes, while softer veggies like zucchini only need 5‑6 minutes. Turn the skewers every 2‑3 minutes for even char.

Use a grill basket. If you’re worried about small pieces falling through the grates, a perforated grill basket holds everything in place without sacrificing flame‑kissed flavor.

Finish with a drizzle. Once off the heat, toss the kabobs with a quick drizzle of honey‑lime sauce or a sprinkle of shredded cheese for extra kid‑appeal.

Serving Ideas and Side Pairings

Kids love to build their own plates. Offer a few simple sides that complement the smoky kabobs:

  • Mini corn‑bread muffins – perfect for soaking up any leftover butter.
  • Apple slices or watermelon wedges – a fresh, sweet contrast.
  • Yogurt‑based dip – mix plain Greek yogurt with a touch of ranch seasoning for a creamy dip.

FAQ

Can I use wooden skewers with the Girla Corn Holders?

Yes. The silicone handles are designed to grip the corn, not the skewer itself, so any standard bamboo or wooden skewer works fine.

What’s the best way to store leftover grilled corn?
