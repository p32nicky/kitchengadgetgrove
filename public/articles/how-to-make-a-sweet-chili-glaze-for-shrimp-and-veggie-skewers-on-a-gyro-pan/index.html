Instead of flipping pieces of shrimp and peppers on a grill and hoping nothing sticks, a vertical setup lets the flavors drip down and baste the food as it cooks. The Secret to the Perfect Sweet Chili Glaze

The goal for a great glaze is a balance of heat, sweetness, and acidity. If it's too thin, it will just run off your shrimp; if it's too thick, it will burn before the shrimp are cooked through. We want a syrupy consistency that clings to the ingredients.

For this recipe, combine 1/2 cup of Thai sweet chili sauce, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce for umami, a squeeze of fresh lime juice to cut through the sugar, and one minced clove of garlic. If you like a bit more kick, add a teaspoon of Sriracha or red pepper flakes. Whisk these together in a small bowl until smooth. This glaze works perfectly with the Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven because the pan catches the runoff, allowing you to baste the skewers periodically.

Prepping Your Shrimp and Veggies

Consistency is key when building vertical skewers. If you have a giant chunk of bell pepper next to a tiny shrimp, the shrimp will overcook while the pepper remains raw. Aim for uniform sizes.

Use large, peeled, and deveined shrimp (tail-on for better presentation). For the vegetables, I recommend red bell pepper, zucchini, and red onion. Cut the vegetables into 1-inch chunks. Toss the shrimp and veggies in a light coating of olive oil and a pinch of salt before adding the glaze. This creates a barrier that prevents the shrimp from sticking to the metal skewer and ensures the glaze adheres evenly.

Assembling Sweet Chili Glaze Shrimp Veggie Skewers

Now, let's build the tower. Because you are using a stainless steel vertical skewer pan for oven-roasted gyros, kebabs, and more, you have the advantage of stability. You aren't balancing skewers on a rack; you are locking them into a base.

  1. Slide a piece of red onion onto the skewer first to create a sturdy base.
  2. Alternate between a shrimp, a piece of bell pepper, and a slice of zucchini.
  3. Press the ingredients firmly together. The tighter the pack, the more stable the tower will be as it rotates or stays stationary in the pan.
  4. Repeat until the skewer is full, leaving about an inch at the top for the handle.
  5. Brush a generous layer of the sweet chili glaze over the entire assembly.

Cooking in the Vertical Gyro Pan

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place your assembled skewers into the Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven. The genius of this design is that as the heat hits the shrimp, the sweet chili glaze melts and drips down the sides of the tower, naturally self-basting the lower layers.

Roast for about 12 to 15 minutes, depending on the thickness of your shrimp. Halfway through, open the oven and brush more of the remaining glaze onto the top and sides. Because the pan captures all the drippings, you can actually use a pastry brush to scoop up the concentrated glaze from the bottom of the pan and apply it back onto the skewers for an extra-sticky finish.

Serving and Plating Tips

Once the shrimp are opaque and the edges of the vegetables are slightly charred, remove the pan from the oven. Let the skewers rest for five minutes. This allows the glaze to set and thicken, so it doesn't run all over the plate when you serve them.

Slide the shrimp and veggies off the skewer onto a platter. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro, toasted sesame seeds, and an extra lime wedge on the side. The result is a restaurant-quality appetizer with a depth of flavor that you just can't get from a standard baking sheet.

FAQ

Can I use frozen shrimp for these skewers?

Yes, but make sure they are completely thawed and patted dry with paper towels. Excess water will thin out your sweet chili glaze and steam the shrimp instead of roasting them.

How do I prevent the glaze from burning in the oven?

Keep an eye on the sugar content. If you notice the glaze darkening too quickly, lower your oven temperature to 375°F. The vertical position of the gyro pan helps prevent burning by allowing excess glaze to drip away from the heat source.

What other vegetables work well with this glaze?

Pineapple chunks are a fantastic addition to sweet chili glaze shrimp veggie skewers, as the acidity of the fruit complements the sweetness of the sauce. Snap peas or baby corn also work well if you slice them to match the size of the shrimp.
