3. Quick Starter: Overnight Sourdough for a Midnight Toast

For a last‑minute surprise, try an overnight starter that’s ready by midnight. Mix 50g active starter with 200g flour and 140g water. Let it sit at room temperature, then bake a simple loaf the next morning.

  • Morning loaf recipe (makes 1 loaf):
  • 200g whole wheat flour
  • 150g bread flour
  • 350g water (70% hydration)
  • 5g salt
  • Use the whisk to combine, then scrape and fold until smooth.
  • Bulk ferment 3 hours, fold every 30 minutes.
  • Shape, proof 2 hours, score with lame.
  • Bake at 475°F (245°C) for 30 minutes, then lower to 425°F (220°C) for 15 more minutes.

4. Festive Sourdough Variations for the Party Menu

Once you’ve mastered the base loaf, diversify with flavors that suit New Year’s Eve. Here are three crowd‑pleasers:

  1. Herb & Cheese Sourdough – add 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, 1 cup shredded cheddar, and a pinch of cracked pepper to the dough before the final fold.
  2. Olive & Tomato Sourdough – fold in ½ cup chopped Kalamata olives and ½ cup sun‑dried tomatoes, then roll into a baguette shape for a Mediterranean twist.
  3. Honey‑Glazed Sourdough – brush the scored loaf with a mixture of honey, melted butter, and a dash of cinnamon after baking for a sweet, crisp finish.

FAQ

What’s the best type of flour for New Year’s Eve sourdough?

Use a blend of whole wheat and bread flour for a sturdy crumb and good rise. Whole wheat adds flavor, while bread flour provides the gluten structure needed for a crisp crust.

Can I make these loaves in a bread machine?

It’s possible, but the machine may not allow you to use the 3‑in‑1 Sourdough Whisk Set for scoring. For the best texture and finish, hand‑mixing and baking on a baking stone is recommended.

How long can I keep leftover sourdough?

Store in a paper bag at room temperature for up to 3 days, or freeze sliced loaves for up to 2 months. Reheat in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 5–7 minutes to restore crispness.
