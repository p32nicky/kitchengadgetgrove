Choosing the Right Onion for Low-Calorie Salads

For a traditional Greek salad, red onions are the non-negotiable choice. Not only do they provide a beautiful pop of purple color against the green cucumbers, but they also offer a milder, sweeter flavor profile than yellow or white onions.

To keep the dish low-calorie, focus on the ratios. Onions are naturally low in calories, but the way you prepare them affects how much dressing you'll need. When you slice your onions extremely thin, they absorb the lemon juice and vinegar more efficiently, meaning you can use less oil while still ensuring every bite is flavorful.

Step-by-Step: How to Slice Onions Greek Salad Low Calorie Style

Once you have your red onion and your tools ready, follow these steps to get the perfect salad base:

  • Prep the Onion: Cut the onion in half from root to stem. Peel back the papery skin and remove the root end.
  • Set the Guide: Insert the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS) into the flat side of the onion half.
  • The Slice: Using a sharp chef's knife, slice across the onion. The prongs act as a stop, ensuring you don't cut too deep and that every piece is a uniform, thin sliver.
  • The Soak: If you find raw red onions too sharp, soak your thin slices in ice water for 10 minutes. This removes the harsh "bite" without adding calories.
  • Drain and Dry: Pat the onions dry with a paper towel. This prevents the salad from becoming watery and helps the dressing cling to the vegetables.

Assembling the Low-Calorie Greek Salad

Now that you have your perfectly sliced onions, it's time to build the rest of the salad. To keep the calorie count down, focus on high-volume, water-rich vegetables.

Start with diced English cucumbers (which have thinner skin and fewer seeds) and halved cherry tomatoes. Add a handful of Kalamata olives for a salty punch and a small amount of crumbled feta. Since feta is calorie-dense, use a high-quality sheep's milk feta—it has a stronger flavor, so you can use less of it while still getting that characteristic tang.

For the dressing, skip the bottled versions which are often loaded with sugar. Whisk together fresh lemon juice, a splash of red wine vinegar, dried oregano, and a small drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Toss the mixture gently so the thin onion slices coat every other ingredient.

FAQ

Can I use white onions instead of red for a Greek salad?

You can, but red onions are preferred for their color and slightly sweeter taste. If you use white onions, be sure to slice them even thinner, as they can be more pungent and may overwhelm the delicate flavor of the feta.

How do I stop onions from making me cry?

Using a very sharp knife helps by crushing fewer cells, which releases less of the gas that irritates your eyes. Additionally, using an Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS) allows you to work more efficiently and quickly, reducing the time you spend hovering over the onion.

What is the best way to store sliced onions for later?

Store your sliced onions in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To keep them crisp, place a damp paper towel inside the container to maintain humidity without making them soggy.
