Step-by-Step: Batch-Baking Sourdough Buns for a Picnic

Follow these steps using your 3-in-1 set for consistent results:

  1. Mix the dough: Whisk flour, water, and levain until combined, then use the Danish whisk to knead for 10 minutes.
  2. Proof in batches: Fold dough with the scraper every 30 minutes during bulk fermentation to build strength.
  3. Shape and rest: Portion dough into buns, shape with the scraper, and let rest for 20 minutes.
  4. Score and bake: Use the lame to score buns, then bake at 450°F (230°C) with steam for 20–25 minutes.

Pro Tips for Perfect Picnic Buns

• Freeze shaped buns for up to 3 months. Thaw and bake fresh just before your picnic.
• Use the scraper to divide dough cleanly, avoiding sticky messes.
• Score at an angle with the lame for even baking and a professional finish.

FAQ

Can I batch-bake sourdough buns without a proofing basket?

A: Yes! Line a rimmed baking sheet with a linen cloth and let buns proof together. The scraper helps lift them cleanly later.

How do I store leftover buns for the picnic?

A: Store cooled buns in airtight bags or containers. Reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 5 minutes before serving to restore crispness.
