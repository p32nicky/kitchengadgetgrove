1. Caramelized Onion & Brie Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet onion, sliced with the Onion Holder
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • 4 oz Brie, cut into small squares
  • Mini baguette slices, toasted
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Heat butter and oil in a skillet over medium‑low heat. Add the onion rings and a pinch of salt, stirring occasionally for 15‑20 minutes until golden and soft. Spoon a small amount of caramelized onion onto each toasted baguette slice, top with a Brie square, and finish with a thyme leaf. The sweet onion balances the creamy Brie, creating a bite that feels festive without extra garnish.

2. Pickled Red Onion & Smoked Salmon Canapés

Quick pickled onions add a tangy crunch that cuts through the richness of smoked salmon. Slice a red onion with the Onion Holder, then submerge the rings in a mixture of ½ cup apple cider vinegar, ¼ cup water, 1 tbsp sugar, and ½ tsp salt for 30 minutes. Drain and pat dry.

To assemble, spread a thin layer of herbed cream cheese on a cucumber round or rye cracker, add a slice of smoked salmon, then top with a pickled onion ring. Garnish with a sprig of dill. The bright acidity of the pickled onion keeps the palate refreshed between bites.

3. Onion & Goat Cheese Stuffed Dates

Ingredients:

  • 12 Medjool dates, pitted
  • 4 oz goat cheese, softened
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped chives
  • 2 tbsp finely diced onion (use the Onion Holder)
  • Crushed pistachios for coating

Mix goat cheese, chives, and diced onion together. Fill each date with the cheese mixture, then roll the stuffed end in crushed pistachios. The creamy goat cheese meets the sweet date, while a hint of raw onion adds a surprising bite that guests love.

4. Mini Onion & Mushroom Tartlets

Use pre‑made phyllo shells or mini tartlet pans. Sauté a blend of sliced onions (again, sliced with the Onion Holder) and sliced mushrooms in butter until the moisture evaporates and the mixture is lightly browned. Stir in a splash of white wine, season with thyme, and let cool.

Fill each tartlet with the onion‑mushroom mixture, top with a dollop of ricotta, and bake at 375°F for 8‑10 minutes until the shells are crisp. The earthy mushrooms complement the sweet onion, delivering a sophisticated flavor in a bite‑size format.

5. Fresh Onion & Avocado Crostini

For a vegan option, mash ripe avocado with lemon juice, salt, and a dash of cumin. Spread the mash onto toasted ciabatta slices, then layer a few thin raw onion rings (cut with the Onion Holder) on top. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of smoked paprika. The creamy avocado and crisp onion create a refreshing contrast perfect for holiday gatherings.

Tips for Efficient Holiday Appetizer Prep

1. Batch slice early. Use the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide to slice all onions at once—store the rings in a sealed container in the fridge.

2. Pre‑assemble on a tray. Arrange each canapé on a single platter, cover with parchment, and refrigerate. This keeps flavors melded and reduces last‑minute stress.

3. Mind the temperature. Warm items (like caramelized onion) should be served slightly hot; cold items (like avocado crostini) stay fresher when kept chilled.

FAQ

Can I use the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide for vegetables other than onions?

Absolutely. The stainless‑steel prongs are designed to grip a range of firm produce—tomatoes, potatoes, and even small cucumbers work well. Just adjust the pressure to avoid crushing softer items.

Is the tool dishwasher safe?

Yes, the entire unit is stainless steel and can be placed in the dishwasher. Hand‑washing with warm, soapy water also keeps the edges sharp and prevents any residual food from sticking.

How do I prevent the onion slices from drying out after slicing?

Store the sliced onions in an airtight container with a damp paper towel over the top. This maintains moisture and keeps the rings crisp for up to 24 hours, giving you flexibility when assembling your holiday canapés.
