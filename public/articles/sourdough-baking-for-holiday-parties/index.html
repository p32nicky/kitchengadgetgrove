Prep Your Starter for Holiday Baking

Before you dive into a marathon of baking, your starter needs to be in peak condition. If your starter has been hibernating in the fridge, give it two or three feedings over a few days to wake it up. You want it doubling in size consistently before you attempt a festive batch of rolls or a celebratory sourdough focaccia.

Time-Saving Tips for the Holiday Rush

The biggest hurdle with sourdough is the timeline. To avoid spending your entire holiday Eve in the kitchen, leverage the power of the cold ferment. After your bulk fermentation, shape your loaves and place them in the refrigerator overnight.

  • Cold Proofing: Baking directly from the fridge the next morning improves the flavor and makes the dough easier to score.
  • Batch Prepping: Mix multiple doughs at once and freeze shaped loaves (before the final proof) for later in the month.
  • Starter Maintenance: Keep a separate "discard" jar in the fridge for quick recipes like pancakes or crackers so you don't deplete your active culture.

FAQ

Can I use sourdough discard for holiday appetizers?

Absolutely. Sourdough discard is fantastic for crackers, savory biscuits, and even as a base for a tangy holiday tart crust. It's a great way to reduce waste while adding complex flavor to your party snacks.

How do I stop my starter from sticking to the jar?

The best way to prevent buildup is to use a skinny stirring stick or spurtle to scrape the sides during every feeding. This prevents a "crust" from forming at the top and ensures the yeast is evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

When should I start my dough for a party?

Plan for a 24 to 48-hour window. Feed your starter the day before you intend to mix your dough, and use a cold overnight proof for the final rise to ensure your bread is fresh and warm right as your guests arrive.
