Sourdough Cinnamon Swirl Buns Recipe: A Sweet Twist on Sourdough

Looking to elevate your sourdough game? These sourdough cinnamon swirl buns combine the tangy depth of naturally leavened dough with warm cinnamon swirls for a bakery-style treat. Why Sourdough Cinnamon Swirl Buns Stand Out

Unlike quick-rise cinnamon rolls, sourdough buns develop complex flavors through long fermentation. The slow rise lets the cinnamon, brown sugar, and dough harmonize, creating a deep, caramelized sweetness. Plus, sourdough’s natural tang balances the spice without needing extra sugar.

Ingredients for 12 Buns

  • 1 cup warm water (105°F/40°C)
  • 1/2 cup active sourdough starter
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 3 1/2 cups bread flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 2 tbsp melted butter, cooled
  • Cinnamon-sugar swirl: 1/4 cup brown sugar + 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Glaze (optional): 1 cup powdered sugar + 2-3 tbsp milk

Step-by-Step Baking Process

  1. Hydrate the sourdough starter: Mix water, starter, honey, and salt in a bowl (use the Danish dough whisk for smooth blending).
  2. Feed the dough: Gradually add flour, then knead until smooth (5-7 minutes by hand or 3 minutes with a stand mixer).
  3. Rise slowly: Cover and let rest at room temp for 4-6 hours, or overnight in the fridge for 8-12 hours.
  4. Shape the buns: Turn dough onto a floured surface (use the dough scraper to divide), roll into a rectangle, spread softened butter, sprinkle cinnamon-sugar, roll up, and slice into 12 pieces.
  5. Prove: Place on a baking sheet, cover, and let rise for 1 1/2 hours.
  6. Score and bake: Use the bread scoring lame to gently score the tops, then bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-22 minutes until golden.

Serving and Storage Tips

Glaze buns immediately after baking with a drizzle of powdered sugar and milk mixture. Serve warm or at room temperature. Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days; freeze baked or unbaked for longer storage. Reheat in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes to restore crispness.

FAQ

Can I use all-purpose flour instead of bread flour?

Yes, but bread flour’s higher protein content gives better rise and structure. If substituting, add 1/2 tsp vital wheat gluten.

How do I know my sourdough starter is active?

Feed your starter 8 hours before mixing; it should be bubbly and doubled in size. If it’s sluggish, give it an extra feeding.

Can I make these vegan?

Replace honey with maple syrup and use vegan butter. The recipe will work as-is with these swaps.
