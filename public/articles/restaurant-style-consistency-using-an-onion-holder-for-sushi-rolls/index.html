Step‑by‑Step: Using the Onion Holder for Sushi Rolls

Follow these steps to get professional‑grade onion slices without a sharp learning curve:

  1. Prep the onion. Peel and halve a medium‑size sweet or red onion. Rinse and pat dry.
  2. Secure the halves. Open the stainless‑steel prongs, slide one half into the holder, and close the clamp. The onion should sit snugly without crushing.
  3. Choose the right blade. A sharp, non‑serrated chef’s knife works best for clean, consistent cuts. If you have a mandoline, you can use it, but the holder still adds stability.
  4. Slice. With a gentle rocking motion, glide the knife over the onion. Aim for a smooth, continuous cut—stop the motion before the knife reaches the holder’s prongs to avoid nicking the steel.
  5. Release and repeat. Open the holder, remove the sliced half, and repeat with the other side.

After slicing, keep the onion rings in a bowl of ice water for a minute; this firms them up and reduces any bitter edge before you layer them onto the rice.

FAQ

Do I need a special knife to use the onion holder?

No. A standard chef’s knife with a sharp edge works perfectly. The holder’s purpose is to keep the vegetable steady, not to replace the knife.

Can the stainless steel prongs damage delicate vegetables like tomatoes?

The prongs are designed to grip without crushing. For very soft items, apply just enough pressure to hold the piece in place; you’ll still get clean slices.

Is the onion holder dishwasher safe?

Yes, the stainless‑steel construction can be placed on the top rack of a dishwasher, but hand‑washing is recommended to preserve the anti‑cut coating longer.
