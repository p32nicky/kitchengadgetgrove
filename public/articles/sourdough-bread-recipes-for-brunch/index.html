Savory Sourdough Options for Brunch

For a savory start, try a Cheddar and Jalapeño Sourdough. During the fold-in process, add two cups of sharp shredded cheddar and a handful of diced pickled jalapeños. The acidity of the sourdough balances the richness of the cheese perfectly.

Another crowd-pleaser is a Roasted Garlic and Rosemary loaf. Roast a whole head of garlic until soft, squeeze the cloves into your dough, and add fresh chopped rosemary. This creates a fragrant bread that pairs beautifully with poached eggs and hollandaise sauce.

When mixing these heavier inclusions, a Danish dough whisk is invaluable. Sweet Sourdough Bread Recipes for Brunch

Sourdough isn't just for savory dishes. A Cinnamon Raisin Sourdough is a fantastic addition to any brunch spread. To make this, incorporate cinnamon, brown sugar, and soaked raisins during the bulk fermentation phase.

For something more decadent, try a Sourdough Brioche. By adding butter and eggs to your sourdough base, you get a pillowy, rich bread that is perfect for French toast. Slice it thick, soak it in a vanilla-cinnamon custard, and sear it in butter for a restaurant-quality breakfast.

Tips for Perfecting Your Brunch Loaves

To ensure your brunch breads have that professional look and texture, keep these practical tips in mind:

  • Temperature Matters: Use lukewarm water when feeding your starter to speed up the process if you're on a tight brunch timeline.
  • Don't Skip the Score: Scoring the top of your bread isn't just for aesthetics; it controls where the bread expands so it doesn't burst randomly.
  • Cold Proofing: Let your shaped loaves sit in the fridge overnight. This develops a more complex flavor and makes the dough easier to score.
  • Steam Your Oven: Use a Dutch oven or a tray of water at the bottom of the oven to create steam, which helps achieve a crisp, golden crust.

Planning Your Sourdough Brunch Timeline

The biggest challenge with sourdough bread recipes for brunch is the timing. Since sourdough takes significantly longer than commercial yeast, you need a plan. I recommend starting your process on Friday evening by feeding your starter.

Mix your dough on Saturday morning and allow it to undergo bulk fermentation throughout the day. Shape your loaves in the evening and place them in the refrigerator for a cold overnight proof. This means all you have to do on Sunday morning is preheat the oven and bake.

By using a consistent set of tools, you reduce the friction of the process. Having your whisk, scraper, and lame all in one kit means you aren't hunting through drawers while your dough is over-proofing.

FAQ

Can I use these recipes with a discard starter?

Yes! Many sweet sourdough brunch recipes, like pancakes or cinnamon rolls, are designed specifically for sourdough discard. For hearty loaves, however, you will need an active, bubbly starter to ensure the bread rises properly.

How do I store leftover brunch sourdough?

Sourdough stays fresh for a few days if kept in a paper bag or a bread box. For longer storage, slice the bread and freeze it with parchment paper between slices. Toast them directly from the freezer for a fresh-baked taste.

What is the best way to clean a Danish dough whisk?

Avoid letting dough dry on the whisk. Rinse it immediately under warm water or give it a quick soak in soapy water right after use. Because of the open design, a quick scrub with a brush usually does the trick.
