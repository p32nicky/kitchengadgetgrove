Step-by-Step Summer BBQ Slaw Onion Prep

Follow these steps to use the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide for your slaw:

  1. Peel and halve the onion. For a slaw, remove the root end with a small knife to make it easier to secure.
  2. Insert the halved onion into the tool’s prongs, stem end facing up. The metal grip keeps it from shifting.
  3. Position a sharp knife against the blade guide (if your model has one) or use the tool alone. Slice downward to create even rounds or strips.
  4. Release the onion after each slice for uniformity. For julienned onions, use the narrowest blade setting if available.

This method takes less than five minutes and leaves your hands free of raw onion residue—ideal for handling other slaw components or grilling.

Pro Tips for Elevating Your BBQ Slaw

Once your onions are prepped, balance their sharpness with sweet and tangy elements. A mix of red and yellow onions adds color and flavor depth. For extra kick, toss the raw slices with apple cider vinegar for 10 minutes before adding to the slaw.
