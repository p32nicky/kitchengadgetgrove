How to Attach Clues to Your Murder Mystery Cocktail Mini Drink Umbrella

The trick to using these umbrellas for clues is the "roll and tuck" method. You don't want the paper to soak in the drink, so the placement is key. Here is the best way to do it:

  • Cut your clues small: Use thin strips of parchment or aged paper (you can stain white paper with tea for a vintage look).
  • Roll tightly: Roll the clue into a very thin cylinder and secure it with a tiny piece of clear tape or a microscopic dab of glue.
  • The Tuck: Gently slide the rolled clue into the folds of the umbrella canopy before you open it.
  • The Deployment: Open the umbrella carefully. The clue should remain nestled against the center stick, protected from the liquid.
  • The Placement: Insert the pick into a piece of fruit (like a pineapple wedge or maraschino cherry) to create a buffer between the clue and the cocktail.

Cleaning Up and Managing the Evidence

Once a guest has extracted their clue, they'll likely leave the umbrella in the glass. To avoid a cluttered bar, provide a designated "Evidence Bin" where guests can drop their used cocktail picks. This keeps your glassware clean and gives you a way to track which clues have been found.

If you find that some guests are hesitant to "destroy" the cute decoration to get the clue, give them a hint at the start of the night. A small sign on the bar saying "The truth is hidden in the parasols" is usually enough to get the detective instincts flowing.

FAQ

Will the paper clues get soggy in the drink?

If you tuck the clue into the canopy and use a fruit garnish (like a cherry or lime slice) to lift the umbrella slightly above the liquid line, the paper should stay dry. For extra protection, you can wrap the clue in a tiny piece of plastic wrap before rolling.

Can I use these umbrellas for food instead of drinks?

Absolutely. The 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks are perfect for appetizers. You can stick them into sliders, caprese skewers, or stuffed mushrooms to deliver clues via the food platter.

How many umbrellas do I need for a medium-sized party?
