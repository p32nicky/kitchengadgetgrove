Step‑by‑Step: Setting Up the Magic Pull‑Out Pizza Slice

1. Prepare the pizza. Bake or heat a pizza until the cheese is fully melted but the crust stays firm. Let it cool for a minute so the cheese sets slightly—this prevents the slice from sliding off the fork.

2. Cut a single slice. Using a pizza cutter, cut one slice that’s about 1‑inch wide at the crust. The narrower the slice, the easier it is to hide behind the fork’s tines.

3. Position the telescopic fork. Take one of the Extendable Prank Forks and slide it into the cardboard or napkin, leaving the tines just barely visible along the edge. The fork should be in its shortest (8.7") position at this stage.

4. Slip the slice onto the fork. Gently slide the pizza slice onto the fork’s tines, with the crust facing down. The fork’s metal tines will hold the slice in place without crushing it.

5. Conceal the fork. Fold the napkin or cardboard around the fork, leaving only a small opening where the tines peek out. If you’re using a pizza box, you can tuck the fork into the box’s edge so it’s invisible from the front.

6. Extend the fork at the right moment. When you’re ready to wow your audience, grip the handle and pull it outward. The fork will smoothly extend to its full 25.4" length, pulling the slice out of its hiding spot as if by magic.

Practice the motion a few times without the pizza first. The smoother the extension, the more convincing the illusion.

Tips for a Flawless Presentation

Even the best gag can fall flat if the execution is shaky. Here are a few pro tips:

  • Keep the fork angled. A slight upward angle helps the slice glide out cleanly and reduces the chance of the cheese tearing.
  • Use a lightweight slice. Over‑topped pizza can be heavy, making the fork struggle. A slice with moderate toppings works best.
  • Practice in front of a mirror. Watching yourself helps you perfect the timing and the look of the “pull‑out” motion.
  • Distract with a joke. A quick one‑liner right before the pull draws attention away from the fork’s handle.

Cleaning Up: Maintaining Your Extendable Prank Fork
