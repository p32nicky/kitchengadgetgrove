Choosing the Right Artisan Flour

Artisan flour isn’t a single product; it’s a family of grain varieties that bring distinct flavor, texture, and nutrition to sourdough. Here are three reliable options that complement the wooden spurtle:

  1. Unbleached Bread Flour (12‑14% protein): Provides strong gluten development for a chewy crumb and crisp crust.
  2. Whole Wheat Flour (13‑15% protein): Adds nutty depth and a denser crumb, perfect for rustic loaves.
  3. Rye Flour (8‑10% protein): Gives a tangy aroma and a moist interior; often used for starter feeds.

When selecting artisan flour for a gift set, look for a flour that’s stone‑milled and free of additives. The fresher the grain, the more lively the starter will be—something the wooden spurtle helps you keep clean and active.

Holiday Cranberry‑Orange Sourdough Loaf

Mix 2 cups of unbleached bread flour, 1 cup of whole wheat flour, 1 ½ tsp salt, and 1 tsp instant yeast. In a separate bowl, combine 1 cup of active sourdough starter, ¾ cup water, and the zest of one orange. Use the skinny spurtle to blend the wet ingredients, then fold in ½ cup dried cranberries. Let the dough rise, shape, and bake at 425°F for 30‑35 minutes. The spurtle’s thin tip makes it easy to incorporate the cranberries without tearing the dough.

Spiced Rye Flatbread

Combine 1 ½ cups rye flour, ½ cup bread flour, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp cinnamon, and ¼ tsp nutmeg. Add 1 cup sourdough starter and ¾ cup warm water. Stir with the Danish‑style whisk until a shaggy dough forms, then let rest for 30 minutes. Roll out thin, brush with melted butter, and bake at 450°F for 8‑10 minutes. The whisk’s gentle motion keeps the rye from becoming overly dense.

Care Tips for the Wooden Spurtle

To keep the spurtle looking beautiful year after year, treat it like a wooden cutting board:

  • Rinse with warm water after each use; avoid soaking.
  • Dry immediately with a clean towel.
  • Occasionally rub a thin layer of food‑grade mineral oil to prevent drying.
  • Avoid exposing the wood to harsh detergents or dishwashers.

These simple steps preserve the wood’s natural antimicrobial properties, ensuring your spurtle remains a safe, non‑scratch utensil for all future baking adventures.
