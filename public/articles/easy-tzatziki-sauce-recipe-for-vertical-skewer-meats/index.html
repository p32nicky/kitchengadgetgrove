Tzatziki Sauce Basics

The base of tzatziki sauce is Greek yogurt, which provides a creamy texture. You can use low-fat or non-fat yogurt, depending on your preference. I prefer to use full-fat yogurt for a richer flavor. Next, you'll need to grate some cucumbers and add them to the yogurt. Make sure to use an English cucumber, as it has fewer seeds and a milder flavor. Don't over-grate the cucumbers, as they can become too watery.

Key Ingredients for Tzatziki Sauce

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (full-fat or low-fat)
  • 1 large English cucumber, peeled and grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to Make Tzatziki Sauce

To make the tzatziki sauce, combine the yogurt, grated cucumber, garlic, dill, and lemon juice in a bowl. Mix well and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Before serving, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

FAQ

Q: Can I make tzatziki sauce ahead of time?

A: Yes, you can make tzatziki sauce ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Just give it a good stir before serving.

Q: What type of yogurt should I use for tzatziki sauce?

A: You can use either full-fat or low-fat Greek yogurt for tzatziki sauce. Full-fat yogurt will give you a richer flavor, while low-fat yogurt will be lighter and healthier.

Q: Can I add other ingredients to tzatziki sauce?

A: Yes, you can add other ingredients to tzatziki sauce to give it more flavor and texture. Some options include diced onions, chopped bell peppers, or minced jalapenos. Just be sure to adjust the seasoning accordingly.
