To build distinct cheese layers, mix separate doughs with varying cheeses (like cheddar, mozzarella, or goat cheese) using your wooden spurtle. The tool’s flexibility helps you gently lift and fold each layer, ensuring clean, crisp strata without collapsing the dough. Here’s how:
  1. Prepare base dough with a primary cheese blend.
  2. Use the spurtle to shape a well in the center for a secondary cheese layer.
  3. Fold the outer dough over the filling, repeating until layers form.

FAQ

What cheeses pair best with layered bread?

Melt-rich cheeses like gouda, provolone, and sharp cheddar create distinct, gooey layers without overpowering the bread’s structure. Soft cheeses add moisture, while harder varieties offer sharp flavor bursts.

How does a wooden spurtle improve mixing?

The spurtle’s tapered shape prevents overmixing by limiting contact with the dough. It also scrapes jars efficiently, reducing waste and ensuring every bit of cheese or starter is used.

Can I use the spurtle for other baking tasks?

Absolutely. It’s excellent for folding in eggs, blending cookie dough, or stirring pancake batter. The wooden material won’t react with acidic ingredients like buttermilk or sourdough cultures.
