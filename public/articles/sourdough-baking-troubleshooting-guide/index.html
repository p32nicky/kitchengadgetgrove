1. Sticky Dough That Won’t Shape

Over-hydrated dough is a common culprit. If your dough feels more like a batter than a dough, adjust your hydration level next time. In the meantime, use a dough scraper (included in the Sourdough Whisk Set) to handle sticky dough without overworking it. Scrape excess flour onto the dough as you fold it, which helps build strength without adding too much flour directly.

2. Collapsed or Flat Loaves

Under-proofing or over-proofing can lead to sad, deflated bread. To test readiness, press the dough gently: if it springs back slowly, it’s proofed. For better structure, use the danish dough whisk from your starter kit to gently stretch and fold the dough during bulk fermentation. This builds gluten without the mess of floured hands.

3. Uneven Crust or Poor Rise

An inconsistent oven temperature or lack of steam can kill your loaf’s rise. Preheat your Dutch oven thoroughly and add steam via a water sprayer or baking on the bottom rack with a tray of hot water. For scoring, the bread scoring lame in the Sourdough Whisk Set lets you make precise cuts, ensuring even expansion in the oven.

4. Sourdough Starter That Won’t Activate

A sluggish starter can derail your whole process. Feed it consistently with equal parts flour and water (by weight), and keep it at room temperature until active. Once bubbly and fragrant, it’s ready to use. The dough whisk helps mix your starter smoothly, ensuring no lumps for consistent feeding.

5. Burning the Crust Too Fast

High heat is key for sourdough, but it can lead to a dark crust before the interior is done. Lower the oven temperature by 25°F (15°C) for the final 10–15 minutes. If your loaf is browning too quickly, tent it with parchment paper—not foil—which maintains steam while protecting the crust.

FAQ

What’s the best way to handle sticky sourdough dough?

Use a dough scraper to minimize contact and add small amounts of flour as needed while folding. Avoid over-flouring, which dries out the dough.

Can I bake sourdough without a lame?

A sharp paring knife works in a pinch, but a lame provides cleaner cuts and better oven spring. The Sourdough Whisk Set includes a durable lame for precise scoring.

How do I know if my sourdough is over-proofed?

Over-proofed dough collapses when pressed and smells overly sour. Under-proofed dough springs back quickly. Aim for a balance—slow, steady proofing yields the best results.
