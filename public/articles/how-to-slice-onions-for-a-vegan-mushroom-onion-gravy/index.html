Choosing the Right Onion for Your Gravy

While any onion works in a pinch, yellow onions are the gold standard for gravy. They have a high sugar content that breaks down during the sautéing process, providing a savory-sweet base that complements the earthiness of mushrooms. If you want a sharper bite, go with white onions; for a more refined, delicate sweetness, shallots are a fantastic alternative.

Before you start slicing, make sure your knife is sharp. A dull blade doesn't just make the work harder; it crushes the onion cells, which releases more of the sulfurous gas that makes you cry. A clean, sharp slice keeps the onion intact and the tears at bay.

Step-by-Step: How to Slice Onions Vegan Mushroom Gravy Needs

Consistency is key. If some onion pieces are thick, they will stay crunchy while the thin ones burn. Follow these steps for the perfect cut:

  • Prep the Onion: Cut the onion in half from root to stem. Peel back the papery skin and remove the root end, but leave a tiny bit of the base intact to hold the layers together.
  • Secure the Vegetable: Press the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide firmly into the flat side of the onion half. This ensures the onion doesn't roll on the cutting board.
  • The Slicing Motion: Using a chef's knife, slice across the onion perpendicular to the grain. Move the holder over by a few millimeters after each cut.
  • Fine-Tuning: If you want a more refined texture, turn the onion 90 degrees and slice across your previous cuts to create a small dice.

Sautéing for Maximum Flavor

Now that you have your uniform slices, the magic happens in the pan. Heat a tablespoon of vegan butter or olive oil over medium heat. Add your onions and a pinch of salt immediately. The salt draws out moisture, allowing the onions to soften and brown without sticking.

Avoid the temptation to crank the heat to high. For a vegan mushroom gravy, you want the onions to reach a golden-brown caramelization. This slow process develops the complex sugars that replace the richness usually found in beef-based stocks. Once the onions are translucent and golden, add your sliced mushrooms and continue sautéing until the mushrooms release their liquid and brown.

FAQ

Can I use a food processor to slice onions for gravy?

You can, but be careful. Food processors often "pulse" the onions into uneven chunks or turn them into a mushy paste. For a professional gravy, hand-slicing with a tool like an onion holder provides the structural integrity needed for the best texture.

How do I stop my eyes from watering while slicing?

Using a very sharp knife is the first step. Additionally, chilling your onions in the fridge for 15 minutes before slicing can slow down the release of the enzymes that cause irritation.

What is the best mushroom to pair with sliced onions in gravy?

Cremini or Baby Bella mushrooms are excellent for everyday gravy due to their balance of flavor. For a more "meaty," umami-rich gravy, try a mix of Shiitake and Oyster mushrooms.
