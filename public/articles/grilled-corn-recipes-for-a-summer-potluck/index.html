Classic Garlic Butter & Herb Grilled Corn

You can't go wrong with the classics. This recipe is the gold standard for grilled corn recipes for potluck crowds because it appeals to everyone. The key is to grill the corn naked first to get those deep brown char marks, then apply the compound butter while the kernels are still sizzling.

Mix softened unsalted butter with minced garlic, fresh parsley, a pinch of smoked paprika, and salt. Brush this generously over the corn immediately after removing it from the grill. The silicone handles provide a steady grip, allowing guests to enjoy the garlic butter without the grease.

Street-Style Mexican Elote (Potluck Version)

Traditional Elote is delicious, but slathering corn in crema and cotija cheese right before serving can make it a bit unstable for a buffet line. For a potluck-friendly version, I recommend a "deconstructed" approach or a tighter coating.

Grill your corn until charred. Instead of a thin sauce, use a mixture of mayonnaise, sour cream, and lime juice to create a thick paste. Spread this on the cob, roll it in crumbled cotija cheese and chili powder, and finish with fresh cilantro. Because this version is heavier, those Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders are essential—they keep the corn stable so the toppings don't slide off onto the guest's clothing.

Sweet and Spicy Honey-Sriracha Corn

If you want something that stands out from the usual buttered corn, go for a sweet-and-heat profile. This recipe is a hit with people who love a bit of a kick. It works particularly well if you are serving other savory BBQ meats like pulled pork or brisket.

Whisk together honey, Sriracha, a splash of soy sauce, and a squeeze of lime. Brush this glaze on the corn during the last 3-5 minutes of grilling. The sugars in the honey will caramelize and create a sticky, glossy finish. Pro Tips for Perfect Potluck Corn

To ensure your corn stays juicy and flavorful from the grill to the table, keep these practical tips in mind:

  • Don't overcook: Corn only needs about 10-12 minutes over medium-high heat. If you leave it too long, the kernels shrink and become chewy.
  • The Foil Wrap Trick: If you're transporting corn to a potluck, wrap the grilled cobs loosely in foil with a few pats of butter. This steams the corn slightly and keeps it warm for up to 30 minutes.
  • Prep the Holders: Insert your corn skewers before the corn cools down. It's much easier to pierce the cob when the heat has softened the core.
  • Keep it Tidy: Use a wide platter lined with parchment paper to catch any dripping butter or sauce.

FAQ

Should I peel the corn before grilling?

For potluck recipes where you want char marks and direct seasoning, yes, peel them. If you prefer a more steamed, tender kernel, you can grill them in the husk, but you'll have to peel them and season them immediately after, which can be messy when serving a large group.

How do I keep grilled corn warm for a potluck?

The best method is to place the grilled corn in a slow cooker on the "Warm" setting or wrap them in heavy-duty aluminum foil and place them in an insulated cooler (without ice) to retain the heat.

Can I make these corn recipes on a stovetop grill pan?

Absolutely. Use a cast-iron grill pan over medium-high heat. Rotate the corn every few minutes to ensure an even char on all sides. The results are nearly identical to an outdoor grill for these specific recipes.
