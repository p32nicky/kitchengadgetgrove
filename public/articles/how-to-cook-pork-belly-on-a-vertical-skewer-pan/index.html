Preparing the Pork Belly for the Skewer

Start with a 2‑ to 3‑pound piece of pork belly with the skin on. If the skin is thick, score it in a cross‑hatch pattern about ¼ inch deep; this helps the fat render and the skin crisp.

Seasoning is where you can get creative, but a basic rub that highlights the pork’s natural richness works every time:

  1. Combine 2 tbsp kosher salt, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp smoked paprika, ½ tsp ground cumin, and a pinch of black pepper.
  2. Pat the pork belly dry, then rub the mixture all over the meat, making sure to get it into the scored skin.
  3. Let the seasoned pork rest uncovered in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours—or overnight—for a dry‑brine effect that intensifies flavor and helps the skin dry out.

When you’re ready to cook, bring the pork to room temperature (about 30 minutes) so it cooks evenly.

Cooking Process: From Crispy Skin to Tender Meat

Follow this timeline for consistent results:

  1. Initial sear (425 °F/220 °C): Roast for 12‑15 minutes. The skin should start to puff and turn golden.
  2. Reduce heat (350 °F/175 °C): Lower the oven temperature and continue cooking for 45‑60 minutes, depending on the thickness of the belly. The internal temperature should reach 190 °F (88 °C) for melt‑in‑the‑mouth texture.
  3. Final crisp (425 °F/220 °C): Turn the oven back up for the last 5‑7 minutes to achieve that crackly skin. Keep a close eye—once the skin pops, it can go from perfect to burnt quickly.

Let the pork belly rest for 10‑15 minutes before slicing. Resting allows the juices to redistribute and the skin to set, making clean cuts easier.

Serving Ideas and Pairings

Once you’ve mastered the pork belly vertical skewer pan technique, the possibilities are endless:

  • Taco night: Warm corn tortillas, top with thin slices of pork belly, fresh cilantro, diced onion, and a squeeze of lime.
  • Shawarma‑style platter: Slice the pork thin, serve over pita with garlic sauce, pickled veggies, and a drizzle of tahini.
  • Kebabs: Cube the cooked pork belly, thread onto skewers with bell peppers and red onion, then finish under the broiler for a quick char.

The rendered fat collected in the drip tray is a treasure—use it to sauté potatoes, roast Brussels sprouts, or as a base for a rich pork‑infused gravy.

Cleaning and Maintenance of Your Vertical Skewer Pan

After the oven cools, disassemble the pan. The stainless‑steel surfaces are dishwasher‑safe, but a quick hand wash with warm, soapy water removes any residual grease. For the skewer rod, a brush or non‑abrasive scrubber prevents buildup. Dry all components thoroughly before storing to avoid rust.

Regular maintenance keeps the pan rotating smoothly and ensures that each pork belly you cook is as delicious as the last.

FAQ

Can I use the vertical skewer pan for cuts other than pork belly?

Absolutely. The pan works well with chicken thighs, beef kebabs, and even whole fish. Just adjust cooking times and temperatures to suit the protein.

Do I need to pre‑heat the oven before using the pan?

Yes. Pre‑heating to the initial temperature (425 °F) ensures the skin begins to crisp immediately, which is key for that signature crackle.

Is the vertical gyro pan safe for convection ovens?

It is. The stainless‑steel construction tolerates the fan‑circulated heat. If using convection, you may lower the temperature by 25 °F to prevent over‑browning.
