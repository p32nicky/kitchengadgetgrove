The Ultimate Garlic Butter Shrimp and Corn Skewers

If you want a one-handed appetizer that guests can graze on, skewers are the way to go. For this recipe, cut your corn on the cob into small 1-inch rounds. Alternate a corn round with a jumbo peeled and deveined shrimp.

Whisk together melted butter, minced garlic, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a pinch of smoked paprika. Brush this generously over the skewers before they hit the grill. Grill over medium-high heat for about 3-4 minutes per side. The shrimp will turn opaque and the corn will get those beautiful charred edges that provide a smoky contrast to the garlic butter.

Grilled Corn and Seafood Recipes for a Summer BBQ: The Low Country Foil Pack

For those who prefer a more hearty main course, foil packs are a game-changer. They trap steam, ensuring your seafood stays juicy while the corn softens perfectly. In a large square of heavy-duty aluminum foil, place a piece of corn (broken in half), a handful of shrimp, a few scallops, and some sliced Andouille sausage.

Add a tablespoon of Old Bay seasoning and a pat of butter on top of each pack. Seal the foil tightly and grill for 12-15 minutes. The result is a self-contained seafood boil that requires zero cleanup. When you serve these, the corn can be a bit slippery due to the butter and steam, which is where the Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders come in handy—just slide them in and let your guests enjoy the meal without the mess.

Fresh Corn and Seared Scallop Salad

Not everything has to be served piping hot. A grilled corn and seafood salad is a refreshing way to use your BBQ. Grill whole ears of corn until charred, then slice the kernels off the cob. Combine the corn with seared sea scallops, diced avocado, cherry tomatoes, and thinly sliced red onion.

Toss everything in a lime-cilantro vinaigrette. The warmth of the corn slightly softens the avocado, while the scallops provide a buttery richness. This dish works beautifully as a side or a light main course on those humid July afternoons when a heavy meal feels like too much.

Pro Tips for Perfect Grilling

To get the most out of your grilled corn and seafood recipes for a summer BBQ, keep these practical tips in mind:

  • Dry your seafood: Pat shrimp and scallops dry with a paper towel before seasoning. Excess moisture leads to steaming rather than searing, meaning you miss out on those golden-brown grill marks.
  • The Corn Soak: If using wooden skewers for your corn and shrimp, soak them in water for 30 minutes first to prevent them from catching fire.
  • Temperature Control: Seafood cooks much faster than corn. If you aren't using skewers or foil packs, start your corn first and add the seafood during the last few minutes of grilling.
  • Storage Hacks: Use the Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders to keep your tool drawer tidy. Because they interlock, they don't roll around or get lost in the kitchen clutter.

FAQ

How do I prevent corn from sticking to the grill?

Lightly brush the corn with oil or melted butter before placing it on the grates. Ensure your grill is preheated to medium-high; a hot surface helps the corn sear and release more easily than a cold one.

What is the best way to tell when grilled shrimp are done?

Shrimp are cooked when they turn from translucent to a pearly white/pink color and curl into a "C" shape. Be careful not to overcook them into a tight "O" shape, as this usually means they have become rubbery.
