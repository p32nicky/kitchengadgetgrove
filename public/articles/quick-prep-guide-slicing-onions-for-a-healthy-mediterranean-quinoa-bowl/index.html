Step-by-Step: Slicing Onions for Your Bowl

  1. Trim & Prepare: Cut off the onion root end first—this helps retain moisture during slicing. Slice the onion in half from root to stem.
  2. Secure the Tool: Place the Onion Holder flat on the counter. Insert the stainless steel prongs into the onion halves (prongs face upward). The weighted base anchors everything.
  3. Angle the Knife: Follow the tool’s natural slicing angle (usually 45 degrees) to glide your knife smoothly through the onion. Pull the knife toward you for even slices.
  4. Adjust Thickness: For Mediterranean bowls, try 1/4" slices for raw toppings or 1/8" for caramelizing. Rotate the onion as needed for consistency.

Pair With Other Mediterranean Quinoa Bowl Ingredients

Once your onions are sliced, combine them with:

  • Cooked quinoa (add lemon zest and olive oil for extra flavor)
  • Diced tomatoes and Kalamata olives
  • Chopped fresh parsley or basil
  • Grilled chickpeas or feta cheese
