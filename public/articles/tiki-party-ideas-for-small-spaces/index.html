Create a Compact Tiki Bar

Aim for a lean menu with tropical classics that use overlapping ingredients. A single pitcher of rum can make mai tais, piña coladas, and tiki mules with swapped mixers. Keep your bar small but efficient:

  • Pre-mix simple syrups (like pineapple or ginger) in mason jars
  • Use a magnetic knife strip for bottles and shakers
  • Opt for stackable, reusable glassware

The 100-Pack Umbrella Straws fit seamlessly into each drink, adding a pop of color without requiring extra prep. They’re disposable, so you clean up in seconds.

Space-Saving Decor Hacks

Stick to lightweight, foldable decor that you can stash easily. Consider:

  • Battery-operated string lights draped over a coffee table
  • Mini tiki torches (use LED candles for safety)
  • Wall decals of palm trees or hibiscus flowers

For tables, layer printed tablecloths over existing surfaces. Use the 100-Pack Umbrella Straws in clear glasses to create an instant tiki vibe—no need for permanent centerpieces.

Compact Seating and Layout Tips

Arrange furniture in tight, intimate clusters instead of long rows. Use floor cushions or low stools to save space. Place the bar near an exit to avoid bottlenecks. For a dance floor, clear just enough room for a few moves—guests will love the cozy atmosphere best.
