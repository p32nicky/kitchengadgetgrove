Getting Started: Feeding Your Starter with a Wooden Spurtle

First‑time starter owners often over‑mix, creating a dense, uneven culture. Here’s a streamlined routine that maximizes the wooden spurtle’s strengths:

  1. Measure and discard: Weigh 50 g of starter and discard the rest. This keeps the starter manageable and reduces flour waste.
  2. Add fresh flour and water: Combine 50 g of all‑purpose flour with 50 g of lukewarm water in the same jar.
  3. Stir with the spurtle: Use the skinny wooden spurtle spatula to fold the mixture from the bottom up, ensuring no dry pockets remain. The flat edge scrapes the jar sides while the tapered tip reaches the bottom.
  4. Cover and rest: Loosely place a cloth over the jar, let it sit at room temperature for 12‑24 hours, then repeat.

Because the wooden spurtle is non‑absorbent, you won’t notice any lingering flour taste after several feed cycles. This means a cleaner starter flavor and less risk of off‑notes that can arise from plastic leaching.

Cleaning Your Wooden Spurtle Without Breaking the Bank

One fear of wooden kitchen tools is that they require expensive upkeep. In reality, maintaining a wooden spurtle is simple:

  1. Rinse immediately after use to prevent dough from drying onto the wood.
  2. Wash with mild dish soap and a soft sponge; avoid abrasive scrubbers that could gouge the surface.
  3. Dry thoroughly on a towel; moisture is the enemy of wood longevity.
  4. Once a month, rub a light coat of food‑grade mineral oil into the wood. This seals the pores and keeps the spurtle from absorbing odors.

These steps cost virtually nothing and extend the life of your spurtle far beyond that of cheap plastic tools, which often need replacement after a few uses.

Budget‑Friendly Tips for the Whole Sourdough Journey

Beyond the tool itself, a few frugal habits will keep your sourdough adventures affordable:

  • Adjust hydration: A slightly drier starter uses less water, which can be a cost saver in dry climates.
  • Plan your baking schedule: Align starter feeds with your baking days to avoid wasteful discarding.

FAQ

Can I use a wooden spurtle with a metal or plastic starter jar?

Yes. The wooden spurtle works on any non‑porous surface, but its non‑scratch property shines on glass. It won’t damage metal or plastic, though you may not need the scraping benefit as much.

How often should I oil my wooden spurtle?

Apply a thin layer of food‑grade mineral oil once a month, or whenever the wood looks dry. This keeps the wood from absorbing moisture and prolongs its life.
