Mixing Vegan Sourdough Recipes with a Wooden Spurtle

Many bakers wonder why they should switch from a silicone spatula to a wooden spurtle. The reason is simple: surface tension and residue. Silicone often "pushes" the dough around, while the carved wood of a spurtle "grabs" and incorporates it.

When you are mixing additions into your vegan sourdough—like roasted garlic, rosemary, or olives—the spurtle is the perfect tool for folding. It allows you to distribute the inclusions evenly without deflating the precious air bubbles you've spent hours cultivating. Plus, because it is non-scratch, you can use it in glass jars or ceramic bowls without worrying about scuffing your equipment.

Tips for Better Dough Incorporation

To get the most out of your tools and your recipes, follow these practical mixing tips:

  • The Circular Motion: When feeding your starter, use a tight circular motion with the skinny spurtle to create a vortex, pulling flour from the edges into the center.
  • Jar Scraping: Use the flat edge of the spurtle to scrape down the sides of your jar. This prevents dried starter from building up, which can attract unwanted mold.
  • Temperature Control: Use lukewarm water (around 80°F). If the water is too hot, you'll kill the yeast; too cold, and the fermentation will take days.
  • The Windowpane Test: After mixing and folding, stretch a small piece of dough. If it thins out without tearing, your gluten is well-developed.

Cleaning and Maintaining Your Wooden Tools

Wooden tools are a staple in any vegan kitchen because they are sustainable and durable. However, they require a bit of care to last. Never put your Wooden Spurtle & Sourdough Starter Stirrer Set in the dishwasher, as the high heat and harsh detergents can crack the wood.

Instead, rinse them with warm soapy water immediately after use. Because sourdough starter is quite sticky, a quick scrub with a brush works wonders. Once a month, rub a small amount of food-grade mineral oil or beeswax into the wood to keep it seasoned and water-resistant.

FAQ

Can I use a spurtle for other vegan recipes?

Absolutely. While they are fantastic for sourdough, spurtles are excellent for stirring risottos, mixing vegan batters, or stirring thick stews and polentas without the bulk of a traditional spoon.

Why is my vegan sourdough not rising?

This is usually due to an inactive starter or a cold kitchen. Ensure your starter is doubling in size within 4-6 hours of feeding before using it in a recipe. If your house is chilly, try placing your dough in an oven with the light turned on for a warm environment.

Does the type of flour matter for vegan sourdough?

Yes. While all-purpose flour works, bread flour has a higher protein content, which creates a stronger gluten network. This results in a better "oven spring" and a more airy, open crumb structure.
