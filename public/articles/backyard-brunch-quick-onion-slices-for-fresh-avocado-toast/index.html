The Secret to Uniform Onion Slices

If you've ever wondered why restaurant avocado toast looks so precise, it's all about the thickness of the garnish. When onion slices are too thick, they overpower the avocado. When they are sliced thin and uniform, they provide a delicate crunch and a beautiful pop of purple color.

To achieve this consistency without spending twenty minutes on one onion, I use the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS). Step-by-Step: Prepping Your Backyard Brunch Onion Slices

Getting your toppings ready ahead of time is the key to actually enjoying your guests. Here is how I prep my onions for a crowd:

  • Choose your onion: I prefer red onions for the color, but shallots work great for a milder flavor.
  • Secure the vegetable: Insert the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide into the center of the onion. This eliminates the "wobble" that usually happens halfway through slicing.
  • Slice thin: Using a sharp chef's knife, slice across the onion. Because the prongs keep the thickness consistent, you don't have to guess where to place your knife.
  • Quick Soak: If you find raw onions too pungent, soak your slices in ice water for 10 minutes. This keeps them crisp but removes the harsh "burn."
  • Dry and Top: Pat the slices dry with a paper towel before layering them over your mashed avocado.

Pro Tips for the Ultimate Avocado Toast

To make the most of your prep work, focus on the layers. Start with a thick slice of sourdough, toasted in butter or olive oil until golden brown. Mash your avocado with a squeeze of lime, a pinch of sea salt, and a dash of red pepper flakes.

Now, add those precision-cut onion slices. For an extra touch of elegance, arrange the onions in a concentric circle or a neat row. Top the whole thing with a poached egg or a sprinkle of feta cheese. Because your onions are uniformly sliced, the toast remains stable and easy to eat, rather than having large chunks of onion slide off with every bite.
