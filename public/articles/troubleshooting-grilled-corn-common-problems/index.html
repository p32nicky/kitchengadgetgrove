Problem: Burnt Outside, Raw Inside

This is the most common frustration. It happens when your grill is screaming hot, but the heat isn't penetrating the core of the cob. The sugars in the corn caramelize (and then burn) long before the interior reaches the proper temperature.

To fix this, move your corn to the indirect heat zone of your grill. If you’re using a gas grill, turn off one burner and move the corn to the cool side. If you're using charcoal, push the coals to one side. Close the lid to create an oven-like environment that cooks the corn evenly from the inside out.

Troubleshooting Grilled Corn Common Problems: The Char Factor

Some people want a light gold color, while others want deep, smoky char marks. If your corn looks pale and boiled rather than grilled, you aren't getting enough direct contact with the heat. This often happens if you leave the husks on for the entire cooking process.

For that professional steakhouse look, try the "hybrid method." Steam the corn in the husks for 10 minutes to ensure the inside is tender, then peel back the husks and place the naked kernels directly over the flames for 2-3 minutes. This gives you the sweetness of steaming and the flavor of grilling.

Problem: Corn Sticking to the Grates

If your kernels are tearing off and falling into the coals, your grates aren't prepped. Corn has a high sugar content, which acts like glue when it hits hot metal. To prevent this, ensure your grill is fully preheated and brushed clean.

Lightly brush the corn with a neutral oil or melted butter before placing it on the grill. This creates a thin barrier that allows the corn to slide and rotate easily, ensuring an even char without the loss of half your kernels.

FAQ

Should I grill corn with or without the husk?

It depends on the result you want. Grilling with the husk on steams the corn, keeping it juicy and sweet but providing no char. Grilling without the husk (naked) provides the best flavor and a beautiful charred appearance. For the best of both worlds, steam in the husk first, then finish on the grates.

How long does grilled corn typically take to cook?

Naked corn usually takes 8-12 minutes, rotating every few minutes. Corn in the husk can take 15-20 minutes depending on the heat of your grill. The corn is done when the kernels are tender and the color has shifted from pale yellow to a deeper gold.

How do I keep corn warm while other food finishes?

Move the corn to the upper warming rack of your grill or wrap the cobs loosely in aluminum foil and place them in a low-temperature oven (about 200°F). This keeps them hot without overcooking the kernels.
