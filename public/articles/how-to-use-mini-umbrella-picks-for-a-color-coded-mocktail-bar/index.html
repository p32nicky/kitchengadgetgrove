How to Set Up a Color-Coded Mocktail Bar

  1. Choose your color scheme: Pick 3-5 colors that match your event theme or the base ingredients of your drinks.
  2. Prepare a selection board: Place small paper umbrellas next to each drink station as visual guides for guests.
  3. Label ingredients creatively: Use mini umbrellas to mark sections of a build-your-own mocktail station (e.g., red for berries, blue for tropical fruits).

The 150-pack includes 50 of each style, so you’ll have plenty for both labeling and garnishing drinks.

Styling Tips for a Tropical Vibe

Mini umbrella picks aren’t just functional—they’re decor. For a cohesive look, pair them with other tiki-themed elements like bamboo cups, pineapples as garnishes, or tropical music. Use all three styles from the 150-pack to add visual texture: the striped, solid, and polka-dot designs work well together or can be mixed to differentiate drink categories (e.g., striped for signature mocktails, solid for classics).

Practical Uses Beyond Mocktails

These cocktail picks are surprisingly versatile. Use them to:

  • Garnish fruit platters with a parasol for each bowl (think watermelon vs. pineapple).
  • Mark appetizer sections at a buffet, like a pink umbrella for shrimp skewers and yellow for citrus grilled chicken.
  • Add flair to iced teas or lemonades at backyard games or BBQs.

The thin paper sticks work best with sturdy drinks that won’t topple them—think mason jars or highball glasses.
