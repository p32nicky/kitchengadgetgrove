Tips for a Successful First Loaf

Even with top‑tier tools, beginners can stumble on a few common pitfalls. Here’s a quick cheat sheet you can slip into the gift box:

  1. Feed the starter regularly. A healthy starter needs equal parts flour and water every 12‑24 hours. Warm rooms (around 75°F) speed up activity.
  2. Don’t over‑mix. Once the dough reaches a shaggy consistency, stop mixing and move to the stretch‑and‑fold stage. Over‑mixing can lead to a tough crumb.
  3. Use the lame confidently. A hesitant slash can cause the loaf to burst unevenly. Practice on a piece of dough first – the knife should glide, not tear.
  4. Steam the oven. Place a tray of hot water on the bottom rack or spray the oven walls with water just before loading the loaf. Steam gives the crust that signature crackle.
