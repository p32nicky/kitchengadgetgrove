Onion Infused Tequila Recipe: Steps to Success

Follow this simple process to create a savory tequila infusion that pairs perfectly with tacos, grilled meats, or bold cocktails:

  1. Prepare the onion: Use the slicing guide to thinly slice one large onion. The holder’s prongs grip the onion firmly, making it easy to slice without slippage.
  2. Infuse the tequila: Place the sliced onion in a 750ml glass jar, pour in 1 bottle of high-quality blanco or reposado tequila, and seal tightly.
  3. Steep and strain: Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 24–48 hours. Strain through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth into a clean bottle.
  4. Optional enhancements: For extra depth, add a few crushed garlic cloves or a pinch of smoked paprika to the jar during infusion.

Store and Enjoy Your Infusion

Proper storage preserves the flavor of your onion-infused tequila. Keep it in a dark, cool place like a cabinet or pantry. The infusion will last 2–3 months, though it’s best consumed within a month for peak freshness. For stronger onion flavor, steep for up to 72 hours, but monitor the infusion closely to avoid overpowering the tequila’s natural character.
