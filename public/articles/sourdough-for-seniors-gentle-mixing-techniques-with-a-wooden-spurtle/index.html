Preparing Your Starter: Gentle Mixing Steps

Follow these five easy moves each morning or evening, depending on your feeding schedule:

  1. Measure and add flour. Use a kitchen scale for accuracy; a 100 g flour addition keeps the starter’s hydration stable.
  2. Pour water. Warm (not hot) water helps dissolve the flour and reduces clumping.
  3. Stir with the wooden spurtle. Hold the spurtle by its narrow end, lift gently, and rotate clockwise for 30–45 seconds. The wood’s natural give prevents over‑mixing.
  4. Scrape the sides. The spurtle’s flat edge reaches every corner, ensuring no dry flour remains stuck to the jar.
  5. Cover and rest. Use a breathable cloth or loose lid; the starter needs airflow but not contamination.

Because the spurtle is skinny, it fits comfortably in standard 16‑oz mason jars, allowing you to see the starter’s activity without removing the cover.

Caring for Your Wooden Spurtle

Wood needs a little TLC to stay food‑safe and long‑lasting. After each use, rinse the spurtle under warm water—no soap is necessary, as it can soak into the wood. Pat dry with a clean towel, then rub a thin layer of food‑grade mineral oil once a month. This routine prevents cracking and keeps the surface smooth for effortless jar scraping.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Even experienced bakers can trip up when they first switch to a wooden tool. Here are three pitfalls seniors often encounter, plus quick fixes:

  • Using too much force. The wood’s softness means you don’t need to press hard. A light, steady motion is all that’s required.
  • Leaving the spurtle in the jar overnight. Moisture can cause the wood to swell. Store the spurtle upright on a drying rack instead.
  • Neglecting the oil treatment. Dry wood can develop splinters, which may harbor bacteria. A monthly oil rub maintains a sealed surface.

FAQ

Can I use the wooden spurtle for wet doughs?

Yes. The spurtle’s smooth surface handles both stiff starters and wetter doughs (up to 80 % hydration) without bending or breaking.

Is the wood safe for people with allergies?

Food‑grade hardwoods are hypoallergenic and free of latex or nickel. As long as the spurtle is kept clean and dry, it poses no allergy risk.

How often should I replace the spurtle?

With proper care—regular oiling and gentle use—a high‑quality wooden spurtle can last several years. Replace it only if you notice deep cracks or a persistent odor that cleaning cannot remove.
