Why Uniform Onion Slices Matter in Salsa Verde

Salsa verde relies on a harmony of tangy tomatillos, zesty chili, and sharp onion. Irregular onion pieces can lead to uneven wilting or overpowering bites. Thin, even slices soften quickly during cooking, blending seamlessly into the sauce. Thick chunks, meanwhile, risk a jarring texture. The key? How to Slice Onions for Salsa Verde (The Right Way)

1. **Trim and prep**: Cut off the root end of the onion (avoid the top to prevent tear-inducing sulfur release). Peel and halve the onion.
2. **Secure the onion**: Insert one half into the stainless steel prongs of the Onion Holder. The prongs stabilize the onion, keeping it from rolling.
3. **Slice safely**: Position your knife against the guard and slice downward. The holder ensures even pressure and uniform cuts, no hand cramping or slipping required.
4. **Adjust slice thickness**: Rotate the Onion Holder to switch between slicing, dicing, or julienning based on your salsa’s texture preference.

Tips for Salsa Verde Success

Once your onions are sliced, combine them with tomatillos, jalapeños, garlic, and a splash of lime. For a quick, flavorful sauce:

  1. Roast tomatillos and chilies until blistered.
  2. Add sliced onions, garlic, and a pinch of salt to a blender.
  3. Blend with fresh lime juice until smooth. Cook briefly to mellow the onions.
