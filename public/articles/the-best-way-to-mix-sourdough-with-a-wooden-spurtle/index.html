Mastering Mixing Sourdough Wooden Spurtle Techniques

Here’s how to get the most out of your wooden spurtle:

  1. Scrape and feed: Use the tapered end of the spurtle to remove old starter from the jar before adding fresh flour and water. This prevents residual sourdough from skewing your feedings.
  2. Fold, don’t stir: Instead of whipping your starter, use the spurtle to slowly fold ingredients. Push the tool to the bottom of the jar and lift up in a circular motion to incorporate air gently.
  3. Scrape thoroughly: After mixing, tilt the jar and drag the spurtle along the sides and corners to gather nooks of flour and starter.

Tips for New Sourdough Bakers Using a Spurtle

  • Start with a dry jar to avoid slipping. If your starter is super sticky, dust the spurtle lightly with flour before scraping.
  • Pair the spurtle with a clear jar to monitor hydration levels during feedings.
  • Clean the tools in warm, soapy water immediately after use. Dry thoroughly to prevent mold.

FAQ

Can I use any wooden spoon as a spurtle?

Close, but not quite. A true spurtle has a skinny, flat shape for deep jar scraping. A regular spoon’s round handle and broader blade won’t reach corners as effectively.

Will the wooden spurtle hold up to frequent use?

If you care for it properly (avoiding dishwashers and drying it completely after each use), the set’s untreated wood will last for years, even with daily sourdough feedings.

How does a spurtle improve my sourdough game?

By preventing waste (you’ll scrape every crumb of starter) and allowing precise mixing, a spurtle helps you maintain a consistent starter, which is key to sourdough success.
