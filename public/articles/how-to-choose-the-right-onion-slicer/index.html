How to Maintain Your Onion Slicer

Proper care extends the life of any kitchen tool. For stainless steel slicers:

  1. Rinse immediately after use to prevent food buildup.
  2. Use a soft sponge; avoid abrasive pads that can scratch the blade.
  3. If the slicer is dishwasher‑safe, place it on the top rack to avoid high heat exposure.
  4. Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots.
  5. Periodically sharpen the blade with a honing stone if you notice a decrease in cutting efficiency.
