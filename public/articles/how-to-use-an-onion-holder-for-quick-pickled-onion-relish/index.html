Ingredients & Equipment

  • 1 large red or white onion, peeled
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar (or apple cider vinegar for a milder note)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • Optional aromatics: 1 tsp peppercorns, 1 bay leaf, a pinch of red‑pepper flakes
  • Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS)
  • Sharp kitchen knife or the same slicer tool
  • Glass jar with a lid (at least 8‑oz capacity)

Step‑by‑Step: Preparing the Onion for Quick Pickled Onion Relish

1. Secure the onion. Place the peeled onion on a cutting board. Slip the prongs of the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide around the onion’s middle, making sure the stems face upward. The holder’s stainless steel grips will keep the onion from rolling.

2. Slice uniformly. Using a sharp chef’s knife, slice the onion into thin half‑rings, about 1/8‑inch thick. Because the holder stabilizes the onion, you can focus on a smooth, even motion instead of worrying about slipping.

3. Separate the rings. Gently push the sliced rings off the holder with the back of your knife or a spoon. The rings will fall into a bowl, ready for pickling.

4. Make the brine. In a small saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium‑low until the sugar and salt dissolve—no need to bring it to a boil.

5. Combine. Place the onion rings into the glass jar. Add any optional aromatics you like. Pour the warm brine over the onions, ensuring they are fully submerged.

6. Seal and chill. Close the jar tightly and let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate. The onions are ready to use after about 30 minutes, but they improve with a few hours of rest.

Tips for Perfect Quick Pickled Onion Relish

• Uniform thickness matters. The more even the slices, the more consistent the acidity level throughout the relish. That’s why the onion holder is a game‑changer for quick pickles.

• Don’t over‑salt. The brine already contains salt; tasting the mixture before sealing helps you avoid a too‑salty result.

• Adjust the heat. If you prefer a milder bite, increase the water ratio (e.g., 1/3 cup water to 1/2 cup vinegar). For a sharper punch, add a splash more vinegar.

• Reuse the holder. After you’re done slicing, the stainless‑steel prongs can double as a meat tenderizer. Just press the holder over a piece of chicken or pork to break down fibers before cooking.

Storing and Using Your Quick Pickled Onion Relish

The relish will keep in the refrigerator for up to two weeks. Use it as a topping for grilled fish, a garnish on avocado toast, or a tangy addition to a classic cheeseburger. Because the slices stay crisp, they add texture even after several days.

Cleaning the Onion Holder

Rinse the stainless‑steel prongs under hot water immediately after use. A quick scrub with a non‑abrasive sponge removes any residual onion fibers. The tool is dishwasher‑safe, but hand‑washing preserves the finish longer.

FAQ

Can I use the Onion Holder for vegetables other than onions?

Yes. The prongs are designed to grip a range of produce—tomatoes, potatoes, even small cucumbers—so you can slice them uniformly for salads, salsas, or stir‑fries.

Do I need to blanch the onions before pickling?

No. The quick pickling method works directly on raw onion rings. Blanching would soften the texture and dilute the crisp bite that makes the relish stand out.

Is the stainless‑steel material safe for acidic brines?

Absolutely. Stainless steel is non‑reactive, so the vinegar in your brine won’t corrode the tool or affect the flavor of your quick pickled onion relish.
