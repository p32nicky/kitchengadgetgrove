Quick Grilled Corn with Chili‑Lime Butter

Grilled corn is the star of many snack tables, and this version takes under 15 minutes from start to finish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn, husks removed
  • 2 tbsp softened butter
  • 1 tsp lime zest
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • Pinch of sea salt

Method:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium‑high heat (about 400°F).
  2. Brush each ear of corn lightly with oil; this prevents sticking.
  3. Place the corn directly on the grates. Turn every 2‑3 minutes until all sides are lightly charred, about 8‑10 minutes.
  4. While the corn cooks, whisk butter, lime zest, chili powder, and salt together.
  5. When the corn is done, use the Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders to secure each ear. Slip the butter mixture over the hot kernels; the heat melts the butter instantly, coating the corn in a tangy, spicy glaze.

Serve the corn still warm, with extra lime wedges on the side for an extra burst of acidity.

Grilled Veggie Skewers: Zucchini, Bell Peppers, and Red Onion

Skewers are the ideal way to keep small pieces together while you grill, and they’re easy to eat with your hands.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into ½‑inch rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1‑inch squares
  • ½ red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Thread the vegetables onto metal or bamboo skewers, alternating colors for visual appeal.
  2. Brush the assembled skewers with olive oil, then sprinkle with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill over medium heat, turning every 2 minutes, until the veggies are tender and lightly charred, roughly 6‑8 minutes.
  4. Remove from the grill and drizzle with a splash of lemon juice for brightness.

These skewers pair beautifully with the buttery corn for a balanced snack plate.

One‑Pan Grilled Corn & Veggie Medley

If you’d rather keep things simple, toss corn kernels off the cob and sliced veggies together on a foil packet. The result is a smoky side dish that can double as a snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of corn, kernels cut off (use the Girla Corn Holders to keep hands safe while you cut)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup sliced baby carrots
  • 1 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Preheat grill to medium heat.
  2. Spread the corn kernels, tomatoes, and carrots on a large sheet of heavy‑duty foil.
  3. Drizzle with melted butter, sprinkle thyme, salt, and pepper, then fold the foil into a sealed packet.
  4. Place the packet on the grill and cook for 8‑10 minutes, shaking once halfway through.
  5. Open carefully; the veggies will be glossy and slightly caramelized. Serve straight from the foil for a rustic feel.

This method is perfect when you’re feeding a crowd and want minimal cleanup.

Pro Tips for Perfect Grilled Snacks

Even the simplest recipes can be elevated with a few tricks:

  • Pre‑soak wooden skewers. Soaking for 15 minutes prevents them from burning.
  • Use a light brush of oil. A thin coating stops sticking and helps those beautiful grill marks appear.
  • Don’t overcrowd the grill. Give each piece enough space to breathe; otherwise you’ll steam rather than sear.
  • Rest for a minute. After removing corn or veg from the heat, let it sit for 30 seconds—this allows the juices to redistribute.
  • Leverage the Girla Interlocking Corn Holders. The silicone grips stay cool, so you can handle hot corn without a mitt, and the interlocking design keeps the holders together for tidy storage.

Apply these tips and you’ll consistently produce snack‑ready, restaurant‑quality results.

Yes. The silicone handles are heat‑resistant enough to grab roasted peppers, grilled asparagus, or even a hot corn muffin without burning your fingers.

Do I need to soak the corn before grilling?

Soaking is optional. If you like extra moisture and a slightly sweeter kernel, soak the ears for 15‑20 minutes in cold water. Otherwise, grill them directly for a drier, more caramelized texture.
