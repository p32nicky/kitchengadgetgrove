Safety Tips for Picnic Pranking

While these games are all about laughs, a little bit of caution goes a long way. Because the Extendable Prank Fork is a functional tool with tines, you want to keep the play focused on the food and the table, not the people. Avoid "poking" games and keep the extension movement predictable so you don't accidentally knock over a glass of wine or a bowl of dip.

It is also a good idea to designate a "safe zone" on the picnic blanket. If someone is in the middle of a serious conversation or carefully assembling a complex sandwich, give them a pass. The best pranks are the ones where everyone ends up laughing, including the person whose cherry tomato just migrated six inches to the left.

FAQ

Can the extendable fork be used for actual eating?

Yes, it is a functional stainless steel dinner fork. While it is designed for jokes and pranks, it works perfectly fine as a standard fork when retracted to its 8.7-inch length.

How do I clean a telescopic fork?

Rinse it thoroughly with warm soapy water. To ensure the telescoping sections stay smooth, make sure you extend it fully during cleaning to remove any food particles trapped in the joints, then dry it completely before retracting.

Is the extension stable enough to hold heavy food?

It is perfect for bite-sized snacks, fruits, and vegetables. However, because it is a telescopic tool, it may wobble if you try to lift something very heavy (like a large steak) at full extension. Keep the targets light for the best results.
