Step‑by‑Step: Scraping a Fermentation Jar Cleanly

  1. Warm the Jar: A quick dip in warm water loosens the starter film. Avoid boiling, which can kill beneficial microbes.
  2. Hold the Spurtle Firmly: Grip the handle with a firm, but relaxed, hold. The wooden handle stays cool, so you won’t burn your fingers.
  3. Slide from the Bottom Up: Insert the tip of the spurtle into the jar’s base and slide upward in a gentle, circular motion. The narrow shaft keeps the tip from digging into the glass.
  4. Reach the Sides: Rotate the jar 180° and repeat. The spurtle’s length allows you to scrape the sides without having to lift the jar.
  5. Check for Residue: After the first pass, give the jar a quick shake. If any starter clings to the walls, do a second pass.
  6. Rinse and Dry: Rinse the jar with warm water, pat dry with a clean towel, and it’s ready for the next batch.

Because the spurtle is non‑scratch, you can use it repeatedly without worrying about chipping the glass. Its natural wood finish also means you don’t need to worry about harsh chemicals that could contaminate the starter.

Maintenance Tips to Keep Your Spurtle in Top Shape

Wood is porous, so proper care extends its life:

  • After each use, rinse with warm, soapy water and dry immediately.
  • Avoid soaking the spurtle for long periods; this can cause warping.
  • Occasionally rub a light coat of food‑safe mineral oil to keep the wood hydrated.
  • Store in a dry place, off the edge of the counter, to prevent cracking.
