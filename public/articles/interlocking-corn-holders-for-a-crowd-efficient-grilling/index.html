The Struggle of Traditional Corn Holders

Most of us grew up with the basic stainless steel corn holders. They work, but they have two major flaws: they are slippery and they are disorganized. When you are trying to hand out corn to a crowd, fumbling with ten separate metal spikes is a recipe for a poked finger or a dropped cob.

Furthermore, standard metal holders get incredibly hot. If you leave them on the grill too long or set them on a hot grate, you're dealing with a burn risk. This is where a thoughtful design upgrade becomes necessary for anyone who grills regularly for friends and family.

FAQ

Are interlocking corn holders better than corn skewers?

Yes, for crowds, they are generally superior. Skewers require you to pierce the corn before grilling, which can be time-consuming. Interlocking holders are inserted after grilling, allowing you to cook the corn however you prefer (husk-on, foil-wrapped, or naked) and then provide a handle for the guest.

Can I use these holders on the grill while cooking?

While the stainless steel prongs can handle heat, it is best to insert them after the corn is cooked. This keeps the silicone handles pristine and ensures the holders remain cool and comfortable for your guests to grip.

How do I stop the corn from spinning on the holders?

The key is to insert the prongs diagonally. Instead of pushing them straight in, angle them slightly. This creates a more secure anchor in the cob, ensuring the corn stays put while your guests enjoy their meal.
