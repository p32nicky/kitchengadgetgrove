The Classic Garlic Butter Char

You can't go wrong with the basics. The key here is the "compound butter." Instead of just slapping a piece of butter on the corn after it's cooked, mix softened salted butter with minced garlic, a pinch of smoked paprika, and fresh chopped parsley.

Pull back the husks but leave them attached to the base to create a natural "boat." Brush the kernels generously with the garlic butter and grill over medium-high heat for about 10-12 minutes. The husks protect the corn from burning while the butter infuses into the kernels. Once plated, slide in the Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders to ensure your dad can enjoy his meal without getting garlic butter all over his favorite polo shirt.

Mexican Street Corn (Elote) Style

If you want to impress the crowd, Elote is the way to go. This is one of the most popular grilled corn recipes for Father's Day because it hits every taste bud: salty, sweet, creamy, and tangy.

Grill your corn naked (husks removed) until you see some charred black spots. While the corn is still hot, brush it with a thin layer of mayonnaise or Mexican crema. Roll the cob in crumbled Cotija cheese, then sprinkle with chili powder and a squeeze of fresh lime juice. Because Elote is so saucy, using the Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders Set is a must—it keeps your fingers completely clear of the creamy coating.

Sweet & Spicy Honey-Sriracha Corn

For the dad who loves a little kick, a honey-sriracha glaze is a game-changer. This recipe creates a sticky, caramelized exterior that pairs perfectly with grilled pork or chicken.

Whisk together equal parts honey and Sriracha with a splash of soy sauce. Brush this glaze on the corn during the last 5 minutes of grilling. Be careful here; the sugar in the honey can burn quickly, so keep a close eye on the heat. The result is a vibrant, reddish-gold cob that tastes like a gourmet treat.

Pro Tips for Perfect Grilling

To ensure your corn comes out juicy and charred rather than dried out, follow these simple steps:

  • Don't overcook: Corn cooks fast. Once the kernels are tender and have those signature grill marks, pull them off. Overcooking makes the kernels chewy.
  • Pre-soak the husks: If grilling in the husk, soak the ears in cold water for 15 minutes first. This prevents the husks from catching fire and steams the corn from the inside.
  • Use a grill basket: If you're worried about cobs falling through the grates, a stainless steel basket is a lifesaver.
  • Clean up efficiently: Choose tools that save time. The Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders are dishwasher safe, meaning you spend less time scrubbing and more time relaxing with your family.

FAQ

Should I grill corn with or without the husk?

It depends on the result you want. Grilling with the husk steams the corn, resulting in a tender, juicy kernel. Grilling without the husk allows the fire to touch the kernels directly, creating a smoky, charred flavor and allowing glazes to caramelize.

How do I stop corn from sticking to the grill?

Lightly oil your grill grates before adding the corn, or brush the corn itself with a high-smoke-point oil (like avocado or canola oil) before placing it on the heat.

Can I make these recipes in an air fryer?

Yes! While you lose the smoky char, you can achieve a similar result. Rub the corn with your chosen seasoning, air fry at 400°F for about 10-12 minutes, turning halfway through. You can still use corn holders to serve them easily!
