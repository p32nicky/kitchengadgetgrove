Simple Grilled Corn Recipes for Small Groups

When cooking for 4 to 6 people, you have the luxury of experimenting with flavors. Here are three ways to serve corn that always get a great reaction:

  • Classic Garlic Butter: Brush the corn with melted salted butter, minced garlic, and a pinch of smoked paprika before grilling. Finish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.
  • Mexican Street Corn Style (Elote): Grill the corn until charred. Slather it in a mixture of mayonnaise, sour cream, and lime juice. Roll it in Cotija cheese and dust it with chili powder.
  • Honey-Lime Glaze: Whisk together honey, lime zest, and a dash of soy sauce. Brush this on during the last three minutes of grilling for a sweet and savory caramelization.

The Secret to Perfect Char

The biggest mistake people make with corn is inconsistency. You end up with some kernels that are undercooked and others that are burnt. For small batches, I recommend the "direct heat" method. Place the corn perpendicular to the grill grates and rotate every 2-3 minutes.

If you prefer a juicier kernel, you can soak the corn (still in the husks) in water for 15 minutes before grilling. The husks steam the corn while the outside chars. However, if you're using the flavor-forward recipes mentioned above, grilling shucked corn is the way to go so the seasonings can actually hit the kernels.

FAQ

How long should I grill corn on the cob?

For shucked corn, 8 to 12 minutes over medium-high heat is usually sufficient. You are looking for golden-brown kernels with a few dark charred spots for maximum flavor.

Can I use these corn holders for other vegetables?

While designed for corn, the Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders can work for thicker grilled vegetables like large pieces of zucchini or thick-cut eggplant, though they are optimized for the diameter of a corn cob.

How do I stop corn from sticking to the grill?

The best way to prevent sticking is to lightly coat the corn in oil or butter before placing it on the grates. Ensure your grill is fully preheated before adding the corn to create a proper sear.
