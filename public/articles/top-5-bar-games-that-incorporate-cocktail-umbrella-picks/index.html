1. Umbrella Flip Challenge

The Umbrella Flip Challenge is a quick, skill‑based game that works perfectly on a sturdy bar top or a large serving tray. Each player gets a single cocktail pick, places the paper parasol side up, and attempts to flip it onto a target cup using only one finger. The first person to land three umbrellas on three different glasses wins.

2. Guess the Garnish Relay

This relay combines memory, speed, and a dash of creativity. Split your group into two teams. One player from each team grabs a handful of the mini paper parasol picks and hides them behind their back. The opposing team must guess which style (tiki, luau, or tropical) is being held, then race to garnish a drink with the correct pick.When the guess is correct, the player runs back and tags the next teammate. The first team to correctly garnish five drinks wins. The game encourages participants to think about flavor pairings while the colorful umbrellas keep the visual excitement high.

3. Umbrella Trivia Toss

Combine a quick trivia round with a light‑hearted tossing game. Write a series of bar‑related questions on index cards—think cocktail history, spirit facts, or famous movie bar scenes. Place a bucket or large glass at the end of the bar.

Players answer a question; if they’re correct, they get to toss a cocktail pick toward the bucket. Each pick that lands inside scores a point. The 150‑pack ensures you never run out of colorful toss‑pieces, and the three styles let you assign extra points for landing a specific style in a designated spot.

4. Mini Parasol Pyramid

Building a pyramid isn’t just for kids’ blocks; it can be a surprisingly competitive bar game. Using the 150 PCS Drink Umbrellas Cocktail Picks, each player builds a small pyramid on a coaster. The catch? They must do it one pick at a time while their drink is being mixed.

Once a player finishes a three‑level pyramid (six picks), the next round begins. The player with the tallest, most stable pyramid after ten minutes wins. This game encourages steady hands—perfect for those who love a good cocktail‑shaking challenge.

5. Cocktail Umbrella Scavenger Hunt

For larger gatherings, turn the entire venue into a game board. Hide the cocktail picks in various spots—under napkins, inside fruit bowls, or tucked behind bottle caps. Provide guests with a checklist that lists each umbrella style and a short clue.

When a player finds a pick, they must return to the bar and attach it to a drink of their choice before the next clue is revealed. The first team to collect all three styles and garnish a matching drink wins a prize. The game keeps guests moving, chatting, and interacting with the décor, while the bright parasols add a festive flair to every discovered drink.
