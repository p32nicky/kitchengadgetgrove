Step‑by‑Step: Classic Grilled Corn on the Cob

1. Soak the corn. Even if the kernels are fresh, soaking in cold water for 30 minutes helps prevent them from drying out on the grill.

2. Preheat the grill. Aim for medium‑high heat (about 375°F). A clean, lightly oiled grates prevent sticking.

3. Brush and season. Toss each ear in olive oil, then sprinkle with salt, pepper, and a pinch of smoked paprika. The oil locks in moisture, while paprika adds that smoky depth.

4. Place on the Girla holders. Slide the corn into the holders. The silicone grips keep the corn steady, and the interlocking design means you can stack multiple pairs without crowding.

5. Grill. Turn every 3–4 minutes. After about 12–15 minutes, the kernels should be tender and slightly charred. The holders make it easy to rotate the corn without fumbling with long tongs.

6. Finish. Remove the corn, slather with melted butter or your favorite flavored oil, and add finishing touches like lime zest or a sprinkle of Parmesan.

Creative Variations to Keep the Crowd Talking

Here are three crowd‑pleasing twists that go beyond the classic recipe:

  1. Mexican Street Corn. After grilling, slather with mayo or a Mexican crema, then dust with cotija cheese, chili powder, and a squeeze of lime. The Girla holders let you flip the corn easily to get an even coating.
  2. Herb & Garlic Butter. Mix softened butter with minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, and parsley. Spread over hot corn for a fragrant, savory bite.
  3. Cheesy Jalapeño. Grilled corn topped with shredded Monterey Jack, diced jalapeños, and a drizzle of chipotle mayo makes a spicy, indulgent side that pairs well with grilled meats.
