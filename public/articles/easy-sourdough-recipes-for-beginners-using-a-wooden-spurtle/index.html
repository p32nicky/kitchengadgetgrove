Two Easy Sourdough Recipes for Beginners Using a Wooden Spurtle

Start simple with these recipes that highlight the spurtle’s utility:

  1. Classic Sourdough Starter: Mix 1/2 cup whole wheat flour and 1/4 cup water in a jar. Use the spurtle to stir until no dry flour remains, then let it rest. The spurtle’s narrow shape ensures every bit of flour mixes in, and the Danish stick scrapes down the jar for a consistent starter.
  2. Simple Sourdough Loaf: Combine 3 cups flour, 1 1/4 cups water, 1/4 cup starter, and 1 tsp salt. Use the spurtle to mix, then let the dough rest. Later, use the Danish stick to gently fold the dough during bulk fermentation. The wooden tools prevent over-handling, which is key for tender bread.
