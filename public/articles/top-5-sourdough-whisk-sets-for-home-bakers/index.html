How to Use Each Piece in the Set

  • Whisk: Mix the starter with flour and water until it’s a thick, loose batter. The flat head distributes the mixture evenly and incorporates air, which helps the starter rise.
  • Scraper: Once the dough begins to form, use the scraper to pull dough from the sides into the center. This action reduces sticking and keeps the dough from drying out.
  • Scoring Lame: After the dough has risen and is ready for baking, use the lame to score the surface before putting it in the oven. Scoring controls the expansion of the loaf and creates a decorative pattern.

How to Care for Your Sourdough Whisk Set

FAQ

Can I use a regular kitchen whisk for sourdough?

While a regular whisk can work, it often lacks the flat, wide head that spreads dough evenly and reduces sticking. A dedicated sourdough whisk provides better aeration and handling for thick doughs.

Is the scraper necessary if I have a good whisk?

The scraper helps pull dough from the bowl’s edges into the center, keeping the mixture uniform and preventing dry spots. It’s especially useful for beginners who may not yet have the technique to fold dough with a whisk alone.

How do I score bread with the lame for the best crust?

Hold the lame at a 30‑degree angle, apply gentle pressure, and make a shallow cut. The depth should be about 1/4 inch to allow the dough to expand without tearing the crust. Practice on a small loaf to get the timing right.
