Three Tips for Better BBQ Prep

Beyond the right tools, a few habits can make your kitchen time much more efficient:

  • Prep in Stages: Slice all your aromatics (onions, peppers, garlic) first. This prevents you from having to wash your cutting board multiple times between different types of produce.
  • Cold Vegetables: If you find your vegetables are too soft to slice cleanly, pop them in the fridge for 20 minutes. Cold produce is firmer and easier to cut uniformly.
  • Sharp Knives: Even with a slicing guide, a dull knife will crush the vegetable rather than slice it. Keep your chef's knife honed for the cleanest cuts.
