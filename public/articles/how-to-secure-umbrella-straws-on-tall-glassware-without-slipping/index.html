  1. Choose the right glass size. The 100PCS set works best with 7‑to‑8‑inch tall glasses. If you’re using a smaller glass, the straw may be too long and the umbrella can float.
  2. Insert the straw fully. Push the straw in until the bottom tip touches the glass floor. The tapered tip of the umbrella straw will press against the rim, creating a gentle suction that holds it in place.
  3. Apply a tiny amount of non‑toxic glue or tape. For a one‑time event, a small dab of clear nail polish on the rim can keep the straw from sliding. If you’re serving repeatedly, use a strip of clear masking tape around the rim; the tape’s adhesive will lock the straw in.
  4. Check the angle. Tilt the glass slightly so the umbrella sits at a 45° angle. This reduces the force of gravity pulling it straight out.
  5. Use a straw clip. If you have a reusable straw clip, slide it over the top of the straw. The clip adds extra friction, especially useful for high‑traffic parties.

Tips for Tall Glassware That Keeps Straws from Slipping

Even with the right straw, tall glassware can still be tricky. Here are a few more tricks:

  • Use a straw with a flared base. The 100PCS set’s base expands slightly, giving it a better grip on the glass rim.
  • Wrap a rubber band. A thin rubber band around the rim and straw can provide additional traction without leaving residue.
  • Serve at a lower temperature. Hot drinks can cause the straw to expand and loosen; keep beverages cold to maintain a snug fit.
  • Pre‑test your setup. Before the party, run a quick test with a single straw and umbrella on each glass to confirm stability.
