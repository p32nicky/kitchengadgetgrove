Typical DIY approaches include:

  • Skewering corn with metal or bamboo sticks and wrapping the ends with cloth or silicone.
  • Carving a notch into a wooden spoon to cradle the cob.
  • Repurposing old metal coat hangers into makeshift grips.

All of these methods can work, but they also bring a few hidden drawbacks.

Drawbacks of Homemade Corn Holders

Even the most creative DIY solution can fall short when the grill heats up. Here are the most common issues:

  1. Heat Transfer: Metal or bare wood conducts heat quickly, meaning the holder can become scorching hot in seconds.
  2. Stability: Improvised grips often slip, especially when the cob gets slick from butter or sauce.
  3. Cleaning: Cloth‑wrapped sticks are difficult to rinse, and wooden handles can absorb oil, leading to lingering odors.
  4. Storage: Homemade holders are usually single‑piece items that take up drawer space and can get tangled.

If any of those sound familiar, you might be better off investing in a purpose‑built product.

How to Use the Girla Interlocking Corn Holders Effectively

Even though the product is ready to go, a few tips can help you get the most out of it:

  • Pre‑soak the corn: Soak ears in water for 10 minutes before grilling. This prevents the kernels from drying out and makes the cob easier to grip.
  • Insert the skewer at an angle: Slide the stainless‑steel tip through the base of the cob at a slight angle. The silicone handle will naturally sit on the opposite side, giving you leverage for turning.
  • Rotate frequently: Use the silicone grip to turn the corn every 3‑4 minutes. Even cooking prevents hot spots and ensures those beautiful grill marks.
  • Store interlocked: After the BBQ, push the handles together. The interlocking mechanism keeps them tidy and prevents the silicone from snagging on other utensils.

Following these steps means you’ll spend less time wrestling with the corn and more time enjoying the flavor.
