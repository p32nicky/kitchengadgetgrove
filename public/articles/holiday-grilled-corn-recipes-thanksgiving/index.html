Classic Charred Corn with Herb Butter

This simple recipe lets the corn’s natural sweetness shine, while a herb‑infused butter adds a festive touch.

  1. Preheat the grill to medium‑high (about 400°F).
  2. Remove the husks and silk from each ear. Pat dry.
  3. In a small bowl, combine ½ cup softened butter, 2 teaspoons minced fresh rosemary, 1 teaspoon thyme, a pinch of salt, and freshly cracked black pepper.
  4. Using the Girla Interlocking Corn Holders, slip each cob into a holder so the silicone grips face outward. Brush the butter mixture generously over the entire surface.
  5. Place the cobs directly on the grill grates. Cook 10–12 minutes, turning every 2–3 minutes, until the kernels are lightly charred and the butter is bubbling.
  6. Remove from heat, let cool for a minute, then serve with an extra pat of herb butter on the side.

Tip: The silicone handles stay cool, so you can lift the cobs without a mitt.

Maple‑Glazed Corn with Pecans

For a sweet‑and‑savory twist that nods to traditional Thanksgiving flavors, try this maple‑pecan glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • ¼ cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • ¼ cup chopped toasted pecans
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of sea salt

Method:

  1. Mix maple syrup, melted butter, cinnamon, and salt in a shallow dish.
  2. Thread each ear into a Girla holder, then brush the glaze onto the kernels, coating well.
  3. Grill over medium heat for 8–10 minutes, turning frequently so the glaze caramelizes without burning.
  4. During the last two minutes, sprinkle the toasted pecans over the corn, allowing them to adhere to the sticky glaze.
  5. Serve warm, letting the sweet maple glaze mingle with the smoky char.

These cobs make a great side or a festive appetizer for guests to enjoy while the turkey roasts.

Spicy Chili‑Lime Corn with Cotija Cheese

If you want a bit of heat, this Mexican‑inspired recipe brings a bold kick to the Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • ¼ cup crumbled Cotija cheese
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Directions:

  1. Combine olive oil, chili powder, smoked paprika, and lime juice in a bowl.
  2. Place each corn ear in a Girla holder and brush the chili‑lime mixture over all sides.
  3. Grill over medium‑high heat for 9–11 minutes, turning until the kernels are tender and lightly blackened.
  4. Remove from the grill, sprinkle Cotija cheese over the hot corn, and garnish with cilantro if desired.

The cheese adds a salty creaminess that balances the spice, making this a crowd‑pleaser for guests who love bold flavors.

FAQ

Can I use the Girla corn holders on a gas grill?

Yes. The silicone grips are heat‑resistant up to 500°F, so they work perfectly on gas, charcoal, or electric grills.

Do I need to soak the corn before grilling?

Soaking isn’t required, but a quick 15‑minute soak in cold water can help prevent the husks from burning if you prefer to grill with them on.

What’s the best way to reheat leftover grilled corn?

Wrap the cob in foil and heat in a 350°F oven for 10 minutes, or place directly on a low‑heat grill for a few minutes to revive the char.
