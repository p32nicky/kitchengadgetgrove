Ingredients for Gourmet Onion Jam

To make a batch that feels "wedding-ready," you want a balance of sweet, savory, and acidic. Here is what you'll need for a standard batch (which you can scale up based on your guest list):

    n
  • 5 lbs of yellow or sweet onions (sliced uniformly using your slicing guide)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional, for a subtle kick)

Step-by-Step: Crafting Your Onion Jam Wedding Favor

Start by prepping your onions. Use the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS) to slice all your onions into thin, half-moon shapes. Having the stainless steel prongs hold the vegetable steady means you can work faster and safer, which is critical when you're processing pounds of produce.

Melt the butter in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt. Cook them slowly, stirring occasionally, until they soften and turn a golden brown. This caramelization process is where the flavor lives, so don't rush it.

Once the onions are golden, stir in the brown sugar, balsamic vinegar, and seasonings. Lower the heat to a simmer and cook for another 30 to 45 minutes. The mixture should thicken and become glossy, resembling a thick jam. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before jarring.

Packaging and Presentation

The presentation is what turns a condiment into a wedding favor. Use 2-ounce or 4-ounce mini mason jars. Sterilize the jars in boiling water first to ensure the jam stays fresh. Spoon the jam in, leaving a small amount of headspace at the top.

For a rustic look, cover the lid with a small circle of brown kraft paper or a piece of linen fabric, secured with a piece of jute twine. Attach a small handwritten tag that says "Sweet & Savory" or "A Perfect Match," along with a brief serving suggestion (like "Pairs great with brie!").

FAQ

How long does homemade onion jam last?

When stored in airtight jars in the refrigerator, homemade onion jam typically lasts for 3 to 4 weeks. If you are making these far in advance of the wedding, you can freeze them for up to 3 months.

Can I use red onions instead of yellow?

Absolutely. Red onions provide a slightly sweeter flavor and a beautiful deep purple hue to the final jam. The slicing process remains exactly the same using the slicing guide.

Is the slicing guide dishwasher safe?

Yes, since the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide (2 PCS) is made of stainless steel, it is dishwasher safe. Just be sure to rinse any onion residue off before popping them in the rack.
