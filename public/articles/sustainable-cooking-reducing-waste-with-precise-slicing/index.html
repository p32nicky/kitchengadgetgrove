The Connection Between Precision and Waste

When you slice a potato or an onion haphazardly, you create inconsistent thicknesses. In the pan, this means some pieces burn while others stay raw. Often, these "misfit" pieces are tossed out because they don't fit the aesthetic of the dish or they've overcooked. Precision slicing ensures that every piece of produce cooks at the same rate, meaning less burnt food hitting the bin.

FAQ

Can I use a slicing guide for vegetables other than onions?

Absolutely. While often called an onion holder, these tools work wonders on tomatoes, potatoes, cucumbers, and zucchini. Anything cylindrical or semi-spherical that tends to roll on the cutting board can be stabilized for safer, more precise slicing.

How does precise slicing actually save money?

It saves money by increasing the yield of your produce. When you aren't trimming away large chunks for safety or tossing unevenly cooked pieces, you get more servings out of every vegetable you buy.

Is a slicing guide better than a mandoline?

Depending on your needs, yes. While mandolines are fast, they can be dangerous and often create more plastic waste. A stainless steel slicing guide is a safer, more durable alternative that gives you manual control over the thickness of your slices without the risk of high-speed blade accidents.
