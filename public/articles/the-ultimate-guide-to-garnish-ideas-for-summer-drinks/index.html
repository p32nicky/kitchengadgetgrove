Fruit‑Forward Garnishes

Fresh fruit is the most versatile garnish. Here are three quick methods:

  1. Layered Citrus Wheels: Slice lemons, limes, or oranges into thin wheels, then press them onto the rim of the glass. The bright rind releases essential oils for an aromatic boost.
  2. Berry Skewers: Thread strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries onto a cocktail pick. The 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks works beautifully here—just add an umbrella for extra flair.
  3. Watermelon Cubes: Cut watermelon into bite‑size cubes and freeze them. They keep drinks cold without diluting flavor, and the pink hue looks stunning in a clear glass.

For drinks that need a bit of crunch, try a thin slice of cucumber or a sprig of fresh mint placed alongside the fruit.

Herbs aren’t just for cooking; they can elevate a drink’s aroma and flavor profile. A sprig of rosemary or thyme tucked into a glass of gin‑soda releases a subtle pine note that pairs well with citrus. For an extra visual pop, freeze herbs inside ice cubes and drop them into the beverage.

Playful Edible Garnishes

Take garnish to the next level with edible elements that add texture:

FAQ

Can I reuse paper cocktail umbrellas?

Paper umbrellas are designed for single use to maintain hygiene, especially with drinks that are shared. If you’re serving a private gathering, you can gently rinse and reuse them, but it’s safest to treat them as disposable.

What’s the best way to store fresh fruit garnishes?

Keep sliced fruit in an airtight container in the refrigerator, and add a few drops of lemon juice to prevent browning. For whole berries, a damp paper towel over the container helps retain moisture without making them soggy.

Are the wooden spurtles safe for acidic drinks?

Yes. The spurtles are made from untreated hardwood that resists staining and odor absorption, making them safe for citrus‑based cocktails and other acidic beverages.
