  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water

  • 1 cup sparkling water or club soda, chilled

  • Ice cubes
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs or mint leaves for garnish

All you need beyond the ingredients is a sturdy pitcher, a small saucepan for the simple syrup, and a few serving glasses. The mini umbrellas are the star garnish; keep the pack nearby so you can pop a few into each glass as you pour.

Make a Quick Cranberry Simple Syrup

Start by turning the cranberries into a syrup that will give your spritzer its deep red hue and natural sweetness. Combine the cranberries, sugar, and water in a small saucepan. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about five minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the berries burst and the mixture thickens slightly, remove from heat and strain through a fine‑mesh sieve into a bowl. Discard the solids; the liquid is your cranberry simple syrup. Let it cool for a few minutes while you prepare the rest of the drink.Combine the Base and Add the Sparkle

In your 2‑liter pitcher, pour the cooled cranberry simple syrup, the cranberry juice, and the orange juice if you’re using it. Stir gently to blend the flavors. Only moments before serving, add the chilled sparkling water or club soda. This timing preserves the carbonation, giving the spritzer a lively fizz that makes every sip feel celebratory.

Season the mixture with a pinch of salt—yes, a pinch—to enhance the fruit flavors without making the drink salty. Then, add a handful of ice cubes to keep the spritzer chilled without diluting it too quickly.

Dress It Up with Mini Umbrellas and Fresh Herbs

Now for the fun part. Take a few of the 150 PCS Drink Umbrellas Cocktail Picks, Mini Paper Parasols Toothpicks Sticks and insert one into each glass. The three styles—tiki, luau, and tropical—offer variety, so you can match the umbrella to the drink’s color or the theme of your gathering. Place a sprig of rosemary or a mint leaf next to the umbrella for an aromatic garnish that complements the cranberry’s tartness.

When pouring, tilt the pitcher slightly and let the spritzer flow over the ice and into each glass, allowing the mini umbrella to float just above the surface. The parasol’s paper canopy catches the light, creating a festive sparkle that looks great in holiday photos.

Tips for Customizing Your Holiday Cranberry Spritzer

Even though the base recipe is solid, you can tweak it to suit different tastes or dietary needs:

  1. Sweetness level: Adjust the simple syrup by using less sugar or substituting honey for a more natural sweetness.
  2. Alcohol boost: Add a splash of vodka, gin, or sparkling wine to turn the spritzer into a cocktail. One ounce per glass works well.
  3. Flavor twists: Mix in a splash of pomegranate juice for extra depth, or a few dashes of orange bitters for complexity.
  4. Seasonal garnish: Swap rosemary for rosemary sprigs dusted with edible gold glitter for an extra holiday sparkle.

All of these variations still pair beautifully with the mini umbrellas, keeping the presentation fun and consistent.

Storing Leftovers and Reusing the Umbrellas

If you’ve made more spritzer than you can drink in one night, store any leftover liquid in a sealed glass container in the refrigerator. The carbonation will fade, but you can re‑sparkle it by adding a splash of fresh club soda before serving.

The paper parasol picks are single‑use, but they can be recycled after the party. Keep a small bin nearby for guests to drop used umbrellas, making cleanup a breeze and reducing waste.
