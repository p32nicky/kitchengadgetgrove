Choosing the Right Drinks for an Outdoor Movie

Think about the setting and the length of the film. You’ll want drinks that stay refreshing for a few hours and can be sipped easily in the dark. Here are three categories that work every time:

  • Classic Cocktails – Margaritas, mojitos, or a simple gin‑and‑tonic. Serve them over ice in clear glasses so the umbrella straw’s color pops.
  • Fruity Mocktails – Pineapple‑coconut punch, watermelon agua fresca, or sparkling cranberry spritzer. These keep kids and designated drivers feeling festive.
  • Cold Brew & Soft Drinks – A chilled iced tea or craft soda can be dressed up with an umbrella straw for a low‑effort upgrade.

All three types pair well with the mini parasol design, ensuring that each sip feels like a mini‑vacation.

Set up a self‑serve bar that lets guests customize their drinks. This reduces bottlenecks and encourages conversation before the film starts.

  1. Prep the Base Liquids: Mix a large batch of margarita or pineapple punch in a cooler. Keep it chilled with ice packs.
  2. Provide Mix‑Ins: Offer sliced citrus, fresh mint, and cocktail cherries in separate bowls.
  3. Lay Out the Umbrella Straws: Open the 100-Pack Umbrella Straws and arrange them in a tall jar so guests can easily pull one out. The bright colors make it simple to spot in low light.
  4. Supply Glassware: Use clear plastic cups for safety, or sturdy glass tumblers if you prefer the classic look.

With this station, guests can build their own drinks, and the umbrella straws become a natural part of the décor.

Keeping Drinks Cool All Night Long

Outdoor evenings can get warm, especially after the sun sets. Here are three tricks to maintain chill temperatures without constant refilling:

  • Use a dry ice block at the bottom of the cooler—just a small piece will keep the liquid cold for hours.
  • Pre‑freeze the umbrella straws themselves. A quick 5‑minute dip in ice water adds a subtle frosty feel to each sip.
  • Place the drink station under a shaded canopy or tablecloth to protect it from residual heat.

These methods keep the outdoor movie night drinks refreshing from the opening credits to the final fade‑out.
