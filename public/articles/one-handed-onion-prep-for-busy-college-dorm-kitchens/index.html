Step‑by‑Step: One‑Handed Onion Slicing

  1. Place the onion on the guide. The prongs bite into the skin, holding the bulb firmly.
  2. Secure the guide. Use the rubber base to lock it on a stable surface. No wobble means fewer accidents.
  3. Use your free hand. Grab the knife with the opposite hand, keeping the blade perpendicular to the guide.
  4. Slice through. The guide forces a clean cut, producing uniform slices in half the time.
  5. Repeat. Flip the onion, adjust the guide if needed, and keep slicing until you reach the desired quantity.

Because the guide keeps the knife from drifting, you can focus on other tasks—like stirring a pot or microwaving a burrito—without worrying about a stray blade.
