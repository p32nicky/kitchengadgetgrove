Smart Cleanup and Organization Habits

The biggest hurdle to consistent meal prep is the cleanup. If you leave a mountain of dishes, you'll dread doing it next week. Try these habits to keep your workspace clear:

  • The 'Clean as You Go' Rule: While your protein is roasting or your dough is rising, load the dishwasher. Never leave a bowl soaking for "later."
  • Station Setup: Designate a "wet zone" for washing and a "dry zone" for chopping. This prevents cross-contamination and limits the number of surfaces you have to wipe down.
  • Batch-Cleaning Tools: Use a bench scraper to push all your vegetable scraps into the compost bin at once rather than making ten trips across the kitchen.

FAQ

What are the best kitchen hacks for meal prep beginners?

Start by investing in tools that reduce manual labor, such as a slicing guide for vegetables and a dedicated dough whisk if you bake. Focus on "batching" similar tasks—chop all your vegetables first, then prep all your proteins, rather than switching back and forth.

How can I reduce cleanup time after prepping?

The most effective method is the "clean as you go" approach. Additionally, using tools designed for easy cleaning—like dishwasher-safe silicone holders and stainless steel pans—reduces the amount of hand-scrubbing required.

How do I keep my sourdough starter jars clean?

Use a skinny wooden spurtle or a dedicated sourdough stirrer. These are designed to reach the sides and bottom of narrow jars, ensuring you scrape out all the starter and leave less residue behind to harden and stick to the glass.
