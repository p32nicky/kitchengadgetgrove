The Secret to Gluten-Free Falafel Stability

The biggest challenge with gluten-free falafel is the "crumble factor." Without wheat flour to bind everything together, falafel balls can fall apart when you slide them onto a skewer. The key is using a combination of soaked chickpeas and a stabilizing agent like chickpea flour (besan) or a small amount of cornstarch.

Avoid using canned chickpeas if you can. Dried chickpeas soaked overnight provide a starchier texture that grips the skewer better. If you must use canned, drain them thoroughly and pat them dry with a paper towel to prevent the mixture from becoming too wet, which leads to mushy centers.

Preparing Your Vertical Skewer Pan Falafel

Before you start stacking, you need a dense, flavorful dough. Pulse soaked chickpeas in a food processor with fresh parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, salt, and a pinch of baking soda. The baking soda is crucial—it creates tiny air bubbles that keep the falafel light despite the lack of gluten.

Once your mixture is ready, form the falafel into firm, slightly oversized balls. Make them about 1.5 inches in diameter. Since the Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven allows for vertical roasting, the balls will shrink slightly as they cook, so a slightly larger initial size ensures a hearty serving.

How to Stack and Roast for Maximum Crisp

To use your gluten free falafel vertical skewer pan effectively, you want to stack the balls firmly but not so tightly that they crush each other. Slide the falafel balls onto the stainless steel skewer, pressing each one down slightly to create a solid column. This ensures the skewer stays stable and the heat penetrates the center of the stack.

Brush the exterior of the falafel column generously with olive oil. Because we aren't submerging these in oil, the brush-on method is essential for that characteristic fried crunch. Place the skewer into the Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven, which will catch all the drippings and keep your oven floor clean.

Set your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roast for 25 to 35 minutes. Halfway through, I recommend rotating the skewer 180 degrees to ensure an even tan on all sides. You'll know they are done when the exterior is a deep golden brown and the aroma of toasted cumin fills the kitchen.

Serving Your Vertical Roasted Falafel

Once the timer goes off, let the skewer rest for five minutes before sliding the falafel balls off. This allows the internal proteins to set, ensuring they don't break when you plate them. Since these are gluten-free, they pair perfectly with a variety of naturally GF accompaniments:

  • Tahini Sauce: Lemon juice, garlic, and sesame paste.
  • Fresh Salad: Diced cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onion with parsley.
  • Wraps: Use a large collard green leaf or a certified gluten-free tortilla.
  • Dip: A dollop of hummus or a spicy harissa paste.

FAQ

Can I use frozen falafel with a vertical skewer pan?

Yes, but I don't recommend it. Pre-frozen gluten-free falafel are often too fragile to be skewered. For the best results, make your own fresh dough so you can control the binding and ensure the balls stay on the skewer during the roasting process.

Why use a vertical pan instead of a standard baking sheet?

On a flat sheet, the bottom of the falafel often becomes soggy or sticks to the pan. The vertical orientation allows hot air to hit the falafel from all angles and lets excess oil drip away from the food, resulting in a crisper exterior and a more uniform cook.

What can I use as a binder if I don't have chickpea flour?

If you're out of chickpea flour, a tablespoon of ground flaxseed mixed with water (a flax egg) or a small amount of mashed potato works well to hold the gluten-free mixture together on the skewer.
