Tips for the Perfect Vertical Roast

To get that professional street-food texture, you need to manage your layers. When stacking meat on the stainless steel vertical skewer pan, press the pieces down firmly. This minimizes air gaps, which prevents the meat from drying out and helps the heat penetrate evenly from the outside in.

When it comes to carving, use a very sharp carving knife or an electric slicer. Slice vertically, cutting thin ribbons of meat from the top down. The Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven is designed to catch the drippings in the base, which you can actually use to brush back onto the meat for extra flavor during the final stages of cooking.

Planning Your Party Logistics

Since the cooking happens in the oven, timing is everything. Start your roast about two hours before guests arrive. This allows the meat to reach its peak internal temperature and gives you a window to let the meat rest for 15-20 minutes before the first slice.

Keep your serving area organized. Place the Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven at the center of the table, surrounded by bowls of sauces and baskets of warm bread. This creates a focal point for the party, encouraging guests to gather around and chat while they customize their wraps.

FAQ

Can I use the vertical skewer pan on an outdoor grill?

The Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven is specifically designed for oven use to ensure stable heat distribution. For outdoor parties, we recommend roasting the meat in the oven and bringing the pan outside for carving and serving.

What is the best meat for vertical roasting?

Meats with a bit of fat work best, as the fat renders and bastes the meat as it drips down. Pork shoulder is ideal for Al Pastor, while boneless chicken thighs are best for shawarma or gyros.

How do I clean a stainless steel skewer pan?

Because it is stainless steel, the pan is durable and easy to clean. Soak it in warm, soapy water to loosen any caramelized bits, then use a non-abrasive scrub sponge to restore the shine.
