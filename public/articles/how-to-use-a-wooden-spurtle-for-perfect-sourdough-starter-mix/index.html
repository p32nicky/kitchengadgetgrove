Preparing Your Tools Before Mixing

Start with a clean, dry spurtle. Even though wood is naturally antimicrobial, any residual flour or moisture can introduce unwanted flavors. Rinse the spurtle under warm water, wipe it dry with a lint‑free cloth, and let it air‑dry for a few minutes. This quick prep ensures the wood won’t absorb excess water during the mixing process.

Step‑by‑Step: How to Use Wooden Spurtle for Sourdough Starter

  1. Measure your ingredients. For a typical 100 g starter, combine 50 g whole‑grain flour and 50 g water in a clear glass jar. Using a kitchen scale keeps ratios exact.
  2. Insert the thin spurtle. Hold the wooden spurtle by the broader end; the tapered tip should be pointing down. This angle lets you stir without lifting the flour‑water mixture out of the jar.
  3. Stir in a figure‑eight motion. Move the spurtle in a gentle figure‑eight, pulling the mixture from the sides and pushing it through the center. The wooden surface encourages a smooth whirl without creating air pockets.
  4. Scrape the bottom and sides. Use the pointed tip to loosen any stubborn bits that cling to the glass. The non‑scratch nature of the wooden spurtle makes it perfect for jar scraping without leaving marks.
  5. Cover and let ferment. After a thorough mix, seal the jar loosely and place it at room temperature. The starter will bubble within 4‑12 hours, depending on ambient temperature.

Repeat the feeding routine daily, and you’ll notice the mixture becomes more homogenous after each stir. The consistency you achieve with a wooden spurtle is harder to replicate with a regular spoon, which often leaves pockets of dry flour.

Maintaining Your Wooden Spurtle for Longevity

Wood can dry out over time, especially with frequent exposure to water. To keep the spurtle supple, oil it lightly once a month with a food‑grade mineral oil or a dab of coconut oil. Wipe off excess oil, and store the spurtle in a dry drawer. Proper care ensures the spurtle remains a reliable tool for years of sourdough feeding.

FAQ

Can I use the wooden spurtle for other doughs?

Absolutely. Its thin profile works well for high‑hydration doughs, pancake batter, and even for stirring wet batters in a mixing bowl.

Is it safe to wash the spurtle in the dishwasher?

No. The high heat and detergent can cause the wood to crack or warp. Hand‑wash with warm water, dry promptly, and oil occasionally.

Do I need to sterilize the spurtle before each use?

Routine cleaning is sufficient. The natural antimicrobial properties of wood, combined with thorough rinsing, keep it safe for daily starter feedings.
