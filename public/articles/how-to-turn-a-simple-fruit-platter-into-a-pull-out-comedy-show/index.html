Three Ways to Execute the Gag

Depending on your level of boldness, there are a few different ways to use your telescopic forks to get a laugh:

  • The Long Reach: Sit comfortably in your chair and wait for someone to mention a specific piece of fruit on the far side of the platter. Instead of standing up, slowly extend the fork to snag the fruit from a distance. The look of confusion on their faces as the fork "grows" is priceless.
  • The "Invisible" Thief: While a guest is distracted, extend the fork from behind a centerpiece or a napkin. Gently snag a piece of fruit and pull it back quickly. When they look up, their snack is gone, and you are sitting there looking completely innocent.
  • The Hand-Off: Hand the fork to a friend who isn't in on the joke. As they reach for the fruit, gently give the handle a nudge or encourage them to "reach further." When the fork telescopes outward, the surprise is on them.

Maintaining Your Prank Gear

Since these are stainless steel, they are durable, but telescoping mechanisms can get gunked up if you're stabbing juicy watermelon or sticky pineapple. To keep your comedy forks in top shape, follow a few simple rules.

First, avoid using them for heavy-duty tasks like stabbing a whole apple or a hard piece of raw carrot; these are for "comedy," not for heavy lifting. Second, rinse them immediately after use. If sugary fruit juices dry inside the telescoping joints, the extension might become jerky or stick. A quick rinse with warm soapy water keeps the glide smooth for the next party.
