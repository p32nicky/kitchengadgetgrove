Why a Wooden Spurtle Elevates Your Focaccia Bread Recipe

Focaccia requires gentle yet thorough dough handling to maintain its airy texture and absorb olive oil, herbs, and toppings. A wooden spurtle—a long, flat tool with a curved end—offers precision and control. Unlike rubber spatulas, its wood surface won’t scratch mixing bowls or sourdough jars, and its ergonomic shape makes folding dough or pressing toppings a breeze.

How to Use a Wooden Spurtle in Your Focaccia Routine

Integrating a spurtle into your workflow streamlines focaccia prep. Here’s how:

  1. Mixing dough: Use the flat side to scrape down bowl edges while combining flour, water, and yeast.
  2. Folding in flavor: Gently swirl in olive oil or herbs with the spurtle’s curved end to avoid deflating bubbles.
  3. Shaping and topping: Press olives, rosemary, or sea salt into the dough’s surface using light, even strokes.
