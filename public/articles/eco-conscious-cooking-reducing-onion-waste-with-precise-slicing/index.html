The Connection Between Precision and Waste

Food waste often happens in the "margins." When we slice haphazardly, we often cut too deep into the root or leave uneven chunks that burn while the thinner slices turn to mush. This inconsistency leads to discarded pieces that were perfectly edible but sliced too poorly to use.

Tips for Zero-Waste Onion Prep

To get the most out of your produce, combine your precision tools with these storage habits. Even with perfect slicing, you might have a few small bits left over. Instead of tossing them, freeze them in a small container with a bit of olive oil. The next time you start a soup or a stew, drop in a frozen "onion puck" to build a flavor base.

Also, consider the skins. While they aren't edible in a sauté, they are gold for vegetable broth. Store your clean onion skins in a freezer bag and simmer them with carrot peels and celery ends for a rich, homemade stock that costs nothing and produces zero waste.
