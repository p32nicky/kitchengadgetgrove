Step‑by‑Step Sourdough Croissants Recipe

Ingredients (makes 12 large croissants)

  • 150 g active sourdough starter (100 % hydration)
  • 250 g cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 500 g bread flour
  • 50 g whole‑wheat flour
  • 40 g granulated sugar
  • 10 g salt
  • 250 ml cold water
  • 1 tsp instant yeast (optional, for a shorter proof)

1. Autolyse – In a large bowl, combine the bread flour, whole‑wheat flour, and water. Stir with the Danish dough whisk until just incorporated. Cover and let rest 30 minutes. This hydrates the flour and starts gluten development.

2. Incorporate Starter – Add the active sourdough starter, sugar, and salt to the autolysed dough. Mix with the whisk until the mixture is smooth and no dry spots remain. If you prefer a quicker rise, dissolve the instant yeast in a tablespoon of warm water and add it now.

3. First Fold – Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Using the dough scraper, fold the dough into thirds (like a letter). Rotate 90°, repeat the fold. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

4. Butter Block – Place the cubed butter between two sheets of parchment. Using the whisk’s flat side, pound the butter until it’s pliable but still cold (about 15 seconds). Shape it into a 10 × 10 cm square.

5. Lamination – Roll the rested dough into a 20 × 20 cm square. Place the butter block in the center, fold the dough over the butter on all sides, sealing the edges. Roll the sealed package into a rectangle 30 × 15 cm. Fold the rectangle into thirds (like a business letter). Wrap in plastic and chill 30 minutes. Repeat the roll‑and‑fold process two more times, chilling 30 minutes between each turn.6. Shaping – After the final chill, roll the dough to a 4 mm thickness. Cut triangles 12 cm on the base and 20 cm tall. Starting at the base, roll tightly toward the tip, tucking the tip underneath the croissant.

7. Proof – Place the shaped croissants on a parchment‑lined tray, spaced apart. Cover loosely with a clean kitchen towel and let them proof at room temperature until they’re about 1.5× their original size (roughly 2‑3 hours, depending on ambient temperature).

8. Bake – Preheat the oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Lightly brush the croissants with an egg wash (optional) and use the bread scoring lame to make a shallow cut on the tip of each croissant – this encourages a controlled expansion. Bake for 15‑18 minutes, until golden brown and crisp.

Cool on a rack for at least 10 minutes before serving. The interior should be airy with distinct layers, and the crust should have that signature buttery snap.

Tips for Consistent Results

Even with a solid recipe, a few nuances can make a big difference:

  • Keep everything cold: The butter must stay firm during lamination; if it softens, the layers will fuse.
  • Don’t over‑mix: Once the starter is added, mix just until combined. Over‑mixing can develop too much gluten, making the dough tough.
  • Watch the proof: Sourdough croissants can over‑proof quickly in a warm kitchen. If the dough feels very soft and puffy, move it to a cooler spot.
  • Use the scoring lame: The 3‑in‑1 Sourdough Whisk Set includes a lame that gives you precise control when scoring the croissants, improving oven spring.

Storing and Reheating

Fresh croissants are best the day they’re baked, but you can freeze them for later enjoyment. After cooling, wrap each croissant tightly in foil and place them in a freezer bag. To reheat, preheat the oven to 180 °C (350 °F) and bake frozen croissants for 12‑15 minutes, or until the crust is crisp and the interior is warm.

FAQ

Can I make sourdough croissants without a starter?

Yes, you can substitute a portion of the starter with commercial yeast, but you’ll lose the characteristic tang and some of the tender crumb that the starter provides.

Do I need to use the Danish dough whisk, or can I mix by hand?

The Danish dough whisk is ideal for evenly distributing the starter without over‑working the dough, but a sturdy wooden spoon or hand mixing will also work if you’re careful.

How long can I keep the dough in the fridge before shaping?

The laminated dough can be refrigerated for up to 24 hours after the final turn. Just keep it wrapped tightly to prevent it from drying out.
