Step-by-Step Slicing Technique for Maki Rolls

Here’s how to use the slicing guide for perfect onion prep:

  1. Prepare the onion: Remove the root end (which tends to bleed tears) and slice off the top cap. The root end can be tricky, so save it for last.
  2. Secure it: Place the onion flat side down in the holder’s prongs. Gently press to anchor it without damaging the knife blade.
  3. Slice vertically: Use a sharp knife to cut straight down, letting the holder guide the blade. Start with 1/8-inch slices for maki rolls; adjust thickness as needed.
  4. Rotate and repeat: Turn the onion 90 degrees and slice the other way for a crosshatch pattern. This creates dice-like pieces that hold up in rolls without crumbling.

The result? Neat, even slices that cut prep time in half and reduce the stress of manual slicing.
