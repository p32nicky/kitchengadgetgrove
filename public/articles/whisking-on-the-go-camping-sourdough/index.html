Why Sourdough for Your Camping Adventures?

Sourdough stands out for its durability and simplicity. Unlike commercial yeast, a sourdough starter thrives on basic ingredients—flour, water, and salt—all easy to pack. The natural fermentation process also pre-digests grains, making campfire loaves easier to digest and richer in flavor. Plus, a well-proofed dough can handle temperature fluctuations better than delicate batters.

Two Portable Sourdough Recipes for the Trail

Here’s how to bake with minimal gear:

  1. One-Bag Campfire Loaf: Mix 2 cups flour, 1 cup starter, and 3/4 cup water in a zipper pouch. Let proof in a dry cooler, then bake in a preheated cast iron skillet over coals.
  2. Flatbreads on a Stick: Use the scraper to shape small rounds, then cook them directly on a grill stick over medium heat. Score with the lame for campfire-style crusts.
