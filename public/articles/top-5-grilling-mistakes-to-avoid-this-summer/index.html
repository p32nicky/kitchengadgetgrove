Summer grilling is all about flavor, but even the best recipes can fall apart with common grilling mistakes. From messy flames to undercooked meat, these errors are easy to avoid with the right know-how and tools. Let’s tackle the top 5 common grilling mistakes to ensure your summer cookouts are a hit.

1. Not Cleaning the Grill Before You Start

A greasy, sooty grill bed is an open invitation for smoke, sticking food, and uneven heat. Neglecting to clean the grates or burners is a top common grilling mistake that ruins texture and taste. Always:

  1. Scrub cold grates with a brush after each use, not hot ones.
  2. Deep-clean weekly with warm, soapy water for stubborn debris.
  3. Check for rust on gas grills and replace damaged parts.

2. Skipping the Temperature Check

Eyeing the clock instead of the thermometer is a classic mistake. Without verifying the grill’s temp, you risk over- or undercooked food. Use an instant-read thermometer for meat (like 145°F for fish, 160°F for burgers) and preheat for 10-15 minutes before cooking. Don’t guess—measure.

3. Forgetting to Oil the Grates

Oiling the grates keeps food from sticking and adds a nice sear. Rub the grates with tongs and a paper towel dipped in high-smoke-point oil (like canola or peanut oil) before cooking. This step is nonnegotiable for steaks, veggies, or corn—yes, even if you’re using a Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders Set (more on that later).

4. Letting Marinade Cause Flare-Ups

Sugary or greasy marinades drip onto hot coals and cause dangerous flare-ups. The fix? Pat food dry before grilling to reduce excess moisture, and brush marinade on during cooking, not before. For meats, even a quick wrap in parchment paper can shield against flare-ups.
