Choosing the Right Corn and Veggies

Start with fresh, in‑season produce. Here’s a quick checklist:

  • Corn: Look for husked ears with bright green silk and firm kernels. If you’re using frozen corn, thaw and pat dry first.
  • Bell peppers: Red, orange, or yellow add color and sweetness.
  • Zucchini or yellow squash: Slice into half‑inches; they hold up well on the grill.
  • Mushrooms: Cremini or button mushrooms work best; they absorb marinades nicely.
  • Red onion: Cut into wedges for a mildly caramelized bite.

Having a variety of colors not only makes the kabobs Instagram‑ready, it also gives you a broader spectrum of nutrients.

Preparing the Kabobs (And Using the Girla Interlocking Corn Holders)

While the veggies soak in a simple oil‑and‑herb glaze, you’ll need a safe way to hold the corn on the grill. The Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders Set – 5 Pairs (10 Pcs) – features silicone handles that stay cool to the touch, preventing burns and keeping your hands clean.

Steps:

  1. Trim the ends of each ear of corn and snap off the husk. Insert a silicone holder into each end of the cob; the interlocking design keeps the two halves together for tidy storage.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together 3 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, ½ tsp garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the corn holders (with the corn inside) and all cut vegetables into the bowl. Toss gently to coat every piece.
  4. Thread the corn, peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and onion onto metal or bamboo skewers, alternating colors for visual appeal.
  5. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium‑high heat. Grill the kabobs for 8‑10 minutes, turning once, until the corn kernels are lightly charred and the veggies are tender.

The silicone handles on the Girla holders let you flip the corn without using tongs or risking a slip, and they’re dishwasher‑safe for quick cleanup.

Flavor Boosters and Serving Ideas

Once the kabobs are off the grill, a few finishing touches can elevate the dish:

  • Lemon‑herb drizzle: Mix fresh lemon juice, chopped parsley, and a drizzle of extra‑virgin olive oil.
  • Spicy aioli: Stir a teaspoon of sriracha into mayo for a creamy kick.
  • Cheese crumble: Sprinkle feta or cotija over the hot corn for a salty contrast.

Serve the kabobs over a bed of mixed greens, alongside quinoa, or simply on a plate with crusty bread. They’re also great for meal‑prep: store in airtight containers and reheat in the microwave or on a skillet for a next‑day lunch.

FAQ

Can I use wooden skewers instead of metal?

Yes. Soak wooden skewers in water for at least 30 minutes before threading the veggies. This prevents them from burning on the grill.

Do the Girla corn holders work with frozen corn?

The silicone handles stay cool, but for best results use fresh or fully thawed corn. Frozen kernels can become soggy once grilled.

How do I store leftover kabobs?

Place them in a shallow airtight container and refrigerate for up to three days. Reheat in a skillet or microwave; the corn will stay juicy thanks to the silicone holders keeping the husk intact during cooking.
