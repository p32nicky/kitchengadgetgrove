1. Mini Sourdough Pizza

Ingredients:
- 1 cup active sourdough starter
- 1½ cups warm water
- 3 cups all‑purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- Olive oil, pizza sauce, shredded cheese, and toppings of choice.

Directions:
1. In a large bowl, mix starter, water, and flour until a shaggy dough forms. Let it rest 30 minutes.
2. Sprinkle a little flour on a clean surface and knead with a wooden spurtle or your hands until smooth (about 5 minutes).
3. Divide the dough into 4 equal pieces, roll into discs, and let rest 10 minutes.
4. Top with sauce, cheese, and toppings. Bake at 475°F for 12–15 minutes.

2. Sourdough Banana Bread

Ingredients:
- 1 cup sourdough starter
- 1 cup mashed ripe bananas (≈2 large)
- ½ cup melted butter
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 eggs
- 2 cups flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- ½ tsp salt.

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a loaf pan.
2. In a bowl, whisk starter, bananas, butter, and sugar. Add eggs.
3. Stir in flour, soda, and salt with the wooden spurtle until just combined.
4. Pour batter into pan and bake 55–60 minutes.

3. Tangy Sourdough Crackers

Ingredients:
- 1 cup sourdough starter
- 1 cup water
- 2 cups whole‑wheat flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Optional: herbs or seeds for topping.

Directions:
1. Mix starter, water, flour, salt, and oil into a dough. Let rest 30 minutes.
2. Roll out thinly on a floured surface using a rolling pin or the wooden spurtle to smooth.
3. Cut into squares, sprinkle herbs, and bake at 375°F for 15 minutes.

How the Wooden Spurtle Makes Mixing Easy

Step‑by‑Step Starter Feeding with a Spurtle

1. Measure equal parts starter and flour (e.g., ½ cup starter + ½ cup flour).
2. Pour into a clean jar and add the same amount of warm water.
3. Stir with the wooden spurtle until smooth.
4. Cover loosely and let sit 12–16 hours. The spurtle’s wood is naturally antimicrobial, so the jar stays clean.

Safety Tips for Kids Using the Spurtle

  • Supervise while the dough is at room temperature to avoid burns.
  • Teach children to hold the spurtle by the handle, not the blade, to prevent splintering.
  • Encourage gentle pressure—over‑kneading can create a tough loaf.

Cleaning and Care for Your Wooden Spurtle

After each use, rinse the spurtle with warm water and a mild soap. Dry immediately to prevent warping. Do not soak or put in the dishwasher; a quick wipe with a damp cloth is enough. The wood’s natural oils will keep it supple and ready for the next bake.

FAQ

What is a wooden spurtle and why is it better than plastic?

A spurtle is a smooth, flat wooden tool traditionally used in Scottish cooking. Unlike plastic, wood doesn’t scratch dough, keeps heat from searing the surface, and adds a natural, earthy feel to the mixing process.

Can I use the spurtle for other baking tasks?

Absolutely. The skinny Danish‑style stick works great for whisking eggs, stirring sauces, or scraping the last bits out of a jar. It’s a versatile kitchen companion.

How often should I replace my wooden spurtle?

With proper care—drying after use and occasional oiling—the spurtle can last years. Replace only if you notice cracks, splinters, or a strong odor that doesn’t go away with cleaning.
