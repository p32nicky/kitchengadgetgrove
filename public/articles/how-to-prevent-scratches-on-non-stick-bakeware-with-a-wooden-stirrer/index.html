Maintaining Your Wooden Tools for Better Performance

To ensure your wooden stirrers continue to protect your bakeware, you have to take care of the wood. A dried-out wooden spoon can develop splinters or rough edges, which—while not as damaging as metal—can still cause wear and tear over time.

Avoid putting your wooden tools in the dishwasher. The high heat and harsh detergents cause wood to warp and crack. Instead, hand wash them with mild soap and air dry. Every few months, rub a food-grade mineral oil into the wood. This keeps the surface smooth and creates a moisture barrier, ensuring your tools remain slick and safe for your non-stick pans.

FAQ

Can I use silicone instead of wood for non-stick?

Yes, silicone is also safe for non-stick surfaces. However, many bakers prefer wood for tasks like sourdough mixing because wood provides more rigidity and a better "feel" for the dough's consistency than flexible silicone.

Will a wooden stirrer ever scratch my pan?

It is extremely unlikely for a smooth wooden tool to scratch a non-stick coating. However, if the wood becomes severely splintered or cracked, those rough edges could potentially cause wear. This is why regular oiling and hand-washing are important.

Is a spurtle better than a regular spoon for baking?

For many tasks, yes. A spurtle's skinny, elongated shape makes it far superior for stirring starters in jars and scraping the edges of bakeware without hitting the sides with a wide, bulky head.
