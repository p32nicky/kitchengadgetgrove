How the 2 PCS Onion Holder & Slicing Guide Works

The tool consists of two identical stainless‑steel units. Each unit features three curved prongs that slide into the top of an onion (or similar round produce) and a recessed slot that guides a standard kitchen knife. To use:

  1. Peel the onion and trim the root end for stability.
  2. Insert the prongs into the onion’s stem cavity; the metal grips without crushing the flesh.
  3. Place the onion on the cutting board, align the built‑in guide, and slice with a gentle rocking motion.

The result is a stack of even rings in seconds. When you’re done, simply lift the holder, and the onion releases cleanly—no extra fiddling required.

Choosing the Right Knife Pairing

While the holder works with most kitchen knives, a 6‑inch chef’s knife or a standard utility knife gives the best balance of control and leverage. Avoid heavy cleavers; they can overwhelm the guide’s slot and make the slicing motion feel jerky. A sharp blade is essential—dull knives require more force, which defeats the safety benefit of the holder.

Maintaining the Stainless‑Steel Finish

RV kitchens are prone to humidity, and stainless steel can develop water spots if not dried properly. After each use, rinse the onion holder under warm water, dry it with a microfiber cloth, and store it in a dry drawer. If you notice any lingering odor (onions love to cling), a quick soak in a mixture of water and a splash of white vinegar will neutralize it without damaging the metal.
