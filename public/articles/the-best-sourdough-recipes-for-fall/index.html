Savory Autumn Sourdough Variations

While we love a sweet treat, fall is the prime time for savory, earthy loaves. I love incorporating roasted garlic and rosemary into my base dough. The key is to roast the garlic cloves in olive oil until they are jammy, then fold them in during the stretch-and-fold phase.

Another fan favorite is the Pumpkin Sage loaf. By replacing a small portion of the water in your recipe with pumpkin puree, you get a gorgeous orange crumb and a slightly denser, more custard-like texture. Use the Danish dough whisk from the 3-in-1 Sourdough Whisk Set to incorporate the heavy puree without overworking the gluten or leaving clumps of pumpkin in the dough.

Sweet Sourdough Treats for Cozy Evenings

Sourdough isn't just for crusty boules. Using a sourdough discard allows you to make incredible pastries with a complex, tangy depth that commercial yeast can't replicate. Apple Cinnamon Sourdough swirls are a staple in my house every October. I roll out the dough, spread a layer of cinnamon sugar and thinly sliced Granny Smith apples, and roll it into a log.

For those who prefer something heartier, try a Maple Pecan Sourdough. Fold toasted pecans and a swirl of pure maple syrup into the dough during the final bulk fermentation. Mastering the Best Sourdough Recipes for Fall

Autumn baking requires a few adjustments. Since your kitchen temperature likely drops in October and November, your bulk fermentation will take longer. Don't rely strictly on the timer; watch the dough. It should be jiggly, have a few visible bubbles on the surface, and have increased in volume by about 50% to 75%.

To ensure your loaves look as good as they taste, focus on your scoring. A deep, confident cut allows the bread to expand upward rather than bursting at the seams. Essential Tips for Fall Baking Success

To keep your starter active and your loaves consistent during the seasonal transition, keep these practical tips in mind:

  • Warm your water: Use lukewarm water (around 80-85°F) to jumpstart fermentation when the house is chilly.
  • Proof in a warm spot: Place your dough in the oven with the light turned on to create a makeshift proofing box.
  • Adjust hydration: Fall flours can vary in absorption. If your dough feels too sticky, add a tablespoon of flour at a time during the initial mix.
  • Use the right tools: Avoid using a standard spoon for mixing; a Danish dough whisk is designed to cut through heavy dough without incorporating too much excess air.

FAQ

Why is my sourdough taking longer to rise in the fall?

Wild yeast is temperature-sensitive. As your kitchen cools down, the yeast metabolic rate slows. This isn't a problem—it actually improves the flavor—but you will need to extend your bulk fermentation time by several hours.

Can I use pumpkin puree in every sourdough recipe?

Not exactly. Pumpkin puree adds moisture and sugar, which changes the hydration and fermentation speed. If you add puree, you should slightly reduce the water in your recipe to maintain the same dough consistency.

How do I store my sourdough starter during winter?

If you bake daily, keep it on the counter. However, if you only bake once a week, move your starter to the refrigerator. This slows the yeast down, meaning you only need to feed it once a week instead of every day.
