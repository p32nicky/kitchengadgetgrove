Ingredients & Tools You’ll Need

  • 2 large cucumbers, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, loosely packed
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 2 tablespoons simple syrup (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups cold sparkling water or club soda
  • Ice cubes
  • 150‑piece cocktail drink umbrellas (3‑style mini paper parasols)
  • Fine‑mesh strainer, cocktail shaker, and a large pitcher

Step‑by‑Step: Making the Cucumber Mint Cooler

1. Prep the cucumber base. In a blender, combine the sliced cucumbers with 1 cup of water. Blend until smooth, then strain through a fine‑mesh sieve into the pitcher. Press the pulp with the back of a spoon to extract as much liquid as possible.

2. Infuse the mint. Add the fresh mint leaves to the cucumber juice and let sit for 5 minutes. For a stronger flavor, gently muddle the mint with a wooden spoon before steeping.

3. Mix the citrus and sweetener. Stir in the lime juice and simple syrup. Taste and adjust the sweetness—remember the sparkling water will dilute the flavor slightly.

4. Chill and carbonate. Add the cold sparkling water to the mixture, then fill the pitcher with ice cubes. Give everything a quick stir.

5. Garnish with mini umbrellas. Place a 150‑pack cocktail drink umbrellas on each glass rim or skewer a few across the surface of the drink. The three styles—tiki, luau, and tropical—let you match the umbrella to the theme of your party.

6. Serve. Pour the cooler into clear glasses, add a cucumber slice and a sprig of mint for extra visual appeal, and let guests enjoy the refreshing sip.

Tips for Perfect Presentation

• Keep the umbrellas dry. Store the paper parasols in a sealed container until ready to use; moisture will weaken the sticks.

• Use clear glass. A tall, slender glass showcases the layered colors of cucumber, mint, and the tiny umbrella.

• Layer flavors. For a twist, add a splash of pineapple juice or a few berries before serving.

• Batch prep. The cucumber‑mint base can be made up to 24 hours ahead and kept refrigerated. Add sparkling water and umbrellas right before serving.

Pairing Ideas & Party Themes

For a tiki night, the tiki‑style parasols complement rum‑based cocktails, letting you transition seamlessly from non‑alcoholic to spirited drinks.

FAQ

Can I make a vegan version of this cooler?

Yes. All the ingredients—cucumber, mint, lime, simple syrup (made with sugar and water), and sparkling water—are plant‑based. Just ensure any sweetener you use is vegan.

How long will the mini umbrellas stay fresh?

When kept dry in an airtight container, the paper parasols retain their shape for several weeks. Once they’re placed in a drink, they’re best enjoyed within the hour for optimal appearance.

Is it okay to reuse the umbrella sticks?

For food safety, it’s recommended to use a fresh stick for each drink. The paper can become soggy, and reusing sticks may spread flavors between beverages.
