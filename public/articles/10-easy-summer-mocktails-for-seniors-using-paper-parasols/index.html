Choosing Ingredients for Health-Conscious Refreshments

Many seniors prefer drinks that are lower in refined sugar. You can easily modify any of the recipes above by swapping corn syrup-based mixers for natural alternatives. Use sparkling mineral water instead of soda, and try honey, maple syrup, or a touch of stevia for sweetness.

Fresh herbs like mint, basil, and rosemary aren't just for decoration—they add aromatic depth that makes a non-alcoholic drink feel more complex. When you pair these fresh scents with the visual pop of a paper parasol, the sensory experience is complete, making the drink feel like a treat rather than just a glass of juice.

FAQ

Can I use paper parasols in hot drinks?

It is not recommended. Paper umbrellas are designed for cold beverages and garnishes. The heat from a hot drink can cause the paper to wilt or the colors to bleed into the beverage.

How do I keep my fruit garnishes from sinking?

The best way to keep fruit floating is to use a cocktail pick. By using the picks included in the 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks, you can spear a piece of pineapple or melon and anchor it into a piece of ice or the side of the glass.

What is the best way to store cocktail umbrellas?

Keep them in a cool, dry place in their original packaging. This prevents the delicate paper from absorbing moisture or getting crushed, ensuring they open fully when you're ready to serve your summer mocktails.
