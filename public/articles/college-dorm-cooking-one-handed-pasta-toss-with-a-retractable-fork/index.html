Mastering the One-Handed Toss Technique

The best way to utilize this tool is with a one-handed pasta toss. This technique coats your noodles evenly without needing a separate pasta spoon. Since you likely don't own a dedicated pasta spoon in the dorm, the telescopic fork does the heavy lifting.

  1. Cook and Drain: Boil your pasta in the microwave or kettle and drain it well, leaving just a tiny bit of water in the bottom of the bowl.
  2. Add Fat: Drop in your butter, olive oil, or pesto while the pasta is piping hot.
  3. Extend and Lock: Pull your extendable fork out to about 12-14 inches. You don't need it fully extended yet, but you want the leverage.
  4. The Lift and Drop: Use the fork to lift a section of noodles high out of the bowl. Drop them back in. Repeat this rapidly. The long handle creates a pendulum motion that uses gravity to help separate the strands as the sauce coats them.
  5. The Safety Distance: Once tossed, extend the fork to its full 25.4-inch length if you want to lean back and eat while watching a movie, keeping your greasy hands far away from the screen.
