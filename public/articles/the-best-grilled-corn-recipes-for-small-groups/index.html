2. Mexican Street‑Style Elote Grilled Corn

Elote is a street‑food staple that transforms ordinary corn into a creamy, cheesy treat. This version is designed for small groups, so you won’t waste ingredients or end up with leftover sauce.

Ingredients:

  1. 5 ears of corn, husked
  2. 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  3. 1/2 cup crumbled Cotija cheese (or feta as a substitute)
  4. 1 tsp smoked paprika
  5. 1 jalapeño, finely diced (optional)
  6. Fresh cilantro, chopped
  7. Lime wedges for serving

Spread a thin layer of mayo on each hot ear right after it comes off the grill. Sprinkle the cheese, smoked paprika, and jalapeño evenly. The heat from the corn melts the cheese slightly, creating a luscious coating. Finish with a handful of cilantro and a squeeze of lime. 3. Sweet‑And‑Savory Maple‑Bacon Corn

If you want a crowd‑pleaser that feels a bit indulgent, try this maple‑bacon twist. The salty crunch of bacon pairs beautifully with a drizzle of pure maple syrup.

What you’ll need:

  • 4 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 4 slices of thick‑cut bacon, cooked until crisp and crumbled
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp melted butter
  • Pinch of sea salt

Brush the corn with melted butter and grill until golden brown, about 10 minutes. Drizzle the maple syrup over the hot kernels, then sprinkle the crumbled bacon and a pinch of sea salt. Practical Tips for Grilling Corn in Small Batches

When you’re feeding a small crew, timing and organization are key. Here are a few tricks that keep the process smooth:

  • Prep the corn ahead of time. Remove husks, shuck the silk, and pre‑brush with oil or butter at least 15 minutes before grilling. This reduces grill time and lets you focus on flavor.
  • Use a two‑zone grill. Set one side on medium‑high heat for searing and the other on low for keeping corn warm without overcooking.
  • Rotate consistently. Even cooking prevents hot spots. The Girla Interlocking Corn on the Cob Holders are designed to interlock, so you can stack a few ears together for easy rotation without cluttering the grill.
  • Serve immediately. Corn is at its best when hot. Place each ear on a plate with a holder underneath; the silicone handles stay cool, allowing guests to grab their portions without waiting.

What’s the best way to reheat leftover grilled corn?
