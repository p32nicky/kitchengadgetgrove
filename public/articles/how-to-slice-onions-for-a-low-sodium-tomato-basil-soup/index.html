Step-by-Step: How to Slice Onions Low Sodium Tomato Basil Soup

To get the most flavor out of your onions, follow this method for a clean, consistent cut:

  • Prep the Onion: Cut off the stem end, but leave the root end intact. This keeps the layers from sliding apart while you work.
  • Secure the Vegetable: Insert the prongs of the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide into the top of the onion. This stabilizes the onion and provides a consistent gap for your knife.
  • The Slicing Motion: Use a sharp chef's knife. Slice vertically across the onion, following the guide of the stainless steel prongs.
  • The Fine Dice: Once you have uniform half-moon slices, turn the onions 90 degrees and slice across them to create a consistent, small dice.
  • The Sauté: Cook the onions over medium-low heat in olive oil until translucent. For low-sodium recipes, let them go a bit longer to develop a golden-brown sweetness.
