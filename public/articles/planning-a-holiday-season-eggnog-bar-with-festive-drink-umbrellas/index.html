Must-Have Mix-ins and Toppings

A great eggnog bar is all about the textures and flavors. Since eggnog is rich and creamy, you need elements that provide contrast—think crunch, spice, and a bit of acidity. Arrange these in small ramekins or bowls for a professional look.

  • The Crunch: Toasted pecans, crushed gingersnap cookies, and candy canes.
  • The Spice: Freshly grated nutmeg, cinnamon sticks, and a pinch of sea salt.
  • The Sweet: Mini marshmallows, whipped cream, and a drizzle of salted caramel or chocolate syrup.
  • The Freshness: Maraschino cherries or fresh raspberries to add a pop of color.

FAQ

Can I use drink umbrellas in thick beverages like eggnog?

Yes! While they are traditional for tropical drinks, the picks in the 150-Pack Cocktail Drink Umbrellas & Picks are sturdy enough to pierce through the foam and cream of eggnog, providing a festive visual element.

How do I prevent the eggnog from curdling in a dispenser?

Keep your eggnog chilled until the moment you serve it, or use a slow cooker on the lowest 'warm' setting. Avoid adding highly acidic mixers (like heavy citrus) directly into the main dispenser; instead, let guests add those to their individual glasses.

What are the best alternatives to traditional eggnog for the bar?

If some guests aren't fans of eggnog, offer a white hot chocolate or a creamy coconut milk punch. Both pair perfectly with the same toppings and the festive drink umbrellas, ensuring everyone has a decorated drink in their hand.
