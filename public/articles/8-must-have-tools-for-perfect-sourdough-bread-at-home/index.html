Without a clean cut, the bread will burst randomly, often resulting in an unattractive "blowout" on the side.

Tips for Long-Term Sourdough Success

The biggest hurdle for new bakers is consistency. Your starter is a living organism, and it reacts to the temperature of your kitchen. If your kitchen is cold, your rise will take longer. If it's hot, your dough might over-proof and flatten out.

Keep a simple log of your bake times and temperatures. When you combine a disciplined routine with the right sourdough bread tools, you stop guessing and start baking with confidence. Remember to keep your wooden tools dry and clean to avoid any unwanted mold growth in your starter kit.

FAQ

Do I really need a Danish whisk, or can I use my hands?

You can absolutely mix sourdough by hand, but a Danish whisk significantly reduces the effort and time required to get a smooth, cohesive dough. It is especially helpful for those who struggle with the initial "shaggy mass" stage of mixing.

Why is my bread not expanding even though I scored it?

This is usually a sign of under-proofing or a weak starter. If the dough hasn't fermented enough, it won't have the gas pressure needed to push through the score marks. Ensure your starter is doubling in size within 4-6 hours before mixing your dough.

How do I clean and store my wooden sourdough tools?

Avoid putting wooden tools like the spurtle or dough stick in the dishwasher, as the heat can warp the wood. Wash them by hand with mild soap and dry them immediately. Store them in a dry area to maintain the integrity of the wood.
