Common Sourdough Mistakes to Avoid in a Small Kitchen

One of the most common mistakes that beginners make when baking sourdough is over-proofing their dough. This can cause the bread to collapse or become misshapen. Another mistake is under-proofing the dough, which can result in a dense or sour-tasting loaf. To avoid these mistakes, make sure to keep an eye on your dough's progress, and be patient when waiting for it to rise.

Here are a few more common mistakes to avoid when baking sourdough in a small kitchen:

  • Using too much flour, which can cause the dough to become tough or dense.
  • Not using a sufficient amount of salt, which can cause the bread to be bland or flat.
  • Not storing your sourdough starter properly, which can cause it to die off or become unhealthy.
