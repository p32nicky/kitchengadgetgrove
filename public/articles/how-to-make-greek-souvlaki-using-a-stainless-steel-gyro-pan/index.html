Marinating the Meat: Key to Flavorful Souvlaki

A good marinade is essential for Greek souvlaki. Use the Gyro Pan’s included skewer to prepare the meat for even marination and cooking:

  • Meat: Opt for 2–2.5 pounds of lamb shoulder or chicken, trimmed of excess fat and cut into 1-inch cubes.
  • Marinade: Whisk together 1/3 cup olive oil, 2 cloves minced garlic, 1 tsp each of oregano and paprika, 1 tbsp lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  • Skewer Prep: Thread meat evenly onto the gyro pan’s vertical skewer, leaving space for the pan to rotate. Brush marinade onto the meat and let it rest for at least 1 hour.

Step-by-Step Guide to Using the Gyro Pan for Souvlaki

  1. Preheat Oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C) and place the rack at upper third height.
  2. Assemble the Pan: Insert the skewered meat into the Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven, ensuring the base is stable. Place the pan on a baking sheet for easy cleanup.
  3. Cook: Place the pan in the oven and cook for 45–60 minutes. Rotate the skewer manually every 15 minutes for even browning.
  4. Shave and Serve: Once the meat is golden and tender, turn off the oven. Let it rest for 10 minutes, then use a sharp knife or carving fork to slice thin slices, starting from the top.

Serving Suggestions for Authentic Greek Flavors

Slice the cooked meat and serve it in warm pita pockets with chopped tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, tzatziki, and a squeeze of lemon. Leftover meat pairs well with grilled vegetables or over rice for souvlaki bowls. The Gyro Pan’s non-stick surface makes cleanup a breeze—just let it cool and wipe with soapy water.

FAQ

Can I use the Gyro Pan for other dishes?

Absolutely! The Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven is ideal for making al pastor tacos, shawarma, kebabs, and even roasted vegetables. Just adjust marination and cooking times based on your recipe.

How do I prevent the meat from drying out?

Don’t overcook the meat. Check for tender doneness—when you poke the meat with a fork, it should feel soft but not falling apart. Cover the pan loosely with foil during the last 10 minutes of cooking if browning is too fast.

Is the Gyro Pan safe for all oven types?

Yes, the stainless steel construction is safe for conventional and convection ovens. Avoid using it under a broiler, as direct high heat can warp the pan.
