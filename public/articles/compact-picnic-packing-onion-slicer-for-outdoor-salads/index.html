Step‑by‑Step: Using Your Picnic Onion Slicer for a Perfect Salad

1. Set up your workspace. Lay out a clean cutting board or a sturdy plate. If you’re on a blanket, a small, flat tray works well.

2. Insert the onion. Place the onion’s root end into the prongs so the layers are exposed. The holder keeps the onion from rolling while you slice.

3. Adjust the guide. Align the cutting guide with the thickness you desire – typically ¼ inch for salads. The guide’s edge will act as a stop for your knife, ensuring uniform slices.

4. Slice confidently. Using a sharp chef’s knife, glide the blade along the guide. The onion stays steady, and you get even rings without wobbling.

5. Transfer to the bowl. Slide the sliced rings onto your salad bowl, add other vegetables, a splash of vinaigrette, and you’re ready to serve.
