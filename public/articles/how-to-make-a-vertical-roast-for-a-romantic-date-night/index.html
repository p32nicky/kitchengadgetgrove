How to Make a Vertical Roast for Date Night

Follow these steps to impress your partner with minimal effort and maximum flavor:

  1. Choose the right cut: Opt for a primal cut like a pork loin, beef tenderloin, or leg of lamb—ideally 2–3 pounds.
  2. Prep the Gyro Pan: Secure the skewer into the Gyro Pan’s base. Ensure the skewer is long enough to hold the meat with at least 2 inches of space on each end.
  3. Season aggressively: Rub the meat with olive oil, then coat with a blend of spices. Try Mediterranean herbs (oregano, thyme, garlic) or Middle Eastern blends (sumac, cumin, paprika).
  4. Skewer and roast: Slide the meat onto the skewer, securing it with the pan’s clamps. Place in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C). Roast for 25–35 minutes, depending on the cut.
  5. Rest and serve: Let the roast rest for 10 minutes. Slice against the grain for tender pieces.

FAQ

Can I use the Gyro Pan for other meats besides a roast?

Absolutely. It works great for chickens, racks of ribs, and even large kebabs. Adjust cook times based on the cut.

How do I clean the Gyro Pan?

Let it cool, then hand-wash with warm soapy water. Avoid harsh scrubbing to preserve the stainless steel finish.

What if my roast isn’t the same thickness on both ends?

Trim excess fat or meat to balance the skewer. This ensures even rotation and prevents uneven cooking.
