Steps to Convert Store Bought Starter to Whisk Method

  1. Feed Your Starter: Start with a ripe, bubbly store-bought starter. Discard half, then feed it with equal parts flour and water by weight (e.g., 50g starter + 50g flour + 50g water).
  2. Whisk Gently: Use the Danish dough whisk to mix until no dry flour remains. The whisk’s motion creates a silky, aerated batter, which kickstarts fermentation.
  3. Rest and Repeat: Let the starter rest for 2–4 hours at room temperature, then repeat the feeding process. After 3–5 feedings, your starter should be robust enough for baking.

During this process, the included dough scraper from the Sourdough Whisk Set helps you cleanly remove starter from containers, preventing contamination and waste.

Tips for Success with Store-Bought Starters

Transitioning a commercial starter to the whisk method takes patience. Here’s how to optimize the process:

  • Use room-temperature water (around 70°F) for consistent fermentation.
  • Store your starter in a slightly breathable container to prevent excess moisture.
  • Monitor activity: A healthy starter should double in size within 4–6 hours after feeding.

FAQ

Can I use the Danish whisk for other baking tasks?

Absolutely! The whisk works wonders for mixing batters, folding egg whites, or even aerating pizza dough. Its versatility makes it a staple in any baker’s arsenal.

How often should I feed my starter during the transition?

Aim for 2–3 feedings daily for the first week. Once your starter becomes active, you can switch to daily feedings or refrigerate it for weekly maintenance.
