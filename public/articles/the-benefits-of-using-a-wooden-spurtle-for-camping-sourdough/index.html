Practical Tips for Using the Wooden Spurtle on Your Next Trip

Even the best tool can feel underwhelming if you don’t use it right. Below are some field‑tested tips that keep your starter thriving:

  • Season the Wood: Before your first hike, rub the spurtle with a thin layer of food‑grade mineral oil. This prevents the wood from absorbing excess moisture and extends its lifespan.
  • Stir in a Circular Motion: Use a gentle clockwise swirl to incorporate fresh flour and water evenly. Avoid vigorous back‑and‑forth motions that can break the gluten network.
  • Scrape the Jar Walls: After each feed, run the spurtle along the interior of the jar to dislodge any dried starter. This keeps the culture active and reduces the chance of mold.
  • Dry After Use: Pat the spurtle dry with a clean towel before packing it away. Moisture left in the wood can encourage bacterial growth.

Following these simple steps ensures the wooden spurtle remains a reliable partner for weeks of camping sourdough adventures.

Integrating the Spurtle into a Full Camp Baking Workflow

Once your starter is fed and active, it’s time to turn it into dough. The wooden spurtle continues to shine during the mixing stage:

1. Combine starter, water, and flour in a large mixing bowl. Use the spurtle to fold the ingredients together until no dry spots remain.

3. Add salt and any flavorings. The spurtle’s thin end is perfect for incorporating salt evenly without over‑mixing.

4. Bulk ferment. During the first hour, give the dough a series of stretch‑and‑folds using the spurtle to lift and turn the dough. Its smooth surface won’t tear the gluten strands.

5. Shape and bake. After the final rise, use the spurtle to scrape any remaining dough off the bench and onto your baking surface.

How do I clean the spurtle after a muddy hike?
