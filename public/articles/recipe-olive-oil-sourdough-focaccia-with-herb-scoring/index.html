  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup active sourdough starter
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

The Magic Happens with Sourdough Starter

Now that we have our ingredients and tools in order, let's talk about the sourdough starter. This is the real hero of this recipe, as it provides the leavening power and tangy flavor that makes olive oil sourdough focaccia truly special. If you're new to sourdough baking, don't worry – the Sourdough Whisk Set comes with a complete starter kit, making it easy to get started. To feed your starter, simply mix 1/4 cup of flour with 1/4 cup of water, and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours before using it in the recipe.

Preparing the Dough

Once your starter is ready, it's time to mix the dough. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, sourdough starter, olive oil, salt, garlic, and flour. Mix the dough using your Danish dough whisk until it comes together in a shaggy mass. Don't overmix – you want to maintain that delicate balance of gluten and yeast.

Shaping and Proofing the Dough

After mixing the dough, transfer it to a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. Form the dough into a ball and let it proof in a lightly oiled bowl, covered with plastic wrap or a damp towel, for 2-3 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Scoring and Baking the Focaccia

Before baking, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Using your bread scoring lame, create a series of deep, parallel scores on the surface of the dough, being careful not to cut too deeply. If using fresh herbs, sprinkle them over the dough in a zig-zag pattern. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Enjoy Your Olive Oil Sourdough Focaccia!

And that's it – your olive oil sourdough focaccia is ready to devour! Slice it up and serve it with your favorite dipping sauce, or use it as the base for a delicious sandwich. Whatever you choose, I'm sure you'll be hooked on this recipe from the very first bite.

FAQ

Q: Can I make this recipe without a sourdough starter?

A: While it's technically possible to make this recipe without a sourdough starter, it won't have the same level of flavor and texture. If you're new to sourdough baking, I highly recommend investing in a starter kit or using a store-bought starter to get started.

Q: Can I customize the herb scores to fit my personal style?

A: Absolutely! The great thing about herb scoring is that you can customize it to fit your personal style. Experiment with different herbs, patterns, and colors to create a look that's uniquely yours.

Q: Can I store leftover focaccia for later?

A: Yes, you can store leftover focaccia at room temperature for up to 2 days, or freeze it for up to 2 months. Simply wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil and reheat it in the oven or toaster when you're ready to enjoy it.
