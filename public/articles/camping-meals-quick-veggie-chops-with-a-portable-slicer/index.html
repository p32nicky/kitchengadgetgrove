Three Simple Recipes Using Quick Veggie Chops

These meals showcase the power of uniform slices, which cook evenly and look appealing on a campsite plate.

1. Campfire Veggie Fajita Packets

Ingredients: 1 bell pepper, 1 small onion, 1 zucchini, 1 tbsp olive oil, fajita seasoning, tortillas.

  1. Place the onion in the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide and slice ¼‑inch rings.
  2. Use the same holder for the bell pepper and zucchini, cutting them into thin strips.
  3. Toss the veggies with oil and seasoning in a silicone bag; shake to coat.
  4. Spread the mix onto a piece of heavy‑duty foil, fold into a packet, and grill over the campfire for 8‑10 minutes.
  5. Warm tortillas on the grill, fill with the cooked veggies, and enjoy.

2. Quick Tomato & Basil Pasta

Ingredients: 8 oz pasta, 2 ripe tomatoes, fresh basil, 1 garlic clove, 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper.

While the pasta boils, use the slicer to cut tomatoes into ¼‑inch rounds. Mince the garlic with a small knife, then toss the tomatoes, garlic, and basil in a pan with oil. After the pasta is al dente, combine everything for a fresh, one‑pot meal.

3. Sweet Potato & Onion Hash

Ingredients: 1 medium sweet potato, 1 onion, 1 tbsp butter, smoked paprika, salt.

Slice the sweet potato and onion using the holder’s prongs for even pieces that brown quickly. Cook in a skillet over a camp stove, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and caramelized. This hash pairs well with scrambled eggs or a fried egg for a protein boost.

Tips for Maintaining Uniform Slices in the Wild

Even the best slicer can wobble on a rocky campsite table. Here are a few tricks to keep your chops consistent:

  • Use a stable surface. A silicone cutting board or a flat rock works better than a folding table leg.
  • Dry the produce first. Moisture can cause the onion holder’s prongs to slip.
  • Apply gentle, even pressure. Let the stainless‑steel guide do the work; you don’t need to force the blade.
  • Clean the slicer after each use. A quick rinse with water and a wipe dry prevents food buildup that could affect slicing performance.
