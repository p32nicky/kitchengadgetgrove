Why Consistent Onion Slices Matter in a One‑Pan Autumn Stew

Stews rely on slow, even heat distribution. If some onion pieces are thick while others are paper‑thin, the thicker bits stay firm while the thin ones dissolve into a sweet broth. The result is uneven mouthfeel and a stew that looks patched together. Consistent slices ensure every bite has the same sweet‑savory balance and that the stew thickens uniformly as the onions release their natural sugars.

Choosing the Right Cut for Autumn Vegetables

For a one pan autumn stew, aim for ½‑inch thick half‑moon slices. This size is sturdy enough to hold its shape during the long simmer, yet thin enough to release flavor quickly. Here’s how to achieve that cut every time:

  1. Trim the root end. Cut off the papery base; the onion will sit flat.
  2. Halve the onion longitudinally. Place the cut side down for stability.
  3. Insert the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide. The stainless‑steel prongs grip the halves securely, keeping them from rolling.
  4. Slice with a rocking motion. Use a chef’s knife and let the guide’s groove steer the blade into even ½‑inch arcs.
  5. Collect the slices. The guide’s built‑in catcher slides the pieces onto your cutting board without extra handling.

When you’re ready to add carrots, potatoes, and squash, the same guide can hold those veggies steady for uniform cuts, which is especially handy if you prefer a rustic, bite‑size dice.

Step‑by‑Step: Building the One‑Pan Autumn Stew

With your onions sliced, the rest of the stew comes together quickly:

  • Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large, heavy‑bottomed skillet over medium heat.
  • Sauté the onion slices for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they turn translucent and begin to caramelize.
  • Add diced carrots, potatoes, and cubed butternut squash. Cook for another 5 minutes, letting the edges brown.
  • Season with salt, pepper, a pinch of smoked paprika, and a sprig of fresh thyme.
  • Pour in 2 cups low‑sodium chicken or vegetable broth. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  • Cover and simmer for 25–30 minutes, or until the vegetables are fork‑tender.
  • Finish with a splash of apple cider and a handful of chopped kale for extra autumn color.

Because the onions were cut uniformly, they meld into the broth at the same rate as the other root vegetables, giving you a stew with consistent texture from the first spoonful to the last.
