Consistency is king in sourdough. A digital kitchen scale lets you weigh flour and water to the gram, eliminating the guesswork that can throw off the starter’s hydration. Glass jars with wide mouths make feeding a breeze, but they need a secure seal to prevent contamination. I prefer jars with screw‑on lids and a silicone gasket; they keep out dust while still allowing gases to escape via a small vent. Here’s a quick checklist to keep your starter on track:

  1. Feed with equal parts flour and water by weight.
  2. Stir with the wooden spurtle or whisk to incorporate air.
  3. Scrape the jar sides to prevent dry crust formation.
  4. Cover with an airtight lid that allows gas release.
  5. Check activity daily using the extendable fork.
  6. Store in a cool, stable temperature environment.
  7. Enjoy the results with a garnish of cocktail umbrellas for extra fun.

FAQ

Can I use a metal spoon instead of a wooden spurtle?

Yes, but metal can react with the acidic environment of a sourdough starter and may scratch glass jars. A wooden spurtle is non‑reactive and gentle on the culture, making it the preferred choice.

How often should I refresh the starter with new flour?

Most bakers feed their starter every 12‑24 hours, depending on temperature. If you notice a layer of liquid (hooch) on top or a sluggish rise, it’s time for a fresh feed.

Do the cocktail umbrellas affect the flavor of my drinks?

No. The mini paper parasols are purely decorative and dissolve harmlessly if left in a liquid, leaving no taste impact.
