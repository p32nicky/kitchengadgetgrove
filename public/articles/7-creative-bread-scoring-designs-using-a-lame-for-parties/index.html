7 Scoring Patterns to Impress Party Guests

Armed with your bread scoring lame, try these designs:

  1. Classic Cross: A simple + or × for rustic loaves; reliable and elegant.
  2. Clover Leaf: Three overlapping half-circles for a whimsical touch.
  3. Diagonal Grid: Crisscross slashes at 45° angles for a modern geometric look.
  4. Celtic Knot: Interwoven lines that mimic traditional knotwork; practice on parchment first.
  5. Swiss Checkerboard: Small squares created by perpendicular cuts; perfect for party rounds.
  6. Floral Vine: Curved, connected teardrops resembling flower stems.
  7. Party Popper: A deep central cut that flares outward, creating a "baking balloon" effect.

For best results, score just before baking. A lame glides through proofed dough effortlessly, avoiding the crushing that might happen with a knife.

Tips for Scoring Success

1. Keep your lame blade at a 20–30° angle for clean, consistent cuts.
2. Lightly dust dough with flour to prevent the scoring tool from sticking.
3. Practice on a test batch before scoring your final loaf.
4. For elaborate patterns, sketch the design on parchment first.

FAQ

Can I use a regular knife instead of a lame?

A lame’s thin, flexible blade is designed for quick, shallow cuts without squishing the dough. A knife tends to drag or compress the surface, leading to uneven expansion.

How do I maintain my bread scoring lame?

Hand-wash the blade immediately after use to prevent rust. For the Sourdough Whisk Set’s stainless lame, a quick wipe with a dry cloth stores it for next time.

What if my scoring pattern doesn’t look perfect?

Imperfection is part of the charm! Sourdough baking celebrates organic textures. Just ensure the cuts are consistent for even rising.
