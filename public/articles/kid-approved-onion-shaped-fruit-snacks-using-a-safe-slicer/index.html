Step‑by‑Step: Turning Apples into Onion‑Shaped Snacks

1. Wash and dry the fruit. A clean surface ensures the slicer sits flat.

2. Place the fruit on the cutting board. If it’s a round fruit, position it so the stem points toward you.

3. Insert the Onion Holder prongs. Align the prongs across the fruit’s center; they should sit snugly but not crush the flesh.

4. Slide the blade. Gently push the blade across the fruit. The prongs hold it steady, so you get a straight, even slice.

5. Repeat. Flip the fruit and slice again to create a symmetrical onion shape. The result is a crisp, eye‑catching snack that kids can enjoy without a knife.

Recipe Ideas: From Apples to Watermelon

  • Apple Onion Rings: Slice apples into rings, then use the slicer to cut a thin wedge from each ring, forming a ring with a small onion‑like center.
  • Peach Onion Boats: Halve a peach, remove the pit, and slice the flesh into thin strips. The Onion Holder will keep the peach halves steady as you cut the strips into onion shapes.
  • Watermelon Onion Pockets: Cut a thick slice of watermelon, use the slicer to make a shallow oval, then cut a smaller oval inside to create a pocket for a burst of berry filling.
  • Banana Onion Fingers: Slice a banana lengthwise, then use the slicer to create thin, onion‑shaped strips perfect for dipping in yogurt.

Safety Tips for Parents and Kids

• Use a firm, flat board. A wobbling surface can throw off the slicer's alignment.

• Keep the blade cool. If you’re slicing multiple fruits, give the blade a minute to rest between cuts to avoid overheating.

• Supervise closely. Even with a safe slicer, children should be watched while they learn to use kitchen tools.

• Store the slicer properly. Clean the stainless steel prongs after each use and dry them to prevent rust.
