Selecting the Right Fruit‑Infused Base

Start with a clean, cold water base and choose fruits that release flavor quickly. Citrus, berries, and melons are all excellent because they don’t overpower the water. Here are three reliable foundations:

  • Classic Citrus – thinly sliced lemon, lime, and orange. Add a few sprigs of fresh mint for brightness.
  • Berry Medley – strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries. Mash them lightly to release juices.
  • Tropical Twist – pineapple chunks, mango cubes, and kiwi slices. A handful of coconut water boosts the island vibe.

For each base, use about one cup of fruit per gallon of water and let it steep for at least 30 minutes. Longer infusion (2‑4 hours) yields a richer flavor, but keep the fruit out of the refrigerator if you plan to serve within a few hours to avoid cloudiness.

Practical Tips for a Seamless Presentation

Even the best flavor combos can fall flat if the presentation is sloppy. Follow these simple steps to keep everything tidy:

  • Prep the straws early. Open the 100‑Pack Umbrella Straws and lay them out on a clean surface. This prevents the delicate parasol from getting crushed.
  • Insert the straw just before serving. Push the straw through the fruit slice at the rim of the glass. The fruit acts as a natural seal, keeping the parasol upright.
  • Use a tall, clear pitcher. A clear vessel showcases the color of the infused water and makes it easy for guests to see the fruit floating inside.
  • Keep the water chilled. A small ice bucket beside the pitcher ensures the water stays cold without diluting the flavor.

These habits keep the mini parasols upright and the fruit‑infused water vibrant, even for longer events.
