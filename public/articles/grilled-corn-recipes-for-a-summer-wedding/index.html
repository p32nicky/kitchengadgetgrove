1. Classic Grilled Corn with Herb Butter

Start with the basics: sweet corn on the cob, a buttery herb spread, and a sprinkling of sea salt. The herb butter melts into the kernels, giving a creamy texture that pairs perfectly with the smoky char.

  • Ingredients: 12 ears of corn, ½ cup melted butter, 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley, 1 tsp minced garlic, sea salt.
  • Prep: Brush each ear with butter, sprinkle with herbs, and grill over medium‑high heat for 10–12 minutes, turning every 2–3 minutes.
  • Serve: Drizzle with a little extra butter, a squeeze of lime, and a pinch of chili powder for a touch of heat.

2. Mexican‑Inspired Char‑Grilled Corn with Cotija

Give your guests a fiesta‑flavored option. After grilling, rub the corn with lime juice, then sprinkle with crumbled Cotija cheese, chopped cilantro, and a dash of smoked paprika.

This dish works great as a side or a topping for a corn and avocado salad on your buffet table.

3. Sweet & Smoky Corn with Brown Sugar & Chipotle

For a sweet‑and‑spicy twist, coat the corn with a mixture of brown sugar, chipotle powder, and a touch of honey before grilling. The sugar caramelizes on the grill, creating a glossy glaze.

Finish with a sprinkle of fresh lime zest and a few microgreens for color.

4. Corn with Lemon‑Garlic Aioli

Make a quick aioli by blending ½ cup mayo, 1 minced garlic clove, juice of one lemon, and a pinch of salt. Spread this creamy sauce over grilled corn and garnish with chives.

This option is great for guests who prefer a lighter, tangy flavor profile.

Serving Tips for a Wedding Reception

  1. Station Setup: Place a small station with a grill, holders, and a bowl of butter or aioli. Let guests help themselves; it’s a fun, interactive element.
  2. Presentation: Arrange grilled corn on a platter with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of sea salt. Add a garnish of fresh herbs or edible flowers for a pop of color.
  3. Temperature Control: Keep the corn warm by covering the platter with foil or a low‑heat warming tray until serving time.
  4. Allergy Awareness: Offer a dairy‑free butter alternative (like olive oil or coconut butter) for guests with lactose intolerance.

FAQ

How long does grilled corn stay fresh at a wedding?

Grilled corn remains fresh for about 1–2 hours if kept warm. Use a warming tray or keep it in a covered tin to maintain moisture.

Can I grill corn ahead of time and reheat it?

Yes. Grill the corn, cool it, and refrigerate. Reheat in a skillet or microwave for 30–45 seconds, then finish with a quick brush of butter.
