Why a Wooden Spurtle Matters for Your Sourdough Starter

When you’re just getting into sourdough, the right tools can make all the difference. A wooden spurtle—long, skinny, and flexible—helps you mix, scrape, and monitor your starter without damaging delicate dough or the jar. It’s gentle on the microbes, keeps the starter from sticking, and gives you a tactile feel for the right consistency.

How to Use a Wooden Spurtle to Feed Your Starter

1. Measure the flour and water you’ll add—usually a 1:1 ratio by weight. 2. Pour the mixture into the jar and use the skinny spurtle to stir until fully incorporated. 3. Scrape the sides with the same tool to lift any bits that might have stuck. 4. Cover loosely and leave the starter at room temperature.

The wooden material keeps the temperature steady, and its smooth surface prevents the starter from sticking to the utensil. This means you can gauge the dough’s rise and feel the texture without the frustration of a plastic or metal whisk that can leave residues.

Cleaning and Caring for Your Wooden Spurtle

Wood is naturally porous, so you’ll want to keep it clean to avoid bacterial buildup. After each use, rinse the spurtle with warm water and let it dry completely before storing. Avoid soaking it in hot water or putting it in the dishwasher; a quick wipe and air‑dry cycle are enough. Over time, a light coat of food‑safe mineral oil will keep the wood from cracking and maintain its smoothness.

Integrating a Spurtle Into Your Routine

Start by setting a regular feeding schedule—every 12 to 24 hours, depending on temperature. Use the wooden spurtle to mix and scrape each time. Over a few weeks, you’ll notice the starter’s texture becoming more elastic and the rise more predictable. The spurtle’s tactile feedback helps you judge when the starter is ready for baking versus when it needs more feedings.

FAQ

Can I use a wooden spurtle for other doughs?

Absolutely. Its gentle action works well with pizza dough, focaccia, and even laminated doughs like croissants.

Is the wooden spurtle dishwasher safe?

No. Wood can warp or crack. Hand wash with warm water and dry immediately.

How often should I oil the wooden spurtle?

A light oiling every few months is sufficient. Use a food‑safe mineral oil and apply a thin coat, then wipe off excess.
