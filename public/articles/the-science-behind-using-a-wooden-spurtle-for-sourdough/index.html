Why Material Matters: Wood vs. Metal

Wood is a natural insulator. Unlike metal, which conducts heat quickly, a wooden spurtle keeps the starter at a stable temperature while you mix. This helps maintain the ideal fermentation range (75‑78°F) and prevents shock to the wild yeast. Additionally, wood is non‑reactive, so it won’t leach any metallic taste into the starter.

The slender, tapered profile of a Danish‑style dough stick creates a gentle, yet thorough, agitation. Gluten Development Made Simple

When you fold and stir the starter with a wooden spurtle, you’re actually aligning protein strands. The elongated shape allows you to:

  1. Lift and fold the dough rather than over‑mixing, which preserves the delicate balance of wild yeast.
  2. Create tension in the starter, encouraging better gas retention during bulk fermentation.
  3. Maintain a low‑shear environment that reduces the risk of breaking down the starter’s natural microbial community.

All of these factors translate into a more vigorous rise and a richer flavor profile in the final loaf.

Yes. Wood will not react with metal, and the spurtle’s non‑scratch surface protects the bowl’s coating.
