How to Clean Your Gyro Pan Like a Pro

After every use, follow these steps to keep your stainless steel vertical skewer pan in top shape:

  1. Let it cool completely before cleaning to avoid warping the metal or damaging the skewer.
  2. Rinse the pan with warm water to remove loose grease and meat bits. Use a soft sponge or cloth — never steel wool, which can scratch stainless steel.
  3. Mix dish soap with warm water and scrub the pan’s exterior and the vertical skewer. For stubborn residue, let the soapy water sit for 5-10 minutes first.
  4. Use a non-abrasive scrubber (like a silicone pad) on the skewer’s threading to avoid stripping it.
  5. Rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a towel. Let air dry completely before storing to prevent water spots.

Why Proper Cleaning Matters for Your Gyro Pan

The Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven is designed for high-fat cooking, which means grease can cake on quickly. If left uncleaned, this buildup can smoke during your next use or alter your food’s flavor. Worse, trapped moisture from improper drying can lead to rust on the stainless steel — a costly repair.

Deep Cleaning for Tough Buildup

For stuck-on bits that won’t budge:

  • Make a paste of baking soda and water and apply it to the affected areas. Let sit for an hour before scrubbing.
  • For heavy grease, boil a solution of vinegar and water (1:1) in the pan, then let it soak before scrubbing.
  • Never use harsh chemicals like bleach — they’ll dull your pan’s finish.

Long-Term Gyro Pan Maintenance

Between deep cleans, these habits will keep your vertical skewer pan working smoothly:

  • Oil the skewer occasionally with food-safe mineral oil to prevent sticking and slow rust.
  • Store your Vertical Gyro Pan with Skewer for Oven in a dry place. If stacking, use a soft cloth between pans to avoid scratches.
  • Check the skewer’s tightness regularly — if it’s wobbling in the base, tighten it with an adjustable wrench (not pliers).

FAQ

Can I put my gyro pan in the dishwasher?

No. Stainless steel can warp in dishwashers, and the high heat may loosen the skewer. Handwashing is always best.

How do I remove stuck-on grease?

Try a baking soda paste first. For extreme cases, simmer vinegar and water in the pan, then let it cool before scrubbing.

Is it okay to use metal scrubbers on my stainless steel gyro pan?

Avoid them. Opt for silicone scrubbers or soft sponges to preserve the finish and prevent micro-scratches.
