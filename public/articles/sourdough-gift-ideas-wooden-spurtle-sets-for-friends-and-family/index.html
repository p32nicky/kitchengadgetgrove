How to Use a Wooden Spurtle in Every Stage of Sourdough

Feeding the starter: Scoop the starter into a clean jar, add equal parts flour and water, then use the skinny spurtle to fold the mixture. The tapered tip reaches the bottom, ensuring no dry flour remains.

Scraping jars clean: After a feeding, you’ll often see a thin layer of starter clinging to the glass. The spurtle’s smooth edge lifts it effortlessly, eliminating waste and keeping the jar ready for the next cycle.

Mixing small batches of dough: For a quick 1‑cup dough, the Danish‑style stick can whisk flour, water, and starter together without over‑developing gluten. Its flat surface creates a gentle vortex that incorporates ingredients evenly.

Transferring starter to a proofing basket: When you’re ready to bake, use the spurtle to lift the starter from the jar and gently place it into a banneton. The wood’s non‑stick nature prevents the starter from tearing.

Care Tips to Keep Your Wooden Spurtle Looking New

Wood requires a little TLC, but the payoff is a tool that lasts for years. Hand‑wash the spurtle with warm, soapy water right after use, then dry it immediately to avoid swelling. Once a month, rub a thin layer of food‑grade mineral oil into the wood; this restores moisture and keeps the surface smooth. Avoid soaking the spurtle or putting it in the dishwasher, as prolonged exposure to water can cause cracks.
