How to Make a Watermelon Slush (3 Ways)

Start with fresh, seeded watermelon cubes. For the best texture, pre-freeze them—here’s how:

  1. Chop watermelon into 1-inch cubes and freeze for 2–3 hours until firm.
  2. Blend with a splash of water or juice until slushy. For extra flavor, add lime juice, mint, or a splash of coconut water.
  3. Strain for a smoother texture or leave it chunky for a tiki-style slush.

For a creamy version, blend with frozen yogurt or a splash of milk. Need a boost? Add a shot of rum or tequila for a grown-up twist.
