Hosting a BBQ means more than just fire and meat. The right bbq tools and equipment make the difference between a so-so cookout and a culinary masterpiece. From flipping burgers to serving corn on the cob, having the right gear ensures everything runs smoothly—and mess-free. Below are 10 essential tools every outdoor chef should own, including a standout solution for serving corn safely.

Key BBQ Tools for Grilling Success

Start with the basics: a sturdy pair of long-handled tongs, a heavy-duty spatula, and a grill brush to clean grates between batches. These tools let you maneuver food without sticking or breaking and keep your grill in top shape. A digital meat thermometer is also non-negotiable for perfect doneness, whether you’re cooking burgers, chicken, or brisket.

Serving is just as important as cooking. Add these to your arsenal:

  • Grill-safe serving trays for direct-to-table transfers.
  • Citrus juicers and zesters for fresh garnishes.
  • Fire-resistant gloves for handling hot tools.

Pro Tips for Using Your BBQ Tools and Equipment

Preheat your grill fully before cooking, and use a two-zone fire for indirect heat. For corn, wrap ears in foil with a pat of butter and a pinch of salt before placing them in the Girla holders. Flip occasionally for even charring. Always clean tools immediately after use—most, like the corn holders, are dishwasher-safe for easy maintenance.
