Setting Up Your DIY Sourdough Bread-Making Station

Start with a clean, flat workspace—your kitchen table, a foldable camping table, or even a shaded spot in the yard. You’ll need a large mixing bowl, a bench scraper for knocking back dough, and a proving basket (or a bowl lined with a towel) to hold your shaped loaves. A Dutch oven works wonders for oven spring, but if you’re baking on-site at the grill, consider a portable cast-iron skillet for crusty, hearth-style results.

Crafting the Perfect Loaf for Your BBQ

Start with a simple hydration level (65-70%) to ease shaping, and use a digital scale for accuracy. The Danish whisk’s sturdy wires handle thick batters and sticky doughs without bending. Once shaped, the dough scraper helps transfer loaves to parchment paper or a baking vessel. Just before baking, use the lame to score the dough—angle the blade for baguette-style slits or make a single cross for rustic loaves. Freshly baked sourdough pairs perfectly with grilled meats, cheese boards, or as a mop for BBQ sauces.

Serving and Pairing Tips for a Backyard Feast

Time your bake to finish just as the grill cools down. Serve warm sourdough with a drizzle of olive oil or soft butter for a crowd-pleasing touch. For a complete BBQ menu, try these combos:

  1. Tomato basil sourdough with smoked turkey
  2. Garlic herb loaf with pulled pork sandwiches
  3. Seeded whole-grain bread for charcuterie spreads

A portable bungee cord or collapsible oven mitts help move hot Dutch ovens safely, while a small cooling rack keeps loaves from getting soggy on the picnic table.
