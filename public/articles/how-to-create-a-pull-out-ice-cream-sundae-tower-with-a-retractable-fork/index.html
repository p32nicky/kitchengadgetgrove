Building Your Tower: A Step-by-Step Guide

Creating the tower requires the right vessel. Avoid wide, shallow bowls. Instead, look for a high-ball glass, a narrow trifle dish, or even a specially tiered serving stand. The narrower the base, the easier it is to achieve height without using a gallon of ice cream.

  1. The Anchor: Start with a dense, firm ice cream at the bottom. Vanilla bean or a dense gelato works best because it won't melt instantly under the weight of the toppings.
  2. The Structural Walls: Line the inside of your glass with vertical wafer cookies or long sticks of pirouette. These act as rebar, holding the soft cream in place.
  3. The filling: Fill the gaps between the cookies with scoops of contrasting flavors. Chocolate and peanut butter are excellent choices here.
  4. The Crunch Layer: Add a distinct layer of crushed nuts, sprinkles, or cookie crumbs in the middle. This is where your extendable fork comes in handy—you can stab this layer specifically without disturbing the ice cream below it.
  5. The Crown: Top with whipped cream, but pipe it high. Place the cherry on the very peak.

Mastering the Retractable Technique

Eating the tower requires a different technique than a standard bowl. Cleaning and Storage

Because these are stainless steel, cleanup is a breeze. You do want to be careful with the telescoping mechanism. The inside can sometimes trap moisture or sticky syrup if you aren't careful. I recommend extending the fork fully after washing it to let it air dry completely before collapsing it back down for storage. Store them in a drawer where the handle won't get bumped, ensuring the locking mechanism stays tight.
