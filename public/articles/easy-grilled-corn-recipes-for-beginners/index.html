Easy Grilled Corn Recipe #1: Classic Charred Corn

This method lets the natural sugars do the work. You’ll need:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn, husks removed
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • Salt to taste

1. Preheat your grill to medium‑high (about 400°F).
2. Using the Girla Corn Holders, slide each ear into a holder so you can turn it safely.
3. Brush the corn lightly with melted butter and sprinkle with salt.
4. Place the corn directly on the grill grates. Cook 10‑12 minutes, turning every 2‑3 minutes until the kernels are lightly charred and tender.
5. Remove, give a final sprinkle of sea salt, and serve immediately.

Easy Grilled Corn Recipe #2: Mexican Street‑Style Elote

If you crave a flavor punch, try this street‑corn twist. Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup crumbled Cotija cheese
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

1. Grill the corn using the same steps as the classic method, but skip the butter.
2. While the corn is still hot, spread a thin layer of mayonnaise over each ear.
3. Sprinkle Cotija cheese and chili powder evenly.
4. Squeeze lime juice over the top and garnish with cilantro if desired. The heat of the corn melts the cheese just enough for a creamy, tangy bite.

Easy Grilled Corn Recipe #3: Herb‑Butter Garlic Corn

This version adds fresh herbs for a garden‑fresh finish. You’ll need:

  • 4 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Pinch of black pepper

1. Mix butter, garlic, parsley, thyme, and pepper in a small bowl.
2. Grill the corn as described in the classic recipe, turning with the Girla Holders.
3. During the last 2 minutes of cooking, brush the herb‑butter onto the corn, allowing it to melt and soak into the kernels.
4. Serve hot, with any remaining butter on the side for extra richness.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Corn Every Time

Even beginners can elevate their corn with a few simple tricks:

  1. Start with fresh corn. Look for ears with bright green husks and plump kernels; they’ll be sweeter and more tender.
  2. Don’t over‑brush. A thin coat of butter or oil prevents soggy kernels and lets the char develop.
  3. Use the Girla Interlocking Corn Holders. They keep your hands safe and make turning the corn effortless, especially when you’re juggling multiple items on the grill.
  4. Let the corn rest. After removing from the grill, give it a minute or two. The residual heat finishes cooking the kernels and lets flavors settle.

FAQ

Can I use the Girla Corn Holders with a charcoal grill?

Absolutely. The silicone handles stay cool whether you’re grilling over gas or charcoal, and the interlocking design works the same way on any grill surface.

Do I need to oil the grill before cooking corn?

No. The corn’s natural moisture and any butter or oil you apply directly to the cob prevent sticking. If you’re using a grill with especially wide grates, a quick spray of non‑stick cooking spray can add extra peace of mind.

How long can I store grilled corn before it goes bad?

Grilled corn is best eaten within 2 hours for optimal texture. If you need to keep it longer, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator and reheat gently on the grill or in a skillet.
