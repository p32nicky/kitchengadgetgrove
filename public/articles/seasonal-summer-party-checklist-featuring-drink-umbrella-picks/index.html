Last-Minute Hosting Tips for a Smooth Flow

The hour before guests arrive is usually the most frantic. To avoid the panic, prep your "garnishing station" early. Slice all your fruit and set out your cocktail umbrellas in a small bowl so you can quickly slide them into drinks as you make them.

Remember to clear a designated spot for trash and recycling right near the drink station. When people finish their drinks, they'll naturally want to discard the umbrella picks and napkins. Having a visible bin prevents your patio furniture from becoming a graveyard of paper parasols.

FAQ

Can I use drink umbrellas in non-alcoholic beverages?

Absolutely! They look fantastic in sparkling cider, virgin mojitos, or even just a fancy glass of lemonade. It makes the non-drinkers feel included in the festive aesthetic.

How do I keep the paper umbrellas from getting soggy?

The best method is to add the umbrella last, just before serving. Avoid stirring the drink after the umbrella is inserted, and place the pick toward the edge of the glass rather than dead center.

Are these picks suitable for heavy appetizers?

They are perfect for light appetizers like fruit, cheese, and shrimp. For very heavy items like large meatballs or thick chunks of steak, I recommend a heavier-duty bamboo skewer, but for 90% of party snacks, these picks work great.
