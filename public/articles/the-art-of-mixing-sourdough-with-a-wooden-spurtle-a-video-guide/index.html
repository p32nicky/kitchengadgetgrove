How to Use a Wooden Spurtle: A Sourdough Mixing Video Guide Step-by-Step

Follow these steps while using the Wooden Spurtle & Sourdough Starter Stirrer Set to master your starter:

  1. Scrape the jar: Use the broad end of the spurtle to reach the jar’s bottom and sides, collecting any stuck starter.
  2. Fold, don’t stir: Insert the spurtle vertically, then gently rock it side-to-side to incorporate ingredients without creating bubbles.
  3. Aerate with the dough stick: Flip the set to the skinny Danish stick, and whisk the starter lightly to introduce oxygen for strong fermentation.

Keeping Your Wooden Spurtle in Top Shape

Wood needs care to last. After each use, rinse the spurtle and stick with warm water only (no soap), then dry immediately with a towel. To prevent cracking, store them in a dry place and oil them monthly with food-safe mineral oil. Avoid soaking or exposing them to direct heat, and never put them in the dishwasher.

FAQ

Can I use a wooden spurtle for other baking tasks?

Absolutely! These tools handle dough folding, mixing dry ingredients, and even stirring yogurt or oatmeal. Their gentle scraping action works for any jar-based task.

How does a wooden spurtle compare to plastic or metal tools?

Wood won’t scratch jars or retain odors like plastic might. It’s also heavier than metal, providing a solid feel for precise movements during folds.

Is the spurtle set suitable for small starter jars?

Yes—the narrow spurtle fits jars of all sizes, from 16 oz feeding containers to 1L storage vessels. The Danish stick’s length gives leverage for deeper jars.
