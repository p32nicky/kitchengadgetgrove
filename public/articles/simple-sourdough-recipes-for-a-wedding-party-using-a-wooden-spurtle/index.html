When baking for a crowd, stick to recipes that can be prepped in advance. The goal is to have the baking done by the morning of the party, leaving you free to decorate and socialize. Here are three crowd-pleasers that work for any wedding event.

1. Honey-Rosemary Sourdough Loaves: A classic boule with a twist. The floral notes of rosemary and the sweetness of honey pair perfectly with a variety of cheeses. Use your Danish dough whisk to incorporate the fresh herbs evenly without overworking the gluten.

2. Sourdough Focaccia Squares: This is the ultimate party food. It's easier to serve than a loaf—just slice it into squares. To get that signature dimpled top, press your fingers deep into the dough after drizzling with olive oil and flaky sea salt.

I recommend starting a "build" two days before the event, feeding your starter twice a day to increase the volume.

Efficient Mixing Techniques for Large Quantities

When you're making four or five loaves at once, manual mixing can feel like a workout. Instead of struggling with a standard spatula, use a dedicated dough blender. The Danish-style dough stick in the spurtle set is designed to cut through thick dough, breaking up lumps and incorporating water far faster than a traditional spoon.

Follow these steps for a streamlined mixing process:

  • Autolyse: Mix your flour and water first and let it rest for 30 minutes. This makes the dough much easier to handle.
  • The Incorporation: Add your starter and salt. Use the Danish whisk to stir in a circular motion, lifting the dough as you go.
  • Stretch and Folds: Instead of kneading on a counter, use a series of stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes. This builds structure while you're busy with other party prep.
  • Cold Proof: Let your shaped loaves sit in the fridge overnight. This develops a deeper flavor and means you only have to bake them off the day of the party.

Serving Sourdough at Your Wedding Event

For the focaccia, serve it on a large wooden board topped with fresh arugula and cherry tomatoes. The rustic look of hand-crafted sourdough adds a warm, homemade touch to the celebration that store-bought rolls simply can't match.

FAQ

Can I make the sourdough dough a day early?

Yes! In fact, it's recommended. Shaping your loaves and letting them proof in the refrigerator overnight (cold retardation) improves the flavor and makes the dough easier to score before baking.

Why use a spurtle instead of a regular spoon for starter?

Standard spoons are often too wide for starter jars, leading to unmixed flour on the sides. A skinny wooden spurtle is designed to fit the geometry of the jar, ensuring a consistent mix and a cleaner jar.

What is the best way to keep sourdough fresh for a party?

Store your baked loaves in a paper bag or a linen bread bag at room temperature. Avoid plastic bags, as they soften the crust. If you're baking a few days early, you can freeze the loaves and refresh them in a 350°F oven for 10 minutes before serving.
