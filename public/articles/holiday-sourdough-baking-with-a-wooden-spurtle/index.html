Preparing Your Starter for Holiday Baking

Before you even think about shaping a loaf, you need a healthy starter. Here’s a quick routine using the wooden spurtle:

  1. Feed the starter. Combine equal parts flour and water (by weight) with the existing starter in a clean jar.
  2. Stir with the skinny spurtle. Insert the short wooden stick, press it against the side, and rotate clockwise, ensuring all flour is hydrated.
  3. Scrape the lid. Use the same stick to clean the jar’s rim so no dry starter clings to the lid.
  4. Cover and let rise. A warm spot (70‑75°F) will coax the starter to double in size within 4‑6 hours.

When the starter is bubbly and has a pleasant tang, it’s ready for the holiday dough.

Mixing the Holiday Dough with the Danish‑Style Dough Stick

The longer wooden spurtle in the set works like a traditional Danish dough whisk. Its slender profile lets you reach the bottom of a mixing bowl without splashing, and the smooth wood surface gently folds the dough, preserving the open crumb you want in a holiday loaf.

Steps for a basic sourdough loaf:

  1. Combine ingredients. In a large container, mix flour, water, salt, and the fed starter.
  2. Autolyse. Let the mixture rest 30 minutes; the flour fully hydrates.
  3. Initial stir. Use the Danish‑style spurtle to blend until no dry streaks remain. The stick’s length gives leverage for a thick batter.
  4. Fold and develop. During bulk fermentation, perform a series of stretch‑and‑folds using the same spurtle. This reduces the need for heavy kneading and keeps the dough airy.

The wooden spurtle’s tapered tip makes it easy to lift the sticky dough from the bowl’s corners, essential when working with high‑hydration holiday recipes that can reach 80% hydration.

Cleaning and Caring for Your Wooden Spurtle

One of the biggest concerns with wooden tools is maintenance. The Wooden Spurtle & Sourdough Starter Stirrer Set is designed for simple, low‑maintenance care:

  • Rinse immediately after use with warm water—no soap needed.
  • Dry on a towel or hang it to air‑dry; the wood will not warp if kept moisture‑free.
  • Periodically rub a thin coat of food‑grade mineral oil into the wood to keep it supple and prevent cracking.

Because the spurtle is non‑scratch, you can even use it on your non‑stick bakeware without fear of damaging the surface.

Tips for a Show‑Stopping Holiday Sourdough

Now that you have the right tool, here are a few holiday‑specific tricks to elevate your loaf:

  • Infuse seasonal flavors. Add a handful of dried cranberries, orange zest, or toasted walnuts during the last stretch‑and‑fold.
  • Score creatively. Use a sharp razor blade to cut a festive pattern—think snowflakes or holly leaves—just before the final proof.
  • Steam for shine. Place a tray of hot water at the bottom of the oven or spray the loaf with water using a spray bottle. The steam gives a glossy crust that looks as good as it tastes.

With a sturdy wooden spurtle, you’ll handle these additions without tearing the dough, ensuring a uniform crumb and a beautiful crust.
