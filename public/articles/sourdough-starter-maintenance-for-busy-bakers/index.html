Keeping a sourdough starter alive and lively doesn’t have to eat up hours of your time. With sourdough starter maintenance tips tailored for busy schedules, you can keep your leaven healthy while juggling life’s chaos. The key? Consistency, smart tools, and a few time-saving tricks.

Daily Feeding: Fast and Foolproof

Your starter needs regular feedings to stay vigorous. For daily use, measure 50g of starter, discard half (or save it for recipes), then add 50g flour and 50g water. Stir thoroughly with a wooden tool to avoid scratches in your jar. Weekly Maintenance: Deep Care Without Overkill

Once a week, give your starter a deeper refresh. Empty the jar, rinse it, and start with a fresh 1:1:1 ratio (starter:flour:water). This prevents acid buildup and keeps the culture balanced. Use the skinny dough stick from your stirrer set to mix vigorously, ensuring no dry pockets remain. Store the jar at room temperature or chill it if you’re not baking weekly.

Essential Tools for Sourdough Starter Maintenance

Quick Fixes for Sourdough Starter Maintenance Hiccups

If your starter acts sluggish, try these fixes:

  1. Double feedings: Feed it twice in 12 hours to boost activity.
  2. Warmer location: Place the jar on a sunny windowsill or near a warm oven.
  3. Check the ratio: Too wet or dry? Adjust to 1:1:1 for balance.

FAQ

How often should I feed my sourdough starter?

Feed it daily if kept at room temperature, or once a week when refrigerated. Adjust based on activity—bubbles and a airy texture mean it’s ready.

Can I use the Wooden Spurtle set for other baking tasks?

Absolutely. The spurtle scrapes bowls and jars, while the dough stick works great for mixing batters or folding in ingredients.

My starter smells bad. Is it ruined?

A slight vinegar scent is normal, but a rotten odor means it’s time to toss it and start fresh. Overfeeding or contamination can cause bad smells.
