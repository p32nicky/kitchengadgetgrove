Honey-Lime Glazed Chicken with Street Corn

This is my go-to recipe when I want a mix of sweet, salty, and tangy. For the chicken, use boneless, skinless thighs. Marinate them in honey, lime juice, minced garlic, and a pinch of cumin for at least two hours. The honey caramelizes beautifully over the open flame, creating a sticky, charred crust.

For the corn, go the "Elote" route. Grill the corn until charred, then slather it in a mixture of mayonnaise, sour cream, and lime juice. Sprinkle with cotija cheese and chili powder. BBQ Rubbed Chicken and Garlic-Butter Corn

If you prefer a classic Americana vibe, stick to a dry rub for the chicken. A blend of smoked paprika, brown sugar, onion powder, and black pepper gives the chicken a deep, savory flavor without the mess of a heavy sauce during the initial sear. Finish with a brush of your favorite BBQ sauce in the last five minutes of grilling.

Pair this with garlic-butter corn. While the corn is on the grill, melt butter with minced garlic and fresh parsley. Brush this mixture onto the cobs every few minutes. The butter soaks into the kernels, and the garlic toasts against the heat. Tips for Perfecting Your Grilled Corn and Chicken Recipes

To ensure your dinner is a success, keep these practical grilling tips in mind:

  • Zone Grilling: Create a two-zone fire. Keep one side of the grill hot for searing and the other side lower for indirect cooking. This prevents the chicken from burning before the inside is done.
  • Corn Prep: Peel back the husks but leave them attached at the base. This creates a natural "handle" during grilling, though you'll still want the Girla holders for the actual eating part.
  • Rest the Meat: Always let your chicken rest for 5-10 minutes after taking it off the grill. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring every bite is moist.
  • The Char Factor: Don't be afraid of a few black charred kernels on the corn. That carbonization is where the deep, smoky flavor lives.

Managing Your Grill Space and Tools

One of the biggest challenges with grilled corn and chicken recipes is the crowded grill. Chicken thighs take longer than corn, so start your meat first. Once the chicken is moved to the cooler side of the grill to finish cooking, slide your corn onto the direct heat.

Healthy Variations for Summer Dinners

If you're looking to lighten things up, swap the honey for a splash of maple syrup or use a Greek-inspired marinade with lemon, oregano, and olive oil for the chicken. Instead of butter for the corn, try a brush of avocado oil and a sprinkle of sea salt and cracked black pepper.

For a complete meal, serve your grilled corn and chicken over a bed of fresh arugula or with a side of grilled zucchini. The charred flavors of the corn and meat pair perfectly with the crispness of raw greens, creating a balanced plate that feels like a feast without feeling too heavy.

FAQ

How do I prevent corn from burning on the grill?

The best way to prevent burning is to rotate the corn every 2-3 minutes. If you are using a butter-based glaze, apply it in stages rather than all at once, as the fats and sugars in butter can burn quickly over high heat.

Can I grill frozen corn for these recipes?

Yes, but it's not ideal. For the best texture and flavor, use fresh corn. If you must use frozen, thaw it completely and pat it dry before placing it on the grill to ensure you get a good sear rather than steaming the corn.

What is the best temperature for grilling chicken thighs?

For boneless thighs, aim for an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). However, many grill masters prefer cooking thighs to 175°F, as the dark meat becomes more tender and the connective tissue breaks down further at a slightly higher temperature.
