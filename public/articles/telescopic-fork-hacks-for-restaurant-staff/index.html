Maintenance and Hygiene for Telescopic Tools

When applying restaurant staff hacks with extendable fork tools, hygiene must come first. Because these forks have sliding segments, they can trap food particles in the joints if not cleaned correctly. To keep them food-safe, avoid just rinsing them under a tap.

The best method is to fully extend the fork and scrub the joints with a nylon brush and soapy water. Since these are stainless steel, they can handle a thorough scrubbing. We recommend keeping them in a designated "utility" area rather than mixing them with the primary silverware to avoid any confusion during the dishwashing process.

FAQ

Is a telescopic fork food-safe for professional use?

As long as the fork is made of stainless steel and is cleaned thoroughly between uses, it is safe for occasional food contact. However, it should be treated as a utility tool rather than primary cutlery.

How do I stop the extendable fork from collapsing?

Most telescoping forks rely on friction. To keep it extended, ensure the segments are pushed firmly into place. If it becomes loose over time, a quick clean of the joints to remove grease can often restore the grip.

Can these be put in a commercial dishwasher?

While stainless steel is dishwasher safe, the high-pressure jets of a commercial machine can sometimes force the fork to collapse or jam. Hand-washing is recommended to preserve the sliding mechanism.
