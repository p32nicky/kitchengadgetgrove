Step‑by‑Step: Classic Grilled Corn for Kids

1. Prep the corn. Peel back the husks but leave the outer layer intact. Remove the silk, then rinse the corn. Brush each ear lightly with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.

2. Insert the holders. Place each corn ear into a Girla holder. The silicone handle keeps your hand safe and the interlocking design keeps the set organized on the grill.

3. Grill. Cook over medium heat, turning every 4–5 minutes, until the corn is tender and lightly charred—about 15–20 minutes total.

4. Serve. Let the kids add a sprinkle of butter, a dash of paprika, or a squeeze of lime. The bright, simple flavors make it a hit with picky eaters.

Creative Veggie Combos: Colorful Skewers That Kids Will Love

Turn veggies into bite‑size, fun skewers. The Girla holders double as a handy way to keep the grill tidy while you cook.

  1. Choose a theme. Pick a color scheme: “rainbow” (red, orange, yellow, green, purple) or “tropical” (pineapple, mango, bell pepper).
  2. Cube the veggies. Cut zucchini, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, and carrots into uniform 1‑inch pieces. This ensures even cooking.
  3. Marinate. Toss the cubes in olive oil, salt, pepper, and a pinch of garlic powder. Let sit for 10 minutes.
  4. Skewer. Alternate veggie colors on long wooden or metal skewers. If using the Girla holders, place the entire skewer inside the holder to protect the grill and keep the skewers from sliding.
  5. Grill. Cook over medium heat, turning every 3–4 minutes, until veggies are tender and slightly charred—about 12 minutes.
  6. Serve. Pair with a yogurt‑based dip or a simple ranch dressing. Kids can help dip and enjoy.

Fun Variations: Sweet, Savory, and International Flavors

Give the classic grilled corn a twist and keep the menu exciting:

  • Honey‑Mustard Corn. Brush corn with a mix of honey, Dijon mustard, and a splash of lime before grilling. The sweetness pairs perfectly with the smoky char.
  • Mexican‑Style Corn. After grilling, slather corn with a mixture of mayo, lime juice, and chili powder. Top with crumbled cotija cheese and chopped cilantro.
  • Italian Veggie Skewers. Add a drizzle of pesto to the veggies before grilling, then sprinkle with grated Parmesan after cooking.

  • Place the corn in the holders.
  • Turn the corn and veggies with a child‑safe tongs.

Healthy Snack Option for Busy Days

When you’re short on time, grill a batch of corn and veggies ahead of the week. Store them in the fridge and reheat in a microwave or on a skillet. Pair with a homemade hummus dip for a quick, nutritious snack that kids will reach for.
