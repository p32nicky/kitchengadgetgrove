  • 2 apples, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 4 cups vegetable broth

2. Quick Beef & Onion Stir‑Fry

This recipe proves that sliced onions can be the star of a fast, protein‑packed stir‑fry. The stainless‑steel prongs of the Onion Holder keep the onion rings steady while you slice, so every bite cooks evenly.

  1. Slice 1 large onion and 1 red bell pepper using the slicer.
  2. Thinly slice ½ lb flank steak against the grain.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok over high heat; add steak, stir for 2 minutes, then set aside.
  4. Add the onions (and pepper) to the wok; stir‑fry 3‑4 minutes until just softened.
  5. Return steak to the pan, splash 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, and a pinch of sugar; toss 1 minute.
  6. Serve over steamed rice, garnished with sliced green onions.

The result is a savory, slightly sweet dish that comes together in under 15 minutes—perfect for busy weekdays.

3. Onion‑Tomato Bruschetta

When you need a simple appetizer that still feels special, sliced onions add a mild bite that complements juicy tomatoes and fresh basil.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced ½‑inch thick
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • ½ onion, sliced thin (the slicer makes the slices paper‑thin)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for brushing bread
  • Fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt, pepper, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze

Brush the baguette slices with olive oil and toast until golden. In a bowl, combine tomatoes, sliced onions, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spoon the mixture onto the toasted bread, finish with a balsamic drizzle, and serve immediately.

4. Roasted Onion & Potato Medley

Roasting sliced onions with potatoes creates a caramelized side that pairs beautifully with roasted chicken or grilled fish. The Onion Holder’s stainless steel prongs also double as a meat tenderizer, so you can prep a chicken breast while the veg roasts.

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Slice 2 medium onions and 3 potatoes into ¼‑inch rounds using the slicer.
  3. Toss onions and potatoes with 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread on a baking sheet in a single layer; roast 30‑35 minutes, turning halfway, until edges are crisp.

The caramelized edges of the onions bring a sweet‑savory depth that elevates any main course.
