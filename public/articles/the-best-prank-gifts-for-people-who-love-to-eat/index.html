FAQ

Are prank forks safe to use with real food?

Telescoping forks are intended for gags and should be used for a few laughs. While they are made of stainless steel, they aren't designed for heavy-duty eating, so use them for light snacks and quick laughs rather than a full steak dinner.

What is a spurtle, and is it actually useful?

Yes! A spurtle is a traditional Scottish tool used for stirring porridge and soups to prevent lumps. In the modern kitchen, it's excellent for stirring sourdough starters and scraping the sides of narrow jars.

How do I store interlocking corn holders?

The interlocking design allows them to stack neatly together, making them easy to store in a silverware drawer or a utility bin without taking up excessive space.
