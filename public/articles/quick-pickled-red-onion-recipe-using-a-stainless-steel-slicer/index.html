Quick Pickled Red Onion Recipe Using a Stainless Steel Slicer

This recipe takes under 15 minutes and requires no heat—all you need is the slicing tool, a jar, and pantry staples. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Prepare the onion: Use the Onion Holder to slice the red onion into thin, even rounds. The anti-cut design keeps your hand steady.
  2. Make the brine: In a jar, combine 2 cups water, 1 tablespoon white vinegar, 1 tablespoon salt, and 1 teaspoon sugar. Bring to a boil.
  3. Combine and chill: Pour the hot brine over the sliced onions, seal the jar, and refrigerate for 1 hour before serving. Store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

Storage and Serving Tips

Quick pickled red onions are best stored in an airtight container in the fridge. They mellow over time, so use them within two weeks. Add them to:

  • Grilled cheese sandwiches for a zesty kick
  • Guacamole or salsa to balance richness
  • Roasted vegetable bowls for texture
