3. Prepare Dessert Bases That Hold Umbrellas

Not every dessert will need a pick, but a few sturdy options work best:

  1. Mini cupcakes or cake pops – Use a thick frosting layer to hold the pick upright.
  2. Chocolate truffles – Press the pick into the center while the truffle is still warm.
  3. Fruit skewers – Thread a small piece of pineapple or mango before the pick, then finish with the umbrella.
  4. Individual parfait glasses – Place the pick on the rim of the glass; the umbrella will hang over the top.

Make sure each base is dry enough to hold the pick securely, but not so dry that the umbrella slides off.

4. Assemble the Mini Umbrella Garnishes

With the desserts ready, it’s time to add the finishing touch:

  • Insert the pick into the center of the cupcake or the side of the truffle.
  • Gently open the paper parasol and let it sit upright; the paper will naturally curve back into shape.
  • For fruit skewers, push the pick through the fruit piece so the umbrella rests on top.
  • Arrange the finished pieces on the dessert bar, alternating colors for visual rhythm.

Because the 150‑pack provides plenty of extra umbrellas, you can replace any that get damaged during setup without scrambling for a backup.

5. Add Complementary Accents and Lighting

Mini umbrellas look best when they’re highlighted by soft lighting. Consider these simple ideas:

  • String lights draped across the back of the bar add a warm glow that catches the paper’s bright hues.
  • Mirrored trays reflect the umbrellas and make the colors pop.
  • Fresh greenery such as eucalyptus or succulents creates a natural backdrop that balances the playful vibe.

Finish with a few decorative elements—like small seashells for a beach wedding or crystal beads for a more formal affair—to tie the whole look together.

6. Serve, Snap, and Enjoy

Once the bar is set, encourage guests to take a photo before they indulge. The mini umbrellas make for eye‑catching Instagram moments, especially when paired with a signature cocktail or mocktail that uses the same picks. After the photos, let guests help themselves to the desserts; the picks double as handy utensils for picking up bite‑size treats.

FAQ

Can I use the 150‑piece drink umbrellas for non‑drink decorations?

Absolutely. The picks work well as garnish sticks for fruit, chocolate, and even small savory appetizers like cheese cubes.

How far in advance should I soak the umbrella picks?

Soaking for 30 seconds right before assembly is ideal. If you soak them too early, the paper can become overly soft and lose shape.

Are the paper umbrellas safe for food contact?

Yes. They are made from food‑grade paper and are intended for garnish use on desserts and drinks.
