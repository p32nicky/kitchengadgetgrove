Step‑by‑Step: Preparing the Core Taco Fillings

Start with the three staples that make a vegan taco shine: onions, tomatoes, and a starch such as sweet potato or cauliflower. Here’s how to use the onion holder to speed the process:

  1. Set up the holder. Open the stainless‑steel prongs and place the vegetable on the flat base. The prongs will clamp down, keeping the item steady.
  2. Trim and peel. For onions, cut off the root and tip, then peel the outer skin. The holder keeps the onion upright, so you can make a clean cut without the knife sliding.
  3. Slice uniformly. Using a chef’s knife, slice the onion or tomato into thin rounds (about 1/8‑inch). The holder’s grip ensures each slice is the same thickness, which translates to even cooking.
  4. Dice if needed. Stack a few slices, then cut them into strips and finally dice. Because the pieces are already uniform, the dice comes out consistent without extra effort.
  5. Repeat for other veggies. Swap the onion for a tomato or a small sweet potato. The same holder works for each, saving space and time.

When you’re done, you’ll have a bowl of evenly sliced onions, tomatoes, and a nicely diced starch ready for the skillet.

Cooking the Fillings Quickly and Safely

With the veggies pre‑sliced, the actual cooking takes only a few minutes. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium‑high heat. Add the diced sweet potato first—its denser texture needs a minute longer. Stir for 2‑3 minutes, then toss in the onion and tomato. Season with cumin, smoked paprika, a pinch of salt, and a splash of lime juice. The uniform pieces ensure every bite gets the same burst of flavor, and the risk of burning is low because the pieces cook at the same rate.

Assembly Tips for the Perfect Vegan Taco

Now that your filling is ready, focus on the taco shell. Corn tortillas work best for a gluten‑free option; warm them on a dry skillet for 20 seconds per side. Layer the filling, then add optional toppings: sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, and a drizzle of vegan chipotle mayo. Because the onion holder gave you evenly cut pieces, the toppings sit evenly, making each taco look as good as it tastes.
