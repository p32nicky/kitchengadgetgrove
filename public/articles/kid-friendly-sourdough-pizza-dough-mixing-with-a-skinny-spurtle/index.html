Ingredients and Equipment Checklist

  • 250 g active sourdough starter (100 % hydration)
  • 300 g bread flour (or a mix of 70 % bread + 30 % whole‑wheat for extra nutrition)
  • 180 ml water, lukewarm
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional for a softer crust)
  • 2 pcs Wooden Spurtle Spatula for Sourdough Starter (the skinny dough stirring stick)
  • Large mixing bowl, kitchen scale, damp cloth
  • Pizza stone or baking sheet

Step‑by‑Step Mixing with the Skinny Spurtle

1. Combine starter and water. Pour the active starter into your bowl, then add the lukewarm water. Use the wooden spurtle to stir in a circular motion, scraping the sides and bottom of the bowl. The tapered tip reaches into the corners where a spoon often leaves residue.

2. Add flour. Sprinkle the flour over the liquid mixture. With the spurtle’s flat side, fold the flour into the wet ingredients, turning the bowl as you go. This method encourages a gentle autolyse, letting the flour hydrate without aggressive kneading.

3. Incorporate salt and oil. Once the dough looks cohesive, sprinkle the salt (and oil, if using) over the surface. Continue to stir with the spurtle until the salt is fully dissolved and the dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl.

4. Rest and fold. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let the dough rest for 30 minutes. After the rest, perform a series of stretch‑and‑folds using the spurtle to lift and turn the dough. This gives the gluten a chance to develop without the need for heavy kneading—a technique that keeps the dough soft enough for kids to shape.

Kid‑Safe Fermentation and Shaping Tips

Fermentation can feel mysterious to children, so turn it into a visual experiment. Place a clear plastic container over the bowl and watch bubbles form. Explain that the bubbles are “tiny pizza‑flavor fireworks.” Let the dough rise at room temperature for 4–6 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for a slower, more flavorful rise.

When it’s time to shape the pizza, turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Show kids how to press the dough with their fingertips, then use the spurtle’s narrow tip to help lift and stretch the edges. The wooden spurtle is smooth enough not to tear the dough, yet sturdy enough to help guide the shape.

Baking the Pizza

Preheat your oven with a pizza stone inside to 500°F (260°C) for at least 30 minutes. Transfer the shaped dough onto a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pizza peel, add sauce, cheese, and toppings of choice, then slide it onto the hot stone. Bake for 8–10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

Let the pizza cool for a couple of minutes before slicing—kids love watching the cheese stretch. Serve with a side of fresh herbs or a drizzle of olive oil for extra flavor.

FAQ

Can I use a metal spoon instead of the wooden spurtle?

You can, but a metal spoon can scratch glass containers and may over‑mix the dough, leading to a tougher crust. The wooden spurtle’s gentle action preserves the starter’s health and keeps the dough tender.

Is the wooden spurtle dishwasher safe?

We recommend hand‑washing with mild soap and letting it air dry. High heat and harsh detergents can warp the wood over time.

How long can I keep the starter active using the spurtle?

As long as you feed it regularly (every 12‑24 hours at room temperature or weekly in the fridge). The spurtle helps you mix without introducing metal ions that could affect the starter’s balance.
