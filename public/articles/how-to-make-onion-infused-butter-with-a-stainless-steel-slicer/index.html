Why Uniform Slices Matter for Butter Infusion

When you melt butter with minced or thinly sliced onions, the surface area of the onion determines how quickly flavor releases. Large, uneven pieces create pockets of raw onion taste, while tiny, uniform slices dissolve evenly, giving a consistent, buttery aroma. Gathering Your Tools and Ingredients

Before you start, assemble the following items:

  • 2 PCS Onion Holder for Slicing (the stainless steel kitchen slicer tool)
  • Sharp chef’s knife or the included guide blade
  • Fresh yellow onions (medium‑size work best)
  • Unsalted butter – 1 cup (2 sticks)
  • Small saucepan with a lid
  • Optional: a pinch of salt, fresh herbs, or a splash of white wine

Having everything at hand prevents the temptation to leave the stove unattended, which can cause butter to scorch.

Step‑by‑Step: Slicing the Onion with the Stainless Steel Slicer

1. Trim and Peel: Cut off the root end and the top, then peel away the papery skin. Leaving a small portion of the root intact helps keep the layers together while you slice.

2. Position the Onion Holder: Open the stainless steel prongs, place the onion inside, and close the holder so the onion sits securely. The holder’s design keeps the onion from rolling, which is especially useful for slippery onions.

3. Set the Desired Thickness: If the slicer guide offers adjustable thickness, set it to a thin setting (about 1‑2 mm). For a finer, almost‑julienne texture, aim for the thinnest possible slice.

4. Slice: Using a smooth, even motion, glide the blade over the onion while the holder stabilizes it. You’ll get a stack of uniform rings in seconds, and because the prongs keep the onion steady, there’s no need to hold it with your fingers—reducing the risk of accidental cuts.

5. Collect the Rings: Gently tap the slicer to release the onion rings onto a cutting board. If you need smaller pieces, run the rings through a quick chop with your knife.

Infusing the Butter

With your perfectly sliced onions ready, melt the butter over low heat. Add the onion rings and stir gently, allowing them to soften without browning—about 3‑4 minutes. For a deeper flavor, you can let the onions sweat a minute longer, but watch closely to avoid caramelization, which changes the profile from sweet to bitter.

Once the onions are translucent and the butter has taken on a faint golden hue, remove the pan from the heat. If desired, stir in a pinch of salt, fresh chopped herbs (like thyme or parsley), or a splash of white wine for acidity. Let the mixture cool for a few minutes, then transfer it to a small ramekin or butter dish. It will solidify into a fragrant spread ready for use.

Cleaning and Maintaining Your Stainless Steel Slicer

After cooking, rinse the Onion Holder & Slicing Guide under warm water. The stainless steel prongs are dishwasher‑safe, but hand‑washing with a mild detergent helps preserve the finish. Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots. If any onion residue sticks to the blade, a soft brush or a non‑abrasive sponge will do the trick without dulling the edge.

Creative Uses for Onion‑Infused Butter

Now that you have a jar of buttery, aromatic onion goodness, here are a few ways to make the most of it:

  • Steak topping: Melt a pat of the butter over a grilled ribeye for an instant flavor boost.
  • Vegetable sauté: Use it as the base for cooking green beans or asparagus, letting the onion essence coat every bite.
  • Garlic‑bread upgrade: Spread the butter on a baguette, toast, and finish with a sprinkle of Parmesan.
  • Soup finish: Swirl a spoonful into creamy soups right before serving for a velvety finish.

Because the butter was infused with uniformly sliced onions, the flavor is consistent throughout, and you won’t encounter any raw‑onion bites.
