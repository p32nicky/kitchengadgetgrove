4 Creative Ways to Use Extendable Forks at Your Reception

  1. Daring Food Swap Game: Challenge guests to trade bites using the fork’s extended length. Serve dishes with messy components (like mac and cheese or lemon squares) to up the difficulty and the laughs.
  2. Wedding Toast Shenanigans: Position guests across the table and have them clink glasses using the fork’s tines. It’s harder than it looks—and the failed attempts are comedy gold.
  3. Photo Booth Prop: Tuck a few extendable forks into the photo booth for guests to pose with in absurd angles. The contrast between serious wedding photos and the goofy fork antics is unforgettable.
  4. Table Decor Prank: Place one fork subtly among regular cutlery. Watch as guests unknowingly pick it up, gasping when it suddenly stretches. Pair with a note that reads, "Your appetizer: stolen."

How to Introduce the Prank Without Ruining the Vibe

Key to success: framing the prank as a fun game. Start with a lighthearted toast: "If you can outwit the fork, you get dessert." Make it clear it’s a joke, not a competition. Avoid targeting introverted guests—keep the energy inclusive. If anyone seems uncomfortable, politely steer them away from the prank.
