Stainless Steel vs. Other Materials

Most extendable forks use either stainless steel or aluminum. Stainless steel wins in durability due to:

  • Corrosion resistance: Ideal for kitchens with steam or humidity
  • Weight: Adds heft for stability during food theft attempts
  • Long-term flexibility: Maintains shape after thousands of extensions

Aluminum is lighter but prone to bending under pressure—a dealbreaker for serious pranksters.

How to Extend Your Fork’s Lifespan

Even durable forks need care to perform. Follow these tips:

  1. Wipe down after use to prevent food residue buildup
  2. Avoid overloading the tines; these aren’t replacement for tongs
  3. Store in a dry place to prevent rust (silica packets help in humid areas)

Regularly check the locking mechanism to ensure it doesn’t slip unexpectedly.
