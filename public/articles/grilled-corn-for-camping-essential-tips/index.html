Choosing the Right Corn

Fresh, sweet corn makes the biggest difference. Look for ears with bright green husks that are still tightly wrapped around the kernels. If you’re buying at a grocery store before you head out, choose corn that is still in its husk – the husk protects the kernels from drying out while you travel.

Prep Before You Pack

Doing most of the prep at home saves time at the campsite and reduces waste. Here’s what I do:

  1. Peel back the husk just enough to expose the kernels, then pull the husk away completely.
  2. Remove the silky threads with a soft brush or your hands.
  3. Brush each ear with a thin layer of oil or melted butter. This helps the kernels char nicely and keeps the corn from sticking to the grill.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and any favorite herbs (thyme, smoked paprika, or a pinch of chili flakes work well).
  5. Re‑wrap the husk around the ear, leaving the bottom open for easier turning on the fire.

Once the corn is pre‑seasoned and re‑husked, wrap each ear in a piece of aluminum foil. The foil protects the kernels from direct flame, keeps the butter from dripping, and makes cleanup a breeze.

Grilling Techniques for Campfires

There are three reliable ways to grill corn while camping:

  • Direct over coals: Place the foil‑wrapped ears directly on hot embers. Turn every 5‑7 minutes until the kernels are tender and lightly charred (usually 15‑20 minutes total).
  • On a grill grate: If you have a portable grill, lay the corn on the grate. The Girla holders let you flip the ears without burning your fingers.
  • In a skillet: For a more controlled environment, heat a cast‑iron skillet over the fire, add a splash of butter, and cook the corn on the foil for a few minutes per side. This method yields a buttery finish and a crisp exterior.

Whichever method you choose, keep an eye on the fire. A moderate heat (medium‑hot coals) gives the corn time to steam inside the foil while still developing that smoky flavor on the outside.

Keeping Corn Warm and Serving Tips

Once the corn is done, you’ll want to serve it while it’s still hot. The silicone handles on the Girla holders stay cool, letting you pass hot ears to guests without a burn. If you’re feeding a crowd, stack the finished corn in a foil‑lined basket and cover it with a towel; the residual heat will keep it warm for another 10‑15 minutes.

For extra flavor, drizzle a little more melted butter, sprinkle fresh herbs, or add a squeeze of lime. The husk can be used as a handy “finger guard” – pull the husk back, bite into the kernels, then push the husk forward to protect your fingers as you work your way down the ear.

FAQ

Can I use the Girla corn holders on metal skewers?

Yes. The silicone grips are heat‑resistant up to about 400°F, so they work great on metal skewers or grill grates. Just make sure the holders aren’t directly exposed to open flame for extended periods.

Do I need to soak the corn before grilling?

No soaking is required if you keep the husk on and wrap the ear in foil. The husk and foil trap steam, which keeps the kernels moist while they cook.
