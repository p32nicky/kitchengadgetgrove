1. Classic Butter & Herb Mix

Everything starts with butter. Melt ¼ cup of unsalted butter and stir in a tablespoon each of chopped fresh parsley, chives, and a pinch of sea salt. Brush the mixture over the hot corn while it’s still on the grill; the heat will melt the butter into the kernels, letting the herbs infuse their bright flavors. This simple topping highlights the natural sweetness of corn and works as a base for more adventurous additions.

2. Spicy Lime & Cotija Crumble

For a Mexican‑inspired twist, combine the zest of a lime with a drizzle of hot sauce and a generous sprinkle of crumbled Cotija cheese. The acidity of the lime cuts through the richness, while the cheese adds a salty, crumbly texture. Sprinkle the topping just after removing the corn from the grill so the cheese stays slightly soft but doesn’t melt completely.

3. Sweet Chili & Toasted Sesame

Mix 2 tablespoons of sweet chili sauce with 1 teaspoon of toasted sesame oil. Brush this glaze over the corn, then toss the ears in a shallow dish of toasted sesame seeds. The result is a glossy, slightly sweet coating with a nutty crunch that pairs perfectly with the charred kernels. This topping is a crowd‑pleaser for those who love a hint of Asian flavor.

4. Garlic Parmesan Parmesan

Turn the classic garlic‑butter combo into a cheesy masterpiece. Melt 3 tablespoons of butter with 2 minced garlic cloves, then brush the mixture onto the corn. While the corn is still hot, sprinkle a mixture of grated Parmesan, a dash of smoked paprika, and a little cracked black pepper. The heat releases the cheese’s aroma, creating a savory, umami‑rich coating.

5. Herbed Yogurt Drizzle

For a lighter, tangy option, whisk together ½ cup of Greek yogurt, 1 tablespoon of fresh dill, 1 teaspoon of lemon zest, and a pinch of salt. After the corn has rested for a minute, drizzle the yogurt sauce over the ears. The cool yogurt balances the grill’s heat, and the dill adds a fresh, garden‑like note.
