Why Silicone Handles are a Must

Old-school corn holders were often just plastic or metal. Metal can get too hot if left near the grill, and cheap plastic can crack or stain over time. Silicone is the superior choice for a few practical reasons:

  • Grip: Even with melted butter and salt on your hands, silicone provides a non-slip grip.
  • Heat Resistance: You don't have to worry about the handles melting or becoming dangerously hot.
  • Color Coding: Bright handles make it easy for guests to keep track of which cob is theirs, reducing the risk of double-dipping.

Tips for the Perfect Grilled Corn

Since you've got your gear sorted, let's make sure the corn is worth the effort. For those with limited outdoor space or only a small grill, try the "foil wrap" method. Peel back the husks, rub the corn with butter and seasonings, then pull the husks back up and grill.

Once you remove the corn from the husk, slide in your corn holders immediately. This allows you to move the corn to a serving platter without touching the hot kernels. It keeps your hands clean and prevents those accidental finger burns that happen when you're trying to juggle a plate and a cob in a cramped dining area.

FAQ

Do corn holders work on large ears of corn?

Yes, most standard holders, including the Girla set, are designed to pierce through the core of the cob regardless of the size of the kernels. Just ensure you push them in deep enough to get a secure grip.

Are interlocking holders better than loose ones?

Absolutely, especially for small kitchens. Interlocking designs prevent the holders from becoming "lost" in your drawers and make it much easier to count your pairs before you head outside to the grill.

Can I use these for other vegetables?

While designed for corn, these can work for other thick, grilled vegetables like large pieces of zucchini or hearty mushrooms if you're looking to serve "kabob-style" appetizers.
